# The Nuclear Question for Beginners

The war's main justification is that Iran was about to build a nuclear bomb. Here is why that claim is contested -- and the history they are not telling you.

Last updated: 13 March 2026 (Day 14 of the war)

---

## What the US says

The stated reason for Operation Epic Fury is to "permanently deny Iran nuclear weapons forever." White House press secretary Carolyn Leavitt listed it as one of the four objectives of the war. President Trump has repeated that Iran was "one month from a bomb." Netanyahu has boasted that Israeli strikes killed top Iranian nuclear scientists: "They are no longer here."

The argument is simple: Iran was about to get a nuclear weapon, and the US and Israel attacked to stop it.

## What the evidence actually shows

Three separate bodies of evidence contradict the claim that Iran had a nuclear weapons program.

### 1. The IAEA found no weapons program

Iran is a signatory to the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty (NPT). Under the NPT, countries have the right to develop nuclear energy for peaceful purposes. In exchange, they submit to inspections by the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA).

Iran submitted to those inspections. The IAEA found safeguard breaches -- late declarations, testing they should have disclosed earlier -- but no evidence of a nuclear weapons program. Former IAEA Director General Mohammed El-Baradei repeatedly told the UN Security Council that there was no evidence Iran was building a bomb.

Scott Ritter, a former UN weapons inspector, wrote in 2006: "No evidence of Iranian nuclear weapons program. The IAEA found no evidence of weapons program; HEU/LEU traces were explained by contamination from imported equipment."

### 2. The US intelligence community agreed -- twice

In 2003, the US National Intelligence Estimate (NIE) -- representing the consensus of all 16 US intelligence agencies -- stated with "high confidence" that if Iran ever had a weapons program, it had halted it in the fall of 2003. A follow-up NIE in 2007 reaffirmed this conclusion.

This is not an Iranian claim. This is the conclusion of the CIA, the NSA, the DIA, and every other American intelligence agency. It was classified at the time but has since been made public.

In February 2026, just before the war started, the National Intelligence Council reportedly leaked an assessment that contradicted the administration's claims about Iran's nuclear capability. The war was launched on a fabricated premise.

### 3. Iran's supreme leader banned nuclear weapons on religious grounds

Ayatollah Khamenei issued a fatwa -- a binding religious ruling -- declaring that the production, stockpiling, and use of nuclear weapons is forbidden under Islam. This fatwa has been in effect for years.

You may or may not believe Khamenei was sincere. But the point is that this fact -- along with the IAEA inspections and the NIE conclusions -- has been almost entirely absent from the media coverage of the war. Dan Kovalik wrote in 2018 that war propaganda would require "a total media blackout" of both the NIE and the fatwa. That is exactly what happened.

## The country that actually has nuclear weapons

There is one nuclear-armed state in the Middle East. It is not Iran. It is Israel.

### How many weapons?

The Institute for Science and International Security estimates Israel has approximately 90 nuclear warheads, with enough plutonium to build roughly 200 more. Israel has a full nuclear triad: land-based ballistic missiles, aircraft capable of delivering nuclear weapons, and Dolphin-class submarines armed with nuclear cruise missiles (the submarines were supplied by Germany).

### Why doesn't anyone talk about this?

Because Israel has never signed the NPT. It has never acknowledged possessing nuclear weapons. It has never submitted to international inspections. Its entire arsenal "officially does not exist."

This is a deliberate policy called "nuclear ambiguity" or "deliberate opacity." The purpose is to avoid the sanctions, oversight, and diplomatic pressure that would come with openly declaring a nuclear arsenal.

The double standard is stark:

| | Iran | Israel |
|---|---|---|
| NPT signatory | Yes | No |
| IAEA inspections | Yes (rigorous) | None |
| Nuclear weapons | No (IAEA, NIE confirmed) | ~90 warheads (estimated) |
| Sanctions for nuclear activity | Decades of crippling sanctions | None |
| International pressure | Debated at every summit | Invisible |

### How did Israel get nuclear weapons?

**The 1950s:** Prime Minister David Ben-Gurion began Israel's nuclear program in the mid-1950s, building the Dimona reactor in the Negev desert with covert French assistance. The site was publicly described as a textile factory, an agricultural research station, and a metallurgical research centre.

**The 1960s:** US U-2 spy planes detected the scale of the Dimona complex as early as 1958. When US officials visited in the 1960s, they were deceived by false walls, concealed elevators, and six secret underground floors that hid the weapons production facilities. In 1968, a CIA report confirmed that Israel was producing nuclear weapons.

**1969 -- The Nixon-Meir understanding:** In 1969, President Nixon and Israeli Prime Minister Golda Meir reached a secret agreement: as long as Israel refrained from publicly testing or declaring its nuclear arsenal, the United States would cease inspections and stop asking questions. This was the birth of "don't ask, don't tell" at the level of nuclear superpowers. By the mid-1970s, the State Department was refusing to confirm to Congress that Israel had the bomb, despite intelligence stating that it did. Every American president since Nixon has upheld the silence.

**1979 -- The Vela incident:** On 22 September 1979, a US Vela satellite detected a "double flash" over the South Atlantic -- the signature of an atmospheric nuclear detonation. Evidence pointed to a joint test by Israel and apartheid South Africa. The Carter administration convened the Ruina panel, which concluded it was "probably not" a nuclear detonation -- perhaps a meteorite had struck the satellite. It was a political whitewash. Carter later acknowledged that his scientists did believe Israel had conducted a nuclear test at sea.

### Mordechai Vanunu -- the man who told the truth

In 1986, Mordechai Vanunu, a nuclear technician who had worked at Dimona for nine years, fled to London with 60 secret photographs. He told the Sunday Times that Israel possessed 100-200 nuclear weapons, including hydrogen bomb capability. He described the false walls, concealed elevators, and six secret underground floors that had deceived American inspection teams.

The Mossad lured Vanunu to Rome, where he was drugged and abducted. He was taken to Israel, convicted of treason and espionage, and sentenced to 18 years in prison -- 11.5 of them in solitary confinement in a cell measuring 3 by 2 metres.

At 71, Vanunu remains under severe restrictions. He is banned from leaving Israel and prohibited from speaking to foreign journalists.

The man who revealed Israel's secret nuclear arsenal has spent decades in punishment. The arsenal itself has never been subject to a single inspection.

## How the double standard works

Noam Chomsky described the pattern in 2006-2007: the rules of international law are applied to enemies and ignored when they get in the way of what powerful countries want to do.

- **Iran** signed the NPT, submitted to inspections, was found to have no weapons program, and was economically strangled with sanctions, then bombed.
- **Israel** never signed the NPT, never submitted to inspections, has ~90 warheads, and has never faced sanctions or even public acknowledgment.
- **Iraq** was invaded in 2003 to eliminate weapons of mass destruction that did not exist.
- **North Korea** left the NPT in 2003, tested nuclear weapons, and was branded a "rogue state" -- but not invaded (because it has nuclear weapons, which is the point).
- **India and Pakistan** both have nuclear weapons and never signed the NPT. The US signed a nuclear cooperation deal with India in 2005, effectively rewarding it for developing weapons outside the treaty.

Ritter summarised it: "The Iran crisis is made in Israel." Chomsky called the NPT a system "designed so the right bombs stay in the right hands."

## What the predictors said about the nuclear question

### Chomsky (2006-2007)

"The invasion of Iraq was basically a plea to Iran to develop nuclear weapons." His logic: the US destroyed Iraq (which had no nuclear weapons) and left North Korea alone (which did). The lesson for any country watching was clear -- if you want to avoid being attacked, get nuclear weapons. Iran's decision not to pursue them, in Chomsky's view, made it more vulnerable, not less.

Chomsky also noted that under the Shah, Henry Kissinger supported Iran's nuclear energy program. When the Shah was in power (a US ally), Iran "needed" nuclear energy. After the revolution (when Iran became independent of US control), the same program became "proof" that Iran was building a bomb. The technology did not change. The relationship did.

### Kovalik (2018)

The JCPOA nuclear deal was a "Trojan Horse." The deal stripped Iran of its nuclear deterrent -- the ability to enrich uranium -- in exchange for promises that the US then broke. Trump withdrew from the deal and launched a war against a country that had complied with it. Kovalik's argument: the deal was "always intended" to leave Iran vulnerable. "The United States was motivated to end Iran's nuclear ambitions for the precise purpose of leaving Iran vulnerable to attack."

He cited Martin van Creveld, a prominent Israeli military historian: "Had the Iranians not tried to build nuclear weapons, they would be crazy" -- given that US forces surrounded Iran on all sides and Iraq's example showed what happened to countries without a deterrent.

### Ritter (2006)

As a former weapons inspector, Ritter brought technical credibility. He argued there was no evidence of an Iranian weapons program, that the IAEA's findings were being ignored when they contradicted the war narrative, and that the entire crisis was "made in Israel" -- manufactured to serve Israeli strategic interests using the same playbook as Iraq.

### Jiang (2024-2025)

Jiang pointed out that the US had been saying "Iran is one month from a bomb" for over ten years and it was never true. He predicted the administration would use the nuclear justification anyway because it was the most effective way to sell the war to the American public. He was right.

## Why this matters

If you understand one thing about this war, understand this: the only nuclear-armed state in the Middle East is Israel. Iran does not have nuclear weapons. The IAEA confirmed it. The CIA confirmed it. Sixteen US intelligence agencies confirmed it. The war was launched to "prevent" something that was not happening, while ignoring an arsenal of ~90 warheads that has never been inspected, never been acknowledged, and never been subject to a single sanction.

Chomsky's method is simple: compare what governments say with what they actually do. Applied to the nuclear question, the record is unambiguous.

The NPT is enforced against countries the US wants to attack. It is ignored for countries the US wants to protect. The law is applied to enemies and suspended for allies. Sovereignty, in this system, is a privilege granted by the powerful -- not a right held by all.

Mordechai Vanunu told the truth about Israel's nuclear arsenal and spent 18 years in prison. The people who built the arsenal have never been questioned. The country that signed the treaty and submitted to inspections is being bombed. The country that refused to sign and built weapons in secret is doing the bombing.

That is the nuclear question.
