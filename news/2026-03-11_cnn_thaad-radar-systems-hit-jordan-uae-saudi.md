# THAAD Radar Systems Hit in Jordan, UAE, Saudi Arabia

## Source
- CNN (5 Mar 2026, updated 6 Mar): https://edition.cnn.com/2026/03/05/middleeast/radar-bases-us-missile-defense-iran-war-intl-invs

## Key Finding

Iran is targeting US-made AN/TPY-2 radars — the critical detection element for THAAD missile batteries — in an apparent effort to degrade air defenses across the region.

## Confirmed Strikes

**Jordan — Muwaffaq Salti Air Base:**
- THAAD radar system struck and **apparently destroyed** (March 1-2)
- Satellite image (March 2) shows debris surrounding blackened radar
- Pair of 13-foot craters in sand near radar suggest multiple targeting attempts
- Radar split across five 40-foot trailers — "all appeared to be destroyed or seriously damaged"
- Base had 50+ fighter jets on tarmac before war, plus drones and transport aircraft
- Dozens of aircraft shelters likely contained more planes

**Saudi Arabia — Prince Sultan Air Base:**
- Satellite image (March 1) shows smoke rising from radar site
- Tent used to shelter radar system "badly charred"
- Debris scattered around
- January satellite imagery showed antenna unit positioned inside, pointed northeast toward Iran
- Unclear if radar was present at time of attack
- US defense official declined comment citing operational security

**UAE — Al Ruwais and Al Sader:**
- At least three buildings at Ruwais and four at Sader damaged (Feb 28 - March 1)
- **Pull-through vehicle sheds used to store radar systems** among buildings struck
- Pattern of strikes "nearly identical" between the two sites
- Sites have hosted THAAD batteries since at least 2016-2018
- Unclear if radars were present at time of strikes; unclear if US or UAE equipment

## Cost and Significance

**AN/TPY-2 radar:**
- Manufactured by Raytheon
- Cost: **just shy of half-a-billion dollars** ($500M) per 2025 Missile Defense Agency budget
- "Cannot be easily replaced"
- "Essentially the heart of the THAAD battery"
- Enables launch of interceptor missiles
- Contributes to "networked air defense picture"

**Expert analysis (N.R. Jenzen-Jones, ARES):**
- "Loss of even a single radar of this type would be an operationally significant event"
- Replacement would have to be redeployed from elsewhere — "will take time and effort"
- THAAD has broad engagement zone but needs to be "layered with other ABM and air defenses (such as Patriot)"

## Additional Damage

**Qatar — Umm Dahal:**
- US-made Qatari early-warning radar system damaged
- Analysis by James Martin Center for Nonproliferation Studies

## Iran's Strategy

Satellite images suggest Iran is seeking to degrade air defenses by **specifically targeting detection and communication infrastructure** rather than only offensive assets:
- THAAD radars (Jordan, Saudi, UAE)
- Early warning radars (Qatar)
- Previously reported: communications, radar and intelligence equipment at US bases across Arabian Peninsula

## US Inventory Context

- US operates **8 THAAD batteries** total
- UAE operates 2
- Saudi Arabia operates 1
- Loss of radars directly impacts regional missile defense capability

## Key Quote

**Jenzen-Jones:** "The AN-TPY/2 radar is essentially the heart of the THAAD battery, enabling the launch of interceptor missiles and contributing to a networked air defense picture. It also happens to be an incredibly expensive piece of kit."
