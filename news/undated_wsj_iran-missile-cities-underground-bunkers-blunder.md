# Iran's underground missile bunkers strategy beginning to look like a blunder (WSJ)

**Date:** 2026-03-00  
**Source:** Wall Street Journal  
**URL:** https://on.wsj.com/4lereBg

---

## tl;dr

Iran spent decades building underground bunkers to shield its missile arsenal. Less than a week into the war with the US and Israel, the strategy is beginning to look like a blunder. US and Israeli warplanes and armed drones circle the cavernous bases and strike missile-carrying launchers when they emerge to fire; waves of heavy bombers have dropped munitions on the sites, apparently entombing Iranian weapons below ground in some locations. Satellite imagery shows smoldering remains of missiles and launchers destroyed near entrances to the "missile cities." Analysts say much of Tehran's remaining stockpile of thousands of medium- and short-range missiles likely remains in underground bases whose locations are mostly known to US and Israeli militaries. Sam Lair (James Martin Center for Nonproliferation Studies, Monterey): fundamental flaw in the missile-city concept — "What was once mobile and difficult to find is no longer mobile, and easier to hit."
