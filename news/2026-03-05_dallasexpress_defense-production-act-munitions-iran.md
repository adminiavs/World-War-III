## Full story

The Trump administration is reportedly discussing whether to invoke the Defense Production Act to compel U.S. manufacturers to accelerate output of munitions as stockpiles come under strain from the war with Iran.

The conversations, raised in private briefings with Congress, reflect growing concerns inside the administration and on Capitol Hill that sustained operations in Operation Epic Fury could deplete key reserves.

"We have unlimited middle and upper ammunition, which is really what we're using in this war," Trump told reporters. A White House official later clarified that "middle and upper" referred to the range of the munitions. White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt said the military has sufficient supplies to achieve the goals of Operation Epic Fury and beyond.

Gen. Dan Caine, chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff, had privately warned Trump before the strikes that air-defense stockpiles were already low following the 12-Day War with Iran in June 2025 and that a prolonged campaign now could create risks.

A senior White House official said Trump has not personally discussed invoking the law but wants contractors to move as quickly as possible. Analysts said the main concern centers on defensive missiles and interceptors rather than offensive munitions.

Mark Montgomery, senior fellow at the Foundation for Defense of Democracies, said the act could give the White House flexibility to draw down reserves knowing they could be rebuilt quickly. "That would solve next year's problem," Montgomery said.

Kelly Grieco of the Stimson Center estimated the U.S. has deployed about half its interceptor missiles to the Middle East and could exhaust them in roughly a month at current usage rates. Allies such as Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates also rely on the same Patriot systems, creating a long replenishment backlog.

Senate Majority Leader John Thune (R-SD) said after briefings that he feels "good about where we are" on defensive munitions and hopes the operation ends quickly enough to avoid emergency measures. Accurate figures remain classified.
