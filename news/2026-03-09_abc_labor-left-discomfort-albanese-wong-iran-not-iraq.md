# Labor acutely aware of risks in backing US-Israeli attacks on Iran

**Region/country:** Australia

## tl;dr

Growing quiet discomfort among Labor backbench and left-faction MPs with the "all-the-way-with-the-USA" framing of the government's response. Albanese backed the US despite knowing the action likely breaches international law; in 2003 he had said regime change in Iraq should be peaceful. Wong on Insiders: "This is not Iraq, and we are not the Howard government. We are not asking Australians to accept deployment into a ground war." Wong reiterated Australia not in a position to determine the legal basis of US-Israel decisions; Australia not engaged in offensive action; future of Iran is for the Iranian people; regime change by external parties historically unsustainable. Government may provide defensive military assistance to Gulf states (protection against drones and missiles) but would not join US attacks on Iran or deploy ground troops; would be transparent if decision made. Liberal senator Andrew McLachlan broke ranks to plead for "mercy" for 23 Australian children and 11 women in Syrian camps (ISIS families); no Labor MP has done the same. Karvelas: Labor left silence may be discipline or political timidity; lack of courage will be exploited by rivals. As more innocent victims mount and no clear strategy is articulated, government may need to pivot to keep a lid on irritated base.
