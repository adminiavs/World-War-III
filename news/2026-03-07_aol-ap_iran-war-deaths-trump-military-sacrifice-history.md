# Iran war deaths could resurface Trump's complicated history with military sacrifice

**Date:** 2026-03-07  
**Source:** AOL (Associated Press)  
**Author:** Jonathan J. Cooper  
**URL:** https://www.aol.com/articles/iran-war-deaths-could-resurface-050614044.html  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** US  
**Tags:** trump, dover, dignified-transfer, casualties, military

## tl;dr

Trump attended dignified transfer at Dover for six US service members killed in the Iran war. AP notes the moment is "potentially fraught" for a president whose White House has done little to build public support for the conflict and who has a record of controversy on military sacrifice. Trump can be reverential (recent Medal of Honor ceremonies) but also terse or dismissive — after launching Iran attacks he said of casualties "that's the way it is." Recaps past controversies: McCain "I like people who weren't captured"; alleged "suckers" and "losers" remark (denied); "knew what he signed up for" to widow; downplaying TBI after Soleimani strike ("headaches... not very serious"); joking about wanting a Purple Heart. At Dover he did not speak; later on Air Force One said "It's a very sad day," "glad we paid our respects."
