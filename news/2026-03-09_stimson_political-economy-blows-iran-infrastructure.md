# The Political Economy of Blows Against Iran's Military and Civilian Infrastructure

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Source:** Stimson Center
**Author:** Ali Dadpay (Editor's note: Barbara Slavin)
**URL:** https://www.stimson.org/2026/the-political-economy-of-blows-against-irans-military-and-civilian-infrastructure/
**Tags:** iran, air-defence, irgc, artesh, missiles, pahlavi

## tl;dr

Iran’s skies have become an “aerial highway” for US/Israeli jets; air defences cannot contest. Failure is not only technological but the result of decades of ideological favouritism: IRGC favoured over Artesh, billions on missiles and regional adventures while air defence was neglected. Iran inherited strong Pahlavi-era air force; post-1979 regime distrusted Artesh and built up IRGC; IRGC got political and resource priority. S-300 deal delayed (2007–2016); system vulnerable in 2025; December 2025 shoulder-fired missile deal came too late. Conventional forces received ~half IRGC funding. Missile program became prestige “Wunderwaffen”-style narrative. Damage to Iran’s infrastructure was predictable.
