## Key Historical Facts (Documented)

### The 1974 Secret Deal

- **August 1971**: Nixon closed the gold window, ending Bretton Woods dollar-gold convertibility
- **1973**: Arab oil embargo demonstrated Saudi geopolitical leverage
- **June 1974**: Kissinger, Treasury Secretary William Simon, and Saudi Prince Fahd established US-Saudi Joint Commission on Economic Cooperation (public)
- **Late 1974**: Separate SECRET agreement reached with two terms:
  1. Saudi Arabia would price oil exclusively in US dollars
  2. Saudi Arabia would invest surplus petrodollars in US Treasury bonds
- **This deal was hidden from the American public and most of Congress for 40 years**
- **2016**: Bloomberg FOIA request to National Archives revealed the confidential diplomatic cables

### Why It Mattered

- After gold backing ended, dollar needed new structural demand source
- When Saudi Arabia priced oil in dollars and accepted only dollars, every country needing Saudi oil needed to obtain dollars first
- By 1975, all OPEC nations followed Saudi lead
- Created "exorbitant privilege": US could run persistent deficits, borrow at lower rates, fund military operations without constraints other nations face
- "Control the oil pricing currency and you control the global reserve currency"

## Current Erosion (Documented)

### Saudi Arabia's Shift

- **2024**: Saudi Arabia joined BRICS (explicit goal: reduce dollar dependence in global trade)
- **2023 (Davos)**: Saudi finance minister publicly stated kingdom "open to settling oil trade in currencies other than the dollar" including euros and Saudi riyal
- **Project mBridge**: Saudi Arabia joined digital currency settlement platform (with China, Hong Kong, UAE, Thailand) operating entirely outside SWIFT

### Yuan Oil Settlement

- **November 2023**: China used digital yuan to purchase $90 million oil at Shanghai Petroleum and Natural Gas Exchange
- **As of early 2025**: Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iran, Venezuela, UAE, Egypt all participating in yuan-denominated oil settlement to varying degrees

### Current Dollar Position

- Dollar still accounts for ~80% of global oil trade
- Saudi Arabia still holds ~$144 billion in US Treasuries (and has been increasing)
- Saudi riyal still pegged to dollar
- BUT: the question is not overnight replacement but gradual erosion from 80% toward 60% toward 40%

## Structural Analysis

### The Margin Problem

> "Every percentage point of that structural demand that erodes means American borrowing costs are slightly higher than they would have been. Slightly higher borrowing costs on $36 trillion in federal debt is not a small problem. It is a trillion dollar problem compounding annually."

### Historical Pattern

- British pound was world's reserve currency 19th/early 20th century
- Not dethroned in single event — eroded over decades by:
  - Excessive debt from two world wars
  - Rise of more dynamic economy (US)
  - Loss of colonial arrangements ensuring pound-denominated trade
- "The pound did not collapse. It just mattered less year by year"

### Dollar Following Same Trajectory

> "The dollar is following the same trajectory not toward collapse but toward reduced dominance. And reduced dominance for a country that has built its entire fiscal model on the privileges that reserve currency status provides is not a comfortable adjustment."

Implications:
- Higher interest rates than market currently prices
- Inflation that cannot be easily suppressed
- Loss of ability to "spend more than you earn indefinitely"

### The Irony

> "Nixon closed the gold window in 1971 and replaced gold backing with oil backing through a secret deal the public would not learn about for 40 years. The consequences of that deal are now playing out as the oil backing itself begins to erode. Driven in part by the same dynamics that destroyed the Bretton Woods system: excessive debt, persistent deficits, the rise of economic competitors unwilling to accept the terms of the existing arrangement."
