## Full story

The US and Israel have continued to carry out strikes on Iran, targeting government, military and civilian infrastructure as the conflict has spread to Lebanon. The US consulate in Dubai was in flames on Tuesday as Iranian retaliatory strikes continued in the Gulf.

Iran has launched hundreds of missiles and drones, targeting energy infrastructure and diplomatic compounds. The conflict entered its fifth day on Wednesday.

**In Iran:** Nearly 800 people reported killed; 50 in Lebanon. Israeli PM Benjamin Netanyahu confirmed Israeli pilots are flying over Iran and Tehran; Israel will continue to strike "with force." Joint US-Israel strikes have targeted western Tehran. Iran launched 500+ ballistic missiles and 2,000 drones (per Adm Brad Cooper). Natanz nuclear facility was struck; Iranian media report no radioactive leakage.

**In Gulf nations:** Oman: US embassy in Muscat instructed staff and US citizens to shelter in place. Saudi Arabia: two drones struck US Embassy in Riyadh; Saudi said 10 suspected IRGC spies arrested. Qatar: air defence intercepted projectiles over Doha. Iran has warned it will target "all economic centres in the region" if attacks continue.

**In Israel/Lebanon:** Israeli military struck ~60 Hezbollah targets in southern Lebanon. Hezbollah announced missile attack on naval base in Haifa and rockets at Metula. Lebanese government banned Hezbollah military activities and demanded handover of weapons.

**In the US:** Four US soldiers killed in Iranian strike on facility in Kuwait (total confirmed US fatalities six). Democrats warn of prolonged ground war after Rubio briefing. Trump criticised UK PM Starmer; Senate scheduled to vote on War Powers resolution. Trump said "the big scale hitting goes now" and that regime change is not the primary goal.

**Lebanon, Iraq, Jordan:** Evacuation flights from Jordan (Slovak, Czech). Airstrikes in Iraq on pro-Iran armed groups. Israel's UN ambassador urged Lebanon to act to dismantle Hezbollah rockets.
