## Full article

The first two weeks of the joint US-Israel war on Iran has been one fought primarily from the air -- something that has not directly affected the lives of most Americans. But that could change if a ground invasion were to be launched.

David Frank (professor of rhetoric, University of Oregon): "With boots on the ground, it would be over for the Trump administration." He was referring to the November 2026 midterm election, in which Democrats are expected to win back the House and possibly the Senate, which would essentially make Trump a lame duck president. "The Vietnam War ended when people saw body bags coming home."

Around two weeks in: at least 11 US military members have died and 140 have been wounded.

A recent New York Times poll found 41 percent of Americans support the conflict -- "the most unpopular foreign war in modern US history." 74% oppose a ground invasion (Quinnipiac).

Ohannes Geukjian (American University of Beirut): for now he doesn't expect US ground forces in Iran, although there is a possibility of limited special operations, depending on the situation with the Kurds. "Currently, sending ground troops is unlikely. It may become likely if the Kurdish and other minorities mobilise."

White House press secretary Karoline Leavitt refused to rule out a military draft or a ground invasion in Iran, leading to widespread criticism from both parties.

Marjorie Taylor Greene has been warning that Republicans could lose the midterms over the war in Iran, particularly if it escalates to a ground invasion or a draft.

Frank: "It would be a disaster if we continue to not learn from history. There's no plan for the day after."
