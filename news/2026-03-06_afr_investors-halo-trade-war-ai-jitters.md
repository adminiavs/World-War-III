## Full story

As the conflict in the Middle East rages on and investors wait for the next catalyst to set in motion either a dramatic rebound or prolonged volatility, strategists are starting to recommend a new trade: "HALO" stocks. Defined as companies with "heavy assets and low obsolescence," the term was coined by Josh Brown, chief executive of Ritholtz Wealth Management in the United States, last month. It refers to "old economy" stocks that have little exposure to the Iran war or are at risk of artificial intelligence disruption. (AFR, 6 Mar 2026; Gus McCubbing.)
