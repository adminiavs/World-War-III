# Shrinking weapon stockpiles and regime-change uncertainty: doubts shadow US-Israel war on Iran

**Date:** 2026-03-07  
**Source:** The Guardian  
**URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/world/2026/mar/07/iran-us-israel-war-regime-change

---

## tl;dr

Washington Post reported a classified National Intelligence Council review (completed mid-February) found the war in Iran is **unlikely to oust the Iranian establishment** despite the Trump administration's desire to continue. In both scenarios the US considered, Iran's government would follow protocols for choosing a successor to the supreme leader. After Khamenei's assassination, Iran quickly named an interim leadership council (president and other top officials) to choose the next supreme leader. Intelligence officials said it was "unlikely" Iran's opposition would take control. Democrats warn airstrikes are **diminishing US stockpiles** of certain weapons; concern arose in closed-door briefing with Congress. Lockheed Martin agreed Friday to "quadruple critical munitions production." Blumenthal (Time): "deeply concerned about Ukraine"; US military "resources and supplies are limited." FDD scholar Brobst (AP): concern is not running out during this conflict but "deterring China and Russia the day after." Trump at Shield of the Americas (Florida): "We've knocked out 42 navy ships... end of the navy. We've knocked out the air force. All telecommunications is gone." AP had reported the deadly strike on the school (168 girls) likely came from the US.
