## Full article

The Pentagon is reportedly sending a unit that includes as many as three warships and up to 2,500 Marines to the Middle East (Wall Street Journal, citing unnamed US defense officials).

The USS Tripoli and the 31st Marine Expeditionary Unit is usually stationed in Japan but is being moved to the region as Iran increases attacks on the Strait of Hormuz.

In addition to 5,000 Marines and sailors total, the unit also includes F-35B Lightning II jets and MV-22B Ospreys, which can be used for troop insertions.

The unit will join more than 50,000 troops already in the region and will take roughly two weeks to reach the area.

The additional troops and warships do not necessarily mean the Pentagon is planning to put boots on the ground, but does make a larger force available should a ground invasion be ordered. The Trump administration has not ruled out the possibility of ground troops in the conflict.

To address oil prices: Trump lifted some sanctions on Russian oil and the US is releasing 172 million barrels from the strategic reserve. Roughly one-fifth of the world's oil moves through the Strait of Hormuz and a pause in activity has contributed to a spike in oil prices globally.
