## Full story

Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu posted a video of himself getting coffee and chatting with his aide on Sunday after rumours that he was dead or injured were aired by Iranian state media and spread online in Iran. In the video, from a cafe on Jerusalem's outskirts and posted on his Telegram account, his aide asks about the rumours. Netanyahu responds with a pun on the word dead (in Hebrew slang it can mean "crazy about") as he reaches for coffee. Reuters verified the video's location and date. Since the US and Israel launched attacks on Iran on 28 February, Netanyahu has visited at least two towns hit by Iranian missiles, a hospital, port and military bases, with little to no media access. He convened his first press conference since the war started via video link on Thursday. Emergency safety restrictions in Israel have kept most people at home or near shelters, with schools shut across most of the country.
