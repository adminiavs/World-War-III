# 'Proud as a Qatari': Qatar PM salutes resilience amid Iranian attacks

**Date:** 2026-03-11  
**Source:** Al Jazeera  
**URL:** https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/11/proud-as-a-qatari-countrys-pm-salutes-resilience-amid-iranian-attacks  
**Region:** Qatar, Gulf  

---

## tl;dr

Qatar's PM Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim Al Thani told a cabinet meeting (11 Mar) that Iran had targeted civilian as well as military sites in Qatar "showing little regard for the harm inflicted." He praised "resilience" and "cohesion of our society and the unity of its ranks, citizens and residents alike" and said he was "proud, as a Qatari." Same day Qatar's UN ambassador condemned Iranian strikes as a violation of international law; UNSC voted in favour of a resolution denouncing Iranian attacks on GCC states. PM has called on both sides to return to negotiations; "Iran's strikes on its neighbours serve benefit for no one."

## Key points

- Iran targeted "not just military sites" but "civilian sites" in Qatar.
- PM committed to ensuring "lives of everyday people" are not disrupted; Emir called on officials to keep "normal course of life" uninterrupted.
- Qatar's armed forces working "day and night" for safety and security.
- Explosions reported in Doha Wednesday; military said it intercepted Iranian missiles.
- UNSC later voted for resolution denouncing Iranian strikes on GCC states.
- Qatar among Gulf nations facing Iranian strikes since US-Israel offensive began 28 Feb; >1,300 civilians killed in Iran; Tehran says nearly 10,000 civilian sites bombed.
- Saudi, UAE, Oman have intercepted or absorbed strikes; drones hit fuel tanks at Oman's Salalah port Wednesday.
