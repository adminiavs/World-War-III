## tl;dr

South Korea's Kospi triggered its second circuit breaker in four sessions on Monday 9 March, leading a regional sell-off as oil breached $100 a barrel for the first time since 2022. Kospi plunged over 8%, 20-minute trading suspension from 10:31 a.m. local time. Samsung Electronics down more than 10%, SK Hynix down 11.6%. Japan's Nikkei 225 tumbled 6.48%, below 53,000 for first time since 6 Feb; Topix down 5.8%. Softbank over 11% lower; Advantest and Lasertec down over 10% and 9%. Australia's S&P/ASX 200 fell 4.15%. Hong Kong Hang Seng down 3%; CSI 300 (mainland China) down 2%. Brent futures spiked 18.38% to $109.84; WTI up nearly 20.88% to $109.83. Surge follows Kuwait, Iran and UAE cutting production after Strait of Hormuz closure. Trump on Truth Social: short-term oil gain "very small price to pay" for destroying Iran nuclear threat; "Only fools would think differently!" Dow futures down over 800 points (1.75%); S&P 500 and Nasdaq-100 futures down ~1.6%. Circuit breaker was also triggered last week when Kospi tumbled more than 12% on Wednesday (worst single-day decline).
