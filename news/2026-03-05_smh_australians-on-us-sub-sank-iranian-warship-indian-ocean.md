## Full story

Two Australians were onboard the nuclear-powered US submarine that sank the Iranian frigate IRIS Dena in the Indian Ocean near Sri Lanka. This masthead understands they are on board as part of an AUKUS training rotation. Defence refused to identify rank or role; for operational security the ADF does not disclose third-country deployment details. The frigate was returning from joint exercises off India when attacked Wednesday. Albanese confirmed military assets (C-17 Globemaster, KC-30A tanker) deployed to the Middle East; fastest way out is commercial flights. Kurdish forces on the Iran-Iraq border are preparing to join the war. White House denied Trump agreed to any Kurdish military plan but confirmed he spoke to Kurdish leaders. Senate voted down a resolution to constrain Trump's ability to order further strikes without congressional approval. Hegseth confirmed the US sank the frigate; first US torpedo sink of an enemy vessel since WWII. He declined to give more on the girls' school strike (165 dead); US never targets civilians. Leavitt confirmed Netanyahu tipped Trump that Iranian senior leaders were gathering Saturday; Trump already had a "feeling" Iran would strike US assets. Iran's Ali Larijani called Trump "clownish" and "Israel First." Tucker Carlson: "This is Israel's war … The United States committed troops because the prime minister of Israel demanded it."
