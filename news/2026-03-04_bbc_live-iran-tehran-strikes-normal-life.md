## Full story

Summary and live reporting from BBC News live page (4 March 2026).

Iranians tell the BBC the country's capital Tehran "feels empty" and that they "deserve a normal life", as the Israeli military says it has launched a new "wave of strikes" there.

Earlier, US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed a US submarine sank an Iranian warship in the Indian Ocean; the Pentagon released footage of the strike. About 180 people were thought to have been on board the Iris Dena; 32 were rescued and Sri Lankan authorities say 80 bodies have so far been found.

At the same briefing, Chairman of the Joint Chiefs of Staff Gen Dan Caine said the US will strike "progressively deeper into Iranian territory." Meanwhile, President Masoud Pezeshkian said Iran had "no choice" but to respond to the US and Israel's joint operation. Israel says it has intercepted new missiles launched by Iran; a three-day funeral ceremony for Iran's ex-Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has been postponed.

Live reporting included: Trump at White House round table saying if the US had not hit within two weeks Iran would have had a nuclear weapon; Hezbollah leader Naim Qassem saying the group "will not surrender" and that Israel did not abide by the ceasefire; Qatar's Foreign Minister calling his Iranian counterpart and rejecting the claim that Iran's attacks targeted US interests not Qatar; Spanish FM Albares saying Spain's position "has not changed" and "no to war" after White House said Spain agreed to cooperate; French President Macron meeting Netanyahu, Aoun and Salam on Lebanon and calling for Hezbollah to cease attacks and Israel to preserve Lebanon's territorial integrity; analysis that Iran's ballistic missile launches have dropped 86% and drones 73%, with US/Israel now using cheaper GPS-guided bombs; White House Press Secretary Karoline Leavitt's briefing on Iran objectives, regime "lying and delaying," and DoD investigating the school strike.
