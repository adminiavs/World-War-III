## Full story

Strategies reviewed. Decapitation removes elite and command and control, limiting Iran's ability to govern. Air supremacy: advanced aircraft destroy military installations and conduct sabotage; carpet bombing and soft targeting (e.g. hospitals) to degrade state resilience. Double tap: strike a place, then strike again when doctors, emergency personnel or relatives arrive to maximise civilian casualties and fear; described as against international law. Third pillar: arm and bribe insurgents (Baluch, Kurds, others). Argument: these help Iran. Decapitation solves elite overproduction and allows leaner, more meritocratic leadership. Carpet bombing cities (where support for progressive policies was) unites urban and rural; Iranians see US and Israel as enemies. Arming minorities activates Persian nationalism and long-lasting identity. US strategy worked in Libya and Syria but backfires in Iran; US is an empire trying to win cheaply and fast without political will or manufacturing capacity. Iranian strategy: siege met with guerrilla warfare. Hide in mountains when US bombs; when US leaves, strike GCC and Israel with drones and rockets. Be a pain in the ass for years; force US to return. Ground invasion would mean US loss; Iran is impossible to occupy. Why is the war happening? Administration not clear. Trump cited nuclear talks; Rubio said Israelis were going to attack first and Iran would hit both, so preemption. Reporting (e.g. John Larson, Substack): US troops told Iran war is for Armageddon, return of Jesus. Christian Zionism. Lecture frames this in game theory: opponents may not want material rewards; real power is human consciousness and attention; consciousness creates reality; war for the soul of humanity; whoever wins controls the soul of human existence.
