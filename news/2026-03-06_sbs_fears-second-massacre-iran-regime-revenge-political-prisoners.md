## Full story

Amid escalating conflict, human rights groups have expressed grave concern for the thousands of political prisoners held in Iranian jails. Gholam Hossein Mohseni-Eje'i, head of the judiciary, said on Wednesday that those who support the US and Israel will be treated as the "enemy" and "must be dealt with based on the same revolutionary and Islamic principles as wartime." The Center for Human Rights in Iran (CHRI) warned that the regime "has a history of using the shadow of war and crisis to carry out abuses in prisons and retaliate against political prisoners." More than 50,000 were arrested during January protests (HRANA); at least 7,000 killed (figures disputed). Families report that a group of prisoners in Evin have been moved to an unknown location. Deniz Toupchi (Brisbane), whose friend is detained, said the regime could use prisoners as human shields or move them to potential strike locations. Atena Daemi (former political prisoner, Canada) said "even those who are in Evin prison right now are human shields" and that the regime may "take their revenge on prisoners." Iran Human Rights Society reported Evin faces "emergency situation" with food and basic necessities severely limited; NOPO riot police surrounding the prison; prisoners told any action would be met with direct gunfire. Amnesty International reported at least 30 people linked to the January protests face the death penalty, including two 17-year-olds; Zaki Haidari warned executions might be used to create fear while the world is not monitoring. Mohammad Hashemi (Sydney), whose cousin Majid Kazemi was executed in 2023, said "we are really worried about the second massacre"; "we all know how brutal they are." Last June, during the 12-day conflict, Evin was struck (around 80 dead); CHRI said the facility was unlawfully targeted and authorities failed to evacuate detainees.
