## Full story

Washington, DC – The United States Senate is expected to hold an initial vote on a resolution to rein in US President Donald Trump's war with Iran, with top Democrat Chuck Schumer saying he fears "now more than ever" that the administration is planning to deploy boots on the ground.

The procedural vote expected on Wednesday represents the first time US lawmakers will be put on the record on their position towards the war, which the US and Israel began on Saturday. Pentagon Chief Pete Hegseth on Wednesday said the operation had just begun, with more US assets being sent to the region. Schumer said that showed "it is clear they are widening the war … and I fear now more than ever that we are going to put boots on the ground."

Speaking from the Senate floor, Democrats condemned shifting justifications for the war. Schumer portrayed Trump as unmoored by evidence: "Whatever pops into his head, he says immediately. He picks one plan one day, then he picks the total opposite the next." Dick Durbin pointed to the array of rationales offered and scant concrete evidence, and compared the situation to the 2003 Iraq vote: "It is far easier to get into a war than it is to get out of a war."

Republicans hold a slim majority; at least one Democrat (Fetterman) will oppose the resolution and one Republican (Rand Paul) is co-sponsoring. Republican John Barrasso said: "Democrats would rather obstruct President Trump than obliterate Iran's national nuclear programme" and that Trump "absolutely acted within his Article Two constitutional powers."

Even if the resolution passes both chambers, Trump would veto it; overriding would require a two-thirds majority in each. Advocates say the vote forces accountability and puts members on record.
