# Australian share market plunges 3.4 per cent as escalating Iran war sends oil soaring

**Source:** The Nightly (Australia)

**Author:** Sean Smith  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** Australia  
**Tags:** australia, asx, stock-market, oil-prices, iran-war, 100-billion

## tl;dr

Australian shares plunged 3.4%, losing more than $100 billion in value, after oil prices soared as much as 22% on the Iran war. S&P/ASX200 opened Monday down 2.7% then widened; at 7.50am was 3.35% or 300.4 points lower at 8550.6. One of the worst days of the past year. Investors worried the war will kick inflation higher and threaten global growth. Oil punched through $100 a barrel to highest since Russia invasion of Ukraine four years ago; Kuwait and UAE reducing output as storage nearly full with Strait of Hormuz closure. Brent spiked as much as 20% to $111.04; WTI jumped 22%. Trump on Truth Social: oil movements are a very small price to pay for the US, the world, and peace.
