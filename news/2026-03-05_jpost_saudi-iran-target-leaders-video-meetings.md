## Full story

Saudi Arabia is concerned that Iran may attempt to target senior officials in the kingdom, either directly or via the Houthis in Yemen. One precautionary measure Riyadh has adopted is to hold meetings among ministers and senior officials via video; Saudi leaders do not typically hold high-level video meetings during routine periods. Since the war began, Iran has launched dozens of missiles and drones toward Saudi Arabia. Targets hit include the CIA headquarters in Riyadh, Aramco oil facilities, and other civilian infrastructure. This crossed a red line for Saudi Arabia, prompting the kingdom to begin discussions about the possibility of striking Iran. Bernard Haykel (Princeton, Hudson Institute): Saudi Arabia strategic calculations have fundamentally changed because Iran attacked vital civilian infrastructure. The Saudi red line was that if their civilian facilities were attacked, they would strike Iran; that is what is happening now. Michael Ratney (former US ambassador to Saudi Arabia): the Iranian attacks are far worse than expected and are making the kingdom very angry, but the response may still be restrained due to fears of uncontrollable chaos. The Jerusalem Post, 5 Mar 2026.
