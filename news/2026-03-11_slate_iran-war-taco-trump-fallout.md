## tl;dr

Slate analysis: Trump may be trying to "TACO" (Trump Always Chickens Out) his way out of Iran — pattern of bluster then retreat when policies "touch the hot stove of reality." Monday he told CBS "war very complete, pretty much," reversing sliding stocks and bringing oil below $90; same day at Doral told House Republicans "we've already won in many ways but we haven't won enough," "we're gonna go further." Wednesday: war would end "soon" ("practically nothing left to target") but also "we could do a lot worse," "we're not finished yet." TACO's limit: "Wars don't end just because someone declares them to be finished" (Armstrong, FT). Iran may not let him — Mojtaba's election signals resolve; Iranian drones "finding their mark" as interceptors flag; Hormuz throttled; Tuesday Iran reportedly began laying mines, Pentagon destroyed minelaying ships. Netanyahu and regional neighbours also have veto power. Even if he TACO's, Trump often returns (tariffs, election claims, Iran bombed before); "another could be right around the corner."
