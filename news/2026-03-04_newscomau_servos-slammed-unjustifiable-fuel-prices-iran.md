## Full story

Fuel prices have already hit astonishing levels in some parts of Australia, amid the war on Iran hitting drivers at the bowser. Remote parts of Northern Territory, including Ramingining in Arnhem Land, are paying $3.99 per litre for diesel; Milingimbi $3.95 and $3.79. Multiple cities reported panic buying and queues earlier in the week.

Industry Minister Tim Ayres accused the Coalition of being "political war profiteers" whose "far right, extremist scaremongering" created people lining up at petrol stations when there was no need. He said Australia's fuel reserves were in the best position they had been at in 15 years. He also attacked the Coalition front bench and former Morrison government's energy record.

Petrol stations across the country are being slammed for charging "unjustifiable" prices. NRMA spokesman Peter Khoury said half of service stations across Sydney, Melbourne and Brisbane are charging almost $2.20 a litre and that retailers are using the Middle East crisis as an excuse to jack up margins; it takes 7–10 days for increased oil prices to flow through. Treasurer Jim Chalmers said he had written to the ACCC to ensure service stations "don't do the wrong thing" and that "retailers cannot be taking people for mugs."

About 20 per cent of the world's oil and gas is shipped through the Strait of Hormuz, a shipping lane on Iran's southern border that has now been effectively closed. Fuel prices have spiked globally as the conflict continues into its fourth day.
