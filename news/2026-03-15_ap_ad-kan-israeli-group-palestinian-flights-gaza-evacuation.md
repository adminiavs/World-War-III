## tl;dr

AP investigation: Israeli group Ad Kan (founder Gilad Ach, settler activist, staunch supporter of Trump's Gaza resettlement proposal) organized mystery flights evacuating Palestinians from Gaza to Indonesia and South Africa via front company Al-Majd. At least three flights since May (Nov landing in South Africa with ~150; May flight ~60 to Indonesia; Oct ~170 to South Africa via Kenya). Ach had published report on implementing "voluntary exit" and supported Trump's transfer plan; Ad Kan works covertly to infiltrate and expose anti-Israel activity. South African FM Lamola called flights "clear agenda to cleanse out the Palestinians out of Gaza and the West Bank." Passengers paid up to $2,000; many didn't know who organized; Israel's Voluntary Emigration Bureau (Defense Ministry) runs departures. Israel approached Somaliland, South Sudan, Sudan to facilitate emigration. Ach denied cleansing motive; said humanitarian. Palestinians need Israeli permission to leave Gaza.

## Full story

An Israeli group whose founder supported Trump's proposal to resettle Palestinians from Gaza is behind flights that have evacuated hundreds of Gazans to Indonesia and South Africa, an AP investigation found. Ad Kan ("enough is enough"), founded by soldiers and former intelligence officers, used another company (Al-Majd, presenting as humanitarian "supporting Palestinian lives") to distance links to Israel. Gilad Ach, West Bank settler activist, was a staunch supporter of Trump's proposal to transfer 2 million Palestinians out of Gaza; he published a report on implementing "voluntary exit" within six to eight months. Since May, at least three flights have landed—November in South Africa with ~150, May ~60 to Indonesia, October ~170 to South Africa via Kenya. Contract with Ad Kan showed Moti Kahana engaged to provide "flight rescue service" for $750,000+; route later changed to South Africa. South Africa revoked 90-day visa exemptions for Palestinian passport holders after the November flight, citing "deliberate and ongoing abuse" by Israelis linked to emigration efforts. Israel created the Voluntary Emigration Bureau under the Defense Ministry; Israel has approached Somaliland, South Sudan and Sudan to facilitate emigration. Ach denied ethnic-cleansing allegation and said flights were humanitarian; passengers who spoke to AP said they didn't know who was behind the trips but wanted to leave. Palestinians need Israeli permission to leave Gaza; COGAT said it allows departures if they have visas to destination countries.
