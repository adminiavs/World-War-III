## Full story

**Kurdish ground offensive:** "Thousands" of Kurdish militants have crossed the Iraqi border and launched a ground offensive in northwestern Iran, according to multiple reports. An official from the Coalition of Political Forces of Iranian Kurdistan (CPFIK) said Kurdish armed groups based in Iraq had already begun a military offensive against Iranian regime forces (i24 News). Fox News correspondent Jennifer Griffin reported on X that a US official told the network "thousands of Iraqi Kurds" were involved; Axios correspondent Barak Ravid posted that a "senior American official confirmed to me in a conversation: Kurdish-Iranian militias have launched a ground offensive." Kurdish fighters affiliated with the Kurdistan Free Life Party (PJAK) began taking combat positions inside Iranian territory on Monday, 2 March. Donald Trump spoke with Kurdish leaders at the weekend as he considers arming militias to dislodge the Iranian regime. The US has admitted it torpedoed an Iranian military vessel, killing at least 87 sailors and possibly as many as 140.

**US Senate:** Confronting footage showed a Senator and three security guards dragging former Marine and anti-war protester Brian McGinnis out of the US Senate. McGinnis shouted "Israel is the reason for this war, America does not want to fight this war" before being removed.

**Carney in Canberra:** At a press conference in Canberra, Canadian PM Mark Carney said he could not rule out Canada's military participation in the escalating war. "You've asked a fundamental hypothetical in a conflict that can spread very broadly. Today's events, recent events, point to that," Carney said at the joint presser with Anthony Albanese. "One can never categorically rule out participation. We will stand by our allies when it makes sense." Both Carney and Albanese said the war must be de-escalated.

**Regional:** Residents near the US embassy in Doha are being evacuated as a temporary precautionary measure (Qatar interior ministry). The US has closed embassies in Beirut, Riyadh and Kuwait and heavily reduced diplomatic presence across the Middle East. Emirates flights to and from Dubai suspended until Saturday 7 March; limited schedule, transit only if connecting flight operating.

**Israel:** Israel's military said air defences were responding to missiles launched from Iran (first such report in over seven hours), with alerts in several areas including Tel Aviv.

**Australia:** Albanese told a joint sitting of parliament with Carney that "if ever nations such as ours had the luxury of imagining that distance alone kept us safe, those days are certainly gone." He cited the Iranian regime's 2024 attacks on a synagogue and business in Australia. Aviation expert Ron Bartsch advised Australians to reconsider travel to Europe for the next five months. DFAT told travellers "all options" to get stranded Australians out are on the table. Associate Professor Foad Izadi (University of Tehran) advised not to take seriously yet claims that Iran had elected a new Supreme Leader (Mojtaba Khamenei): "The news that we have is they haven't voted yet."
