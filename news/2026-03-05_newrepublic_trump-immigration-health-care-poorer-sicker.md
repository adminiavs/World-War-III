# Trump's immigration policies are making us poorer and sicker

**Date:** 2026-03-05  
**Source:** The New Republic  
**URL:** https://newrepublic.com/article/207357/immigrants-crucial-health-care-jobs  
**Author:** Grace Segers  
**Content type:** news (policy)  
**Region/country:** US  
**Tags:** immigration, trump, health-care, h1b, labour-force, domestic-policy

## tl;dr

Despite Trump's claim that limiting immigration gives jobs to American-born citizens, evidence shows lower immigration reduces workforce growth, economic vitality and wage growth; native-born unemployment was higher in Jan 2026 than a year earlier. Brookings: net migration close to zero or negative in 2025, likely negative in 2026. H1-B fee raised to 100,000 dollars; experts say high-skilled immigrants boost productivity and wages; Cato found immigrants reduce deficits. Immigrants account for ~18% of health care workers; NBER paper: 25% increase in immigration would mean 5,000 fewer elder deaths annually. Home health aides ~40% immigrant; crackdowns reduce elder care and aging in place. Construction, hospitality, agriculture also heavily affected. Tangential to Iran war: domestic policy context during wartime.
