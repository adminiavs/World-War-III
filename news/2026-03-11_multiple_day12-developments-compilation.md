## Military / Casualty Revelations

### Landstuhl Hospital "Flooded" With Injured Troops
- **30+ military personnel hospitalized** at Landstuhl Regional Medical Center in Germany from March 1 Kuwait strike
- 20 arrived with injuries designated "urgent" requiring immediate evacuation
- Injuries include: **traumatic brain injuries, memory loss, burns, shrapnel wounds**
- **100+ medical personnel** dispatched to Landstuhl to handle surge
- 12 additional troops at Walter Reed; 1 at Brooke Army Medical Center in San Antonio
- Pentagon initially downplayed: claimed only 5 seriously wounded, "several minor shrapnel injuries and concussions"
- Hegseth called the strike that killed 6 troops "a squirter"
- First-person account from Sgt. First Class Cory Hicks: "I remember turning my head to the left and I'd seen the nose of that drone pop through... everything was just smoke and fire and crazy and chaos"
- **Gap between Pentagon's public statements and actual casualty numbers widening**

### Bahrain Military Family Evacuations — Chaotic Departures
- First alert to evacuate came **less than an hour before explosions began**
- "THIS IS NOT A DRILL" text followed 42 minutes later by "MISSILE THREAT WARNING. Seek hardened shelter immediately. Real World."
- One of largest movements of US military families out of Middle East in years
- **No explanation** why US waited until AFTER launching Operation Epic Fury to authorize departures
- Families spent days sheltering in closets, listening to drones and missiles
- Children asking "Is Iran bombing here now, too?" and saying "no closet"
- Service member: "It was plenty of time to evacuate Americans" before initiating conflict
- Retired Cmdr. Chris Moran: "If we're going to do an evacuation, we need to do it ahead of time... once the war starts, you don't have control over everything anymore"

## War Costs / Domestic Impact

### War Costing $1 Billion Per Day
- Pentagon estimates $1 billion/day (Atlantic journalist Nancy Youssef citing congressional official)
- For same amount, US could cover:
  - Daily cost of food stamps for **41 million people**
  - Daily cost of Medicaid for **16 million people** expected to lose coverage due to cuts
- National Priorities Project: "It's not protecting Americans, but it is preventing Americans from having enough resources"
- American Prospect: "The government could end all of the suffering in our country. We could have health care and access to food"

### Fed in "Challenging Position" — Stagflation Risk
- February inflation held at 2.4% (before war's impact fully priced in)
- RSM chief economist: headline inflation could climb to 3% in March, "3.5% or greater" in April
- Gas prices: **$3.58/gallon** (up 64 cents in past month, 22% higher than last month)
- US economy **lost 92,000 jobs** in February (plus 69,000 downward revision to Dec/Jan)
- Citi: individual federal tax refunds tracking $30 billion higher than last year — well below estimates
- Fed faces "stagflation" scenario: higher prices + slowing growth = can't cut rates to ease pressure
- RSM: "The Federal Reserve may step away from any action on interest rates" until Hormuz opens

### Trump Tapping Strategic Petroleum Reserve
- Trump to Cincinnati station WKRC: "We'll do that, and then we'll fill it up"
- US currently has **415 million barrels** in SPR (58% of authorized 714 million capacity)
- Interior Secretary Burgum: Trump will decide whether US participates in IEA action

### IEA Releases 400 Million Barrels — Largest in History
- 32 IEA member countries agreed to release
- More than **twice the amount** released during Russia's Ukraine invasion
- Could replace supplies off market for roughly 3 weeks (SMU Maguire Energy Institute)
