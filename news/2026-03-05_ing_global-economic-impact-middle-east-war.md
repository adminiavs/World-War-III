## Full story

ING base case: roughly two weeks of ongoing combat (US, Israel, Iran, entire region); air traffic down, Strait of Hormuz basically blocked. After two weeks, political instability in Iran remains but broader uncertainty recedes and Hormuz gradually reopens; within 4–6 weeks conditions could return close to pre-war. Downside risks: boots on the ground, longer military action, Iranian retaliation (sleeper cells, cyber). Global trade: war adds to strain from Trump tariffs and supply-chain fragmentation; Hormuz is the single most important energy chokepoint in an active warzone; insurers cancelling, shipping premiums spiking, vessels re-routing; Gulf airspace disrupts Europe–Asia aviation; Houthi reactivation would close Red Sea alternative. Oil supply at risk from Hormuz blockade 15–20% of global (vs 7–8% Russian oil early Ukraine war); OECD stocks ~200m barrels higher than pre Ukraine but two-week full blockade would erase buffer. LNG up to 125bcm at risk. US: higher oil prices fuel cost-of-living, gasoline politically sensitive for midterms; second supply-side inflation shock plus tariffs could make rate cuts hard to justify; growth darkens if conflict drags. Eurozone most exposed: emerging from stagnation, energy shock on top of trade shock; fiscal capacity more under pressure than 2022. Asia: heavy dependence on Middle East oil; trade balances and inflation pass-through at risk. (ING THINK, 5 Mar 2026.)
