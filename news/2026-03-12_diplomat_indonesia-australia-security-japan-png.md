# Indonesia and Australia to expand security cooperation to include Japan, Papua New Guinea

**Date:** 12 March 2026
**Source:** The Diplomat
**URL:** https://thediplomat.com/2026/03/indonesia-and-australia-to-expand-security-cooperation-to-include-japan-png/

---

## tl;dr

A month after signing the Jakarta Treaty (Australia-Indonesia Treaty on Common Security), Indonesia and Australia plan to expand their security cooperation into two trilateral arrangements: Indonesia-Australia-Japan, and Indonesia-Australia-Papua New Guinea. Indonesian Defence Minister Sjafrie Sjamsoeddin met Australian Defence Minister Richard Marles in Jakarta on March 12. Also discussed: a joint defence training facility on Morotai island (North Maluku) -- a WWII-era strategic air base. An Indonesian colonel will embed with Australian troops in Darwin from early next year. Framed explicitly as a response to China's assertiveness and a "distracted" Trump-era US. The Diplomat: "As long as things continue to unravel, similar trilateral and minilateral security agreements will continue to proliferate."

---

## Key details

**The Jakarta Treaty (February 2026):** Australia-Indonesia Treaty on Common Security -- based on the 1995 Suharto-Keating treaty. Commits both countries to increased security consultations; joint military training facilities in Indonesia; information sharing; leader/minister-level consultations on security matters.

**New trilateral arrangements announced (March 12):**
1. Indonesia-Australia-Japan
2. Indonesia-Australia-Papua New Guinea

**Morotai island facility:** Small island in North Maluku, North Maluku province. Was a major Japanese base in WWII, captured by Allied forces in 1944; became a strategic air base for the liberation of the Philippines. An operational airfield still exists. Indonesia and Australia discussing developing it as a joint defence training facility. Marles: the facility would belong to Indonesia; Indonesia would determine use; "opportunities for Australia to engage in training there." Philippines and Singapore military personnel would also have access.

**Darwin embedding:** An Indonesian colonel will embed with Australian troops as deputy commander of the 1st Brigade of the Australian Army in Darwin from early next year.

**Context:** Australia has existing frameworks with both PNG (Mutual Defence Treaty, October 2025) and Japan (Reciprocal Access Agreement, 2022). Indonesia has also recently expanded defence ties with PNG and Japan.

**Why now:** The Diplomat is explicit -- this is a response to China's growing assertiveness and the "distant, mercurial, and increasingly distracted" Trump administration. Middle powers are hedging by building regional security architecture that doesn't depend on the US being reliable.

**The Iran war connection:** Australia's Defence Minister Marles is simultaneously managing the UAE/Gulf deployment (Wedgetail, missiles, 85 personnel) and deepening regional security ties with Indonesia. These are related: as the Iran war tests the US alliance and exposes AUKUS dependence, Australia is also building alternative regional relationships. Indonesia has NOT joined the Iran war coalition; it maintains a formal non-aligned stance. The security cooperation with Jakarta is therefore strategically significant -- it is not Iran-war coalition building, but broader Indo-Pacific architecture in an era of US unreliability.
