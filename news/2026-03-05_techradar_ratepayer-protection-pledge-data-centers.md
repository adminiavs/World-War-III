# Tech giants sign White House pledge to not pass on data center electricity costs to consumers

**Date:** 2026-03-05  
**Source:** TechRadar Pro  
**URL:** https://www.techradar.com/pro/the-american-people-should-not-be-footing-the-bill-for-the-benefit-of-private-companies-tech-giants-sign-white-house-pledge-to-not-pass-on-data-center-electricity-costs-to-consumers-heres-everything-you-need-to-know  
**Author:** Benedict Collins  
**Tags:** trump, data-centers, ai, energy, ratepayer-pledge, oracle, amazon, microsoft, openai

## tl;dr

White House introduced voluntary Ratepayer Protection Pledge so private companies bear cost of rising electricity demand from data centers powering AI. Amazon, Google, Meta, Microsoft, OpenAI, Oracle, xAI signed. Trump: tech companies and data centers will get electricity they need without driving up costs for consumers. Pledge is voluntary and not legally binding. Areas with heavy data center build have seen prices rise as much as 267% over five years. Trump administration cut renewables subsidies and expedited fossil fuel projects; NEDC secured $15B for Mid-Atlantic and Midwest. Supply chain and America first industry may conflict with companies building new energy.
