## Full story

Donald Trump was at Dover Air Force Base Saturday for the dignified transfer of the six United States service members killed in Kuwait, and there were some folks who felt like the President did not handle himself in a very dignified manner.

The issue: as others bowed their heads, Trump did not appear to do so. Also, he wore a white USA baseball cap that he did not take off during the ceremony.

Former RNC chairman Michael Steele wrote on X while sharing a photo of Trump with his hat on: "This fool has ABSOLUTELY no sense of dignity of appreciation for the moment. It is called the Dignified Transfer for a reason. Take your hat off."

Others on X called it disgraceful, contrasted it with past criticism of Biden for glancing at his watch, and noted that Trump wore a campaign-style hat while dead US soldiers were taken off the plane at Dover. One user wrote: "The president of the United States has a baseball cap on during a dignified transfer of six American service members who were killed in war with Iran. He could not even be bothered to take his hat off."

The soldiers killed in action were Maj. Jeffrey O'Brien, Capt. Cody Khork, Chief Warrant Officer 3 Robert Marzan, Sgt. 1st Class Nicole Amor, Sgt. 1st Class Noah Tietjens and Sgt. Declan Coady. They were killed in a drone strike at a command center in Kuwait after the U.S. and Israel launched the military campaign against Iran. AP photo caption identified Sgt. 1st Class Noah Tietjens, 42, of Bellevue, Neb. First lady Melania Trump and White House Special Envoy to the Middle East Steve Witkoff were also present at the ceremony.
