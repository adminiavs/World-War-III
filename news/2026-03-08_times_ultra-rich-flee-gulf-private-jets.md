## Full story

As hundreds of thousands of tourists in the Middle East wait anxiously for news of scant repatriation flights, a smaller group of ultra-wealthy people have sought a more exclusive way out: private jets.

In four days last week SHY Aviation fielded some 200 requests for 700 people in the Middle East to charter its planes, which carry up to 12 passengers. Overall, the company estimates, some 30,000 people have tried to evacuate the area by private jets hired through SHY or its rivals.

Fees for such flights have soared to three times their usual rate, driven by increased demand for limited aircraft, added risk insurance and the need to pay crew more for flying through a war zone.

One family of 12, plus their dog, flew from Oman to Turkey on a chartered flight costing more than 143,000 pounds. A source at SHY said the company had organised armoured security vehicles and bodyguards to escort some clients to the airport and at their destination. Because of the closure of many of the region's airports, most of the private jet flights have taken off from Muscat in Oman - about four hours' drive from Dubai - and landed in Istanbul. The suites at Soho House in the Turkish city were fully booked on Friday night.

The British influencer and investor Samuel Leeds chartered a 150,000 pound private jet to return home despite claiming Dubai was perfectly safe. Leeds was criticised on social media after sharing pictures of him and his family on board the Gulfstream jet bound for Heathrow and saying: If you're trapped in Dubai, it's very simple. Drive three hours to Oman, and then when you're in Oman, go down to the Muscat airport, and there's literally planes waiting for you. You can pay about 100,000 pounds, jump on a plane, and it'll take you straight to Heathrow. I don't know why everyone's not doing this.

A source at SHY said: Multiple furry friends were evacuated along with their families. Once they land in safe airspace, many of our clients travel further afield to warmer European destinations - either their own homes or to paid accommodation - to hunker down.

The cost of private charter flights from the Middle East to London, which require long-range aircraft to make the nine-hour journey, is thought to have risen to about 190,000 pounds.

Having the money does not guarantee a getaway, however, said Bernardus Vorster, the SHY chief executive. Only around 30 business jets are available in the area to be able to do repatriation flights. A lot of the other jets are based in Europe and they will not be able to fly in and out of the region, so we are limited to using the jets in the region and moving people four to five hours away, with Muscat to Istanbul the most popular route.

According to WingX data, between 156 and 164 business jets are grounded or parked at airports across the Gulf, as well as in Turkey, Cyprus and Israel.

On average 30 jets a day are coming out of Muscat. With eight people on each jet, you're looking at fewer than 300 people who were actually moved a day, Vorster said. So, my guess is that for the last three to four days maybe only 1,200 people have actually been able to leave Muscat with a private jet.

A drone strike disrupted operations at Dubai international airport on Saturday.
