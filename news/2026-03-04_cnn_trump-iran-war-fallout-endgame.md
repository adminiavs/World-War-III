## Full story

President Trump insists he is willing to wage war on Iran forever. Many around him are already trying to get out. The assault has stoked fears among aides about political consequences: prolonged war, no clear endgame, little public buy-in. Six American lives lost; stock market and gas prices endangering the midterm pitch. Aides still struggle to explain why the nation went to war and what comes next. The war is broadly unpopular in early polling; voters wary of another Middle East entanglement. It has driven a split among MAGA figures (Tucker Carlson, Candace Owens, Megyn Kelly criticising). Messaging has been chaotic: Rubio said the US joined after Israel was set to strike; Trump said he might have forced Israel hand; Leavitt said Trump had a feeling and Netanyahu tipped him that Iranian leaders were gathering Saturday. The administration has offered little clarity on endgame and has refused to rule out ground troops. Best-case among allies: Venezuela-style outcome, wrap up in weeks, declare victory. Iran is far more complicated than Venezuela; political upside is sharply limited.
