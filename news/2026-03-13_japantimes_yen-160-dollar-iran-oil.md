# Yen tests 160 to the dollar and nears levels not seen in 39 years

**Date:** 13 March 2026
**Source:** Japan Times
**URL:** https://www.japantimes.co.jp/business/2026/03/13/markets/yen-weakens-us-dollar/

---

## tl;dr

The yen has weakened sharply against the dollar as oil prices soared after the US and Israel attacked Iran in late February. In afternoon trading in Tokyo (Friday 13 March) the currency broke through 159.44 and reached 159.68. If it breaks 160, intervention could be triggered; if 161.95, it would be the weakest in 39 years. Japan is heavily dependent on Middle East oil; the Iran war and Strait disruption are driving both oil costs and yen weakness.

**Impact on predictions:** Jiang warned Japans oil dependence would create panic. Yen at 39-year lows is the financial expression of that vulnerability.
