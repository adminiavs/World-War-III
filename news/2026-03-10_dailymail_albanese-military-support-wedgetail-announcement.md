# Australia to provide military support in Iran conflict: Albanese makes announcement

**Date:** 10 March 2026 (Tuesday)
**Source:** Daily Mail Australia / MSN
**URL:** https://www.msn.com/en-my/news/other/australia-to-provide-military-support-in-iran-conflict-anthony-albanese-makes-huge-announcement/ar-AA1XRtrF

---

## tl;dr

PM Anthony Albanese confirmed Australia will deploy an E-7A Wedgetail aircraft to the UAE, 85 ADF personnel, and provide advanced medium-range air-to-air missiles to the UAE -- after a formal request from UAE president Mohamed bin Zayed. Initial four-week deployment. Albanese: "first priority of my government is and always will be to keep Australians safe." ~115,000 Australians in the Middle East; ~24,000 in UAE. Government insists deployment is defensive only. Also: five Iranian women's soccer team members granted asylum in Australia after refusing to return home from the Gold Coast.

---

## Full details

**The announcement:** Albanese (Tuesday): "The first priority of my government is and always will be to keep Australians safe. There are around 115,000 Australians in the Middle East, around 24,000 of those in the UAE. Helping Australians means helping the UAE and other nations to defend themselves."

**What is being deployed:**
- E-7A Wedgetail surveillance aircraft (UAE, initial four-week deployment)
- 85 ADF personnel
- Advanced, medium-range air-to-air missiles (to the UAE, in response to a formal UAE request)

**Why:** Formal request made by UAE president Mohamed bin Zayed. The UAE has been forced to shoot down over 1,500 rockets and drones since the war began.

**Albanese:** "The wedgetail and supporting Australian Defence Force personnel will be deployed for an initial four weeks in support of the collective self-defence of Gulf nations. Additionally, in response to a request, my government intends to provide advanced, medium range air to air missiles to the United Arab Emirates. My government has been clear that we're not taking offensive action against Iran, and we've been clear that we are not deploying Australian troops on the ground in Iran."

**Defence Minister Richard Marles:** "What this aircraft will be doing is very similar to what it has been doing in recent times in Poland in respect to the defence of Ukraine. These are defensive weapons, I want to make that clear. The announcements that we are making today, all of this is in the defence of the Gulf countries. I want to be really clear about that."

**Coalition response:** Dan Tehan (frontbencher, ABC Radio National): "Obviously, we want to be doing our bit to keep the region safe and obviously bring this conflict to an end."

**Penny Wong:** Reiterated that Australians stranded in the Middle East should attempt to return home via commercial flights now, while they remain available.

**Side story -- Iranian soccer players:** Five members of the Iranian women's soccer team were granted asylum in Australia after making what appeared to be an "SOS" hand signal on their team bus after their Asian Cup campaign ended Sunday night. Five members escaped from their Gold Coast hotel on Monday to avoid returning home, where they faced possible persecution. Late-night talks between Trump and Albanese also emerged in this period.
