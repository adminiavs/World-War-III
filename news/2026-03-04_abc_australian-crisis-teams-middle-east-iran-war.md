## Full story

The federal government is sending six crisis response teams to the Middle East to help thousands of Australians stranded in the region, while the foreign minister continues to brush off questions about whether the US and Israel's strikes on Iran were legal.

Penny Wong told journalists in Canberra that the six teams would assist diplomats on the ground. She would not disclose where and how they are travelling for security reasons; they are deploying into conflict zones. She said one flight had taken off from Dubai for Sydney with about 200 Australians and there were still "many thousands" in need of help, in the biggest consular operation ever undertaken.

Wong fielded multiple questions on whether the initial strikes on Iran by the US and Israel were in breach of international law. French President Emmanuel Macron said the attacks "were carried out outside the framework of international law, which we cannot approve of." Canadian PM Mark Carney, who had backed the strikes with Albanese over the weekend, said his support was not a "blank cheque." He conceded the strikes were likely not legal: "Prima facie, it appears these actions are inconsistent with international law." He said both the US strikes and the global failure to rein in Iran were "another example of the failure of the international order."

Wong said the legality was "a matter for Israel and the US"; Australia was not party to all the intelligence; this did not begin with the strikes. She said Australia supports action to prevent Iran obtaining a nuclear weapon and to prevent Iran continuing to be a risk to global peace and security, and noted Iran had attacked no less than 10 countries including non-participants and had been engaged in attacks on Australian soil.

Canada's Defence Minister David McGuinty urged both sides to look towards a ceasefire. He said Canada was not involved, not consulted, not providing support; it was for the US and Israel to make their case. Middle powers like Australia and Canada should stick together, look after people in the region, monitor and share information and get civilians out; "the American-Israeli incursion is theirs."
