# Press conference -- Parliament House (Penny Wong, Foreign Minister)

**Date:** 10 March 2026
**Source:** Australian Minister for Foreign Affairs -- foreignminister.gov.au

## tl;dr

Penny Wong press conference at Parliament House, 10 March 2026. Key announcements: E-7A Wedgetail to UAE (four-week deployment), advanced medium-range air-to-air missiles to UAE, 85 ADF personnel. Wong urged all remaining Australians in the Middle East to leave immediately via commercial flights while still available. 115,000+ Australians in the region; ~20,000+ in UAE. Iran has sought to strike 11 countries since the conflict began; 12 targeted. UAE has shot down 1,500+ rockets and drones. 2,600+ Australians returned home. DFAT crisis teams on the ground. Australia is "not taking offensive action against Iran."

---

## Key statements

**On the deployment:**
"The wedgetail and supporting Australian Defence Force personnel will be deployed for an initial four weeks in support of the collective self-defence of Gulf nations. Additionally, in response to a request, my government intends to provide advanced, medium range air to air missiles to the United Arab Emirates."

**On Australia's position:**
"My government has been clear that we're not taking offensive action against Iran, and we've been clear that we are not deploying Australian troops on the ground in Iran. We continue to work on contingencies to keep Australians safe and to help travelers leave the Middle East."

**To Australians in the region:**
"Do so now, do so while commercial flights are still available, however limited."

**Context she provided:**
- Iran has sought to strike 11 countries since the conflict began
- 12 countries have been targeted
- The UAE has been forced to shoot down over 1,500 rockets and drones
- 20,000+ Australians based in UAE; 115,000+ in Middle East overall
- 2,600+ Australians returned home; DFAT crisis response teams on the ground
