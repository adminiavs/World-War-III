## Full story

A market sell-off resumed on both sides of the Atlantic on Thursday as fears mounted that there would be no quick resolution to the conflict in the Middle East. Early gains in European markets were wiped out; Wall Street was also trading sharply lower. The US-Israel war with Iran has driven up the price of oil and gas, triggering fears of a renewed inflation shock and dampening hopes of interest rate cuts. In London the FTSE 100 closed down 1.5% (154 points at 10,414); Germany's DAX and Italy's FTSE MIB both fell 1.6%; France's CAC down 1.5%, Spain's IBEX down 1.4%. In the US the Dow was down 2%, the S&P 500 down 1.3%, the Nasdaq off about 1%. Oil prices continued to rise: Brent up 4% on Thursday at nearly $85; European gas up more than 3%. Brent has jumped 15% in the past five days. Danni Hewson (AJ Bell): "The optimism which helped lift Asian and European markets early in the day evaporated like water droplets on a smouldering stove top. It's becoming harder to see a quick resolution to the conflict in the Middle East and that in turn is forcing markets to look again at their interest rate expectations." Wizz Air has cancelled flights to and from Israel, Dubai, Abu Dhabi and Amman until 15 March; warned of €50m hit to annual profits and higher jet fuel costs; shares fell 11.3%. easyJet -5%, IAG -2%. US Treasury yields on track for fourth consecutive day rise. Markets are likely to improve if tankers can start moving through the Strait of Hormuz, which has been in effect closed by Iran since the weekend; about a fifth of global oil and liquefied natural gas supplies run through the Strait. (Guardian, 5 Mar 2026; Tom Knowles.)
