## Full story

The skies over Israel are exploding once again. Missiles have roared in from Iran as air defence batteries scrambled to intercept. After days of intense American and Israeli strikes on Iranian military targets, Tehran has launched one of its most aggressive retaliatory barrages yet. Sirens erupt across cities; residents rush for shelters and seconds later the sky lights up. Missiles streak towards Tel Aviv and Jerusalem as Iranian forces unleash another wave of attacks. Interceptors launch rapidly trying to break the incoming barrage before it reaches the ground. Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps claims to have launched the Korum Share missile, which can carry about two tons of explosives and has a range of about 3,000 km. The IRGC claims the missile was used to target Ben Gurion airport. Israel has accused Iran of using cluster munitions (air dropped or ground launched weapons that eject numerous smaller submunitions and cause damage over a wider area). The battle in the sky has become a relentless exchange: Iran launches missiles and drones, Israel fires interceptors and responds with air strikes deep inside Iranian territory. Each round pushes the conflict further for civilians across the region. The skies over West Asia are turning into one of the most heavily contested battle zones in the world. Europe report on World is one.
