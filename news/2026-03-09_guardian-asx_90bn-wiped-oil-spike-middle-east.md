# ASX closes after $90bn wiped from sharemarket amid spike in oil prices over Middle East crisis

**Date:** 2026-03-09
**Source:** The Guardian (Australia)
**Author:** Jonathan Barrett
**URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2026/mar/09/asx-australian-shares-plunge-as-spike-in-oil-prices-over-middle-east-conflict-sparks-global-inflation-fears
**Tags:** asx, australia, oil-100, inflation, markets, rba, materials-sector

## tl;dr

Australian shares plunged Monday, wiping about **$90bn** from the ASX. Benchmark S&P/ASX 200 closed down **2.85%** (below 8,600) — single biggest one-day drop since Trump's "liberation day" tariffs last year. Global oil surged past US$100/barrel before the open; selldown linked to disrupted oil supplies and inflation fears. Ten of 11 sectors fell; materials sector down more than 5%; energy was the only sector in green. ASX staged an afternoon recovery after initially falling more than 4%. Market fallout was originally subdued on expectations the war would be short-lived; losses have accelerated as the conflict expanded and hopes of a quick resolution faded. Tony Sycamore (IG Australia): "local markets are mirroring the intense global risk-off mood"; RBA hawkish signals add pressure. If RBA raises rates in March, Australians face higher mortgages alongside petrol and household cost rises. Archival Garcia (Fluent Cargo): fuel costs, war-risk insurance, vessel reroutes and freight rates are all rising. Guardian links to its piece: "Investors are expecting Donald Trump to back down in the war with Iran – but what if he doesn't?"
