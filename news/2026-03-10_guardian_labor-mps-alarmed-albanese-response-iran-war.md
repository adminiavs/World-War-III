# Labor MPs quietly alarmed by Albanese government's response to US-Israel strikes on Iran

**Date:** 2026-03-10  
**Source:** Guardian Australia  
**URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2026/mar/10/albanese-government-response-iran-war-labor-mps  
**Author:** Dan Jervis-Bardy and Sarah Basford Canales  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** Australia  
**Tags:** australia, labor, albanese, wong, marles, internal-dissent, aukus, international-law

## tl;dr

Labor MPs privately alarmed at government's immediate endorsement of legally questionable U.S.-Israel strikes on Iran. Concerns aired at Labor left faction meeting (March 2) after Feb 28 strikes. MPs questioned rushing to endorse attacks likely in breach of international law; statement omitted any reference to international law or rules-based order. Wong, Marles, Albanese maintained it was up to U.S./Israel to explain legality. Government announced March 10 it would send surveillance aircraft and air-to-air missiles to UAE (defensive, not offensive). Labor Against War group circulating motion to NSW/Victoria branches condemning war as "illegal act of aggression" and criticizing Albanese's support as breach of ALP principles. Motion calls decision contrast to Labor's opposition to Iraq War 2003. Greens voice fears Australia could be "fully dragged into another US forever war." Unease extends to Labor right faction members. Labor's grassroots push ahead of July national conference in Adelaide.

## Key details

### Initial government response (Feb 28)
- Statement from Albanese, Wong, Marles hours after strikes began
- "We support the United States acting to prevent Iran from obtaining a nuclear weapon"
- No reference to international law, rules-based order, or explicit acknowledgment Iran was being bombed
- Contrast with June 2025 response when government waited ~24 hours before backing U.S. bombing of Iranian nuclear sites

### Internal Labor concerns
- "Robust" debate at Labor left faction meeting (March 2)
- Wong not present; Pat Conroy answered questions on her behalf
- Details confirmed by six Labor sources (anonymous re confidential discussions)
- MPs raised concerns directly with senior ministers including Wong
- Unease extends to Labor right faction members

### Government position
- Albanese: Australia will not be part of offensive action against Iran
- Australia will not deploy troops to Middle East
- March 10: Sending surveillance aircraft and air-to-air missiles to UAE for defensive purposes

### Labor Against War motion
- Circulating to NSW and Victoria branches
- Condemns war as "illegal act of aggression against a sovereign nation"
- States Albanese's support "breaches fundamental principles and policies" of ALP
- Contrasts with Labor's opposition to Iraq War 2003
- Notes war is "concrete evidence that Aukus locks Australia into support for US military adventurism"
- Targets July national conference in Adelaide

### Expert opinion
- International law experts including UN special rapporteur Ben Saul argue attacks breach UN charter
