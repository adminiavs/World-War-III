## Full story

Aussie borrowers are being warned to brace for two back-to-back interest rate hikes from the Reserve Bank of Australia. The war in the Middle East is expected to push inflation higher, with oil again hitting $100 a barrel. Commonwealth Bank, Westpac, NAB and ANZ all forecast a rate rise at the RBA board meeting on Tuesday followed by another in May, lifting the cash rate back to 4.35 per cent and reversing the three rate cuts of last year.

Inflation was already above the RBA's 2–3% target before the Iran war (headline 3.8%, underlying 3.4%). CBA head of Australian economics Belinda Allen said inflation would lift further as energy and linked goods prices rise; domestic data since February confirmed higher rates needed and the bank added a March hike to its forecast. RBA deputy governor Andrew Hauser had warned about inflation risk from surging oil and Middle East war fallout; he now expects inflation to rise above the RBA's previous 4.2% forecast by June. NAB chief economist Sally Auld said senior RBA officials view the Iranian conflict as an inflationary shock; Westpac's Luci Ellis said the oil effect on headline inflation was "large but temporary" but the board would feel compelled to react. ANZ pre-emptively raised fixed home loan rates by up to 0.25 percentage points. If the RBA hikes in March and May, repayments on a $600,000 mortgage rise by $91 then $182 per month (total $272 across three hikes). Canstar advised mortgage holders to prepare for higher rates and seek lower starting rates.
