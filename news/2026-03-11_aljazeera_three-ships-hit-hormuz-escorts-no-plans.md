# Three Commercial Ships Hit Near Strait of Hormuz

## Source
- Al Jazeera (11 Mar 2026): https://youtu.be/hcDjcG7JYN0

## Key Facts

Three commercial ships hit by unidentified projectiles near Strait of Hormuz in close succession. UK Maritime Trade Operations Center monitoring.

All three attacks "around the same time and around the same place" just off coast of UAE and Oman (Ras al Khaimah area).

Vessels hit:
- One container ship
- One cargo ship  
- One bulk carrier (carries unpacked dry goods like grain or coal)

Cargo vessel caught fire. All crews safe and evacuated. Investigations underway.

## US Claims vs Reality

Trump says US destroyed 16 Iranian vessels that were "planning to lay mines."

CENTCOM posted video showing destruction of vessels but "does not go into any details about whether those mine laying vessels had actually deposited any mines."

No evidence of actual mines in strait.

## Escorts: Trump Promise vs Pentagon Reality

Last week Trump said Navy would "soon begin escorting tankers."

Joint Chiefs Chairman Gen. Dan King revealed "no plans to do so yet":

"If tasked to escort, we'll look at the range of options to set the military conditions to be able to do that. And then, like we always do with every potential mission, come to the secretary and the president with both what are the resources required, what is the command and control required, and what are the risks, and how do we mitigate those risks. So, we're looking at a range of options there. We'll figure out how to solve problems as they come to us."

## Iranian Official Statements

IRGC Navy Commander (written statement): "We announced from the very beginning of these confrontations that any vessel associated with the aggressors of Iran has no right to pass through this Strait of Hormuz. If you have doubts, come closer and try."

Larijani (Supreme National Security Council, posted in five languages): "Strait of Hormuz will be either a strait of peace and prosperity for all or will be a strait of defeat and suffering for war mongers."

Parliament Speaker Qalibaf (mocking Trump escort claims): "An oil tanker crossing Strait of Hormuz escorted by a US ship may be on PlayStation."

## Analysis

Iran warned before war it would use Strait as leverage. Now employing that leverage.

Al Jazeera correspondent: "The longer this carries on, the risk to maritime traffic in the Strait of Hormuz simply continues."

Iran's position: As long as Iran is under attack, it will make sure the strait is blocked and use it as pressure point.

20% of global oil supplies transported through waterway.
