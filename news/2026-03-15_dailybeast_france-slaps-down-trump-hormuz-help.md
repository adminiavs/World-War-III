## Full story

Trump conducted diplomacy via Truth Social on Saturday, asking allies to send warships to the Strait of Hormuz. The strait is blockaded by Iran after US–Israeli attacks; about 20% of world oil is not moving and gas and aviation fuel prices are rising in the US. Trump wrote that many countries "especially those affected by Iran's attempted closure" would send warships with the US to keep the strait open, and hoped China, France, Japan, South Korea, the UK and others would send ships. French Response, the official X account of the French government's foreign office, replied: "No. The [French] aircraft carrier strike group remains in the Eastern Mediterranean. France's posture is unchanged: Defensive. Protective." It added: "Stop the scaremongering." The UK Ministry of Defence has said it is discussing a range of options to ensure security of shipping. Trump had also promised the US would bomb the shoreline and shoot Iranian boats out of the water and get the strait "OPEN, SAFE, and FREE." Defense Secretary Hegseth said on Friday the strait is open for transit but carries high risk of Iranian bombing. Iran has said it will continue to block shipping and drive up oil prices (already above $100/bbl; largest disruption to global oil supplies in history). Trump said the US struck 90+ military targets on Kharg Island; fuel infrastructure reportedly remains intact. Iran is targeting fuel ports in Dubai, Abu Dhabi and the UAE.
