# Is Australia at war with Iran?

**Date:** 14 March 2026
**Source:** The Guardian (Australia)
**URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2026/mar/14/is-australia-at-war-with-iran-ntwnfb

---

## tl;dr

Legal and political analysis of whether Australia is at war with Iran despite Albanese's insistence it is only providing "defensive" support. ANU professor Donald Rothwell: assisting a Gulf state in its self-defence "means Australia would legally become part of an international armed conflict." The government's position -- "not taking offensive action," "not deploying ground troops in Iran" -- is increasingly hard to sustain: Pine Gap provides targeting data; 3 Australians were on the submarine that sank IRIS Dena (killing 87); 85 ADF personnel and a Wedgetail are deployed to the UAE. Greens senator Shoebridge: signed up "by deception and stealth." Albanese says "this is not Iraq," but critics argue the mission creep is following the same pattern.

---

## Key points

**Australia's legal position:** International law professor Donald Rothwell (ANU) told the Guardian that assisting a Gulf state in self-defence against Iran makes Australia legally part of an "international armed conflict," regardless of whether its own actions are labelled offensive or defensive. The distinction Albanese is drawing does not hold in international law.

**What Australia has committed:**
- Joint statement of support on Day 1 (28 Feb) -- Albanese, Marles, Wong
- Pine Gap intelligence and targeting data for US strikes
- 3 Australian crew on US submarine that sank Iranian frigate IRIS Dena (87 dead, 32 rescued)
- 85 ADF personnel deployed to UAE
- E-7A Wedgetail surveillance aircraft to UAE (4-week deployment)
- Advanced medium-range air-to-air missiles supplied to UAE
- 3 embassies/consulates closed (Abu Dhabi, Tel Aviv, Dubai) due to missile strikes

**What the government says:** Albanese: "My government has been clear that we're not taking offensive action against Iran, and we've been clear that we are not deploying Australian troops on the ground in Iran." Marles: "These are defensive weapons."

**What critics say:**
- Greens senator David Shoebridge: Australia "signed up to another US forever war by deception and stealth." "There is no rational Australian interest in sending troops to the Gulf and only American interests are being served."
- Guardian columnist Zoe Daniel (previously): "There's already mission creep, without an exit strategy."
- IPAN: "No matter how deep we have got ourselves into this alliance with the United States, I don't think we should ever get to a point where we cannot say no."

**The political framing:** Albanese: "This is not Iraq and we are not the Howard government." But critics note that in 2003, Labor's Simon Crean correctly called Iraq "illegal, unnecessary and unjust" -- and was politically destroyed for it. Albanese has observed what happened to Crean.

**The bottom line:** Australia is not formally "at war" with Iran. But it is providing intelligence, missiles, surveillance, and personnel that are part of the war effort. Whether that distinction holds legally, morally, or politically is now an open question.
