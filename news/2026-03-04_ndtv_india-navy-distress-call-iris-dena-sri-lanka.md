## Full story

The Indian Navy launched search and rescue operations following a distress signal from the Iranian frigate IRIS Dena, which sank in the Indian Ocean after being struck by a torpedo from a United States submarine.

A distress call from IRIS Dena reached the Maritime Rescue Coordination Centre (MRCC) in Colombo during the early hours of March 4. The frigate was positioned 20 nautical miles west of Galle, within the search and rescue region under Sri Lankan responsibility. Upon receiving the information, the Indian Navy initiated efforts without delay. A long-range maritime patrol aircraft was deployed at 1000 hours on March 4 to support search operations under Sri Lankan leadership. A second aircraft, equipped with air-droppable life rafts, remained on standby. INS Tarangini, a sailing training ship operating in the vicinity, received orders to assist and reached the search area by 1600 hours. INS Ikshak, a survey vessel, sailed from Kochi to reinforce the operation and remains in the area conducting searches for missing personnel as a humanitarian effort.

The sinking occurred on March 4 when a US submarine torpedoed IRIS Dena in international waters, approximately 40 nautical miles off Galle on Sri Lanka's southern coast. The vessel issued its distress call at dawn reporting an explosion but had sunk by the time Sri Lankan rescue ships arrived. The frigate had been returning to Iran after participating in a military exercise at Visakhapatnam. Iran's Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi described the incident as an atrocity at sea; the ship, carrying almost 130 sailors, had been struck without warning in international waters 2,000 miles from Iran's shores; he called IRIS Dena a guest of India's Navy and warned the United States would bitterly regret setting such a precedent. US Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed the action at the Pentagon, describing it as a quiet death. A second Iranian warship, IRIS Bushehr, approached Sri Lankan waters the same day with nearly 300 crew and cadets. Sri Lanka has maintained neutrality and repeatedly called for resolution through discussion. Iran remains a major buyer of Sri Lankan tea. NDTV, 4 Mar 2026.
