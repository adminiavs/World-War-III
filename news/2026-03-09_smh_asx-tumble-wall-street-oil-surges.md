# ASX set to tumble as Wall Street falls; Oil surges higher

**Date:** 2026-03-09  
**Source:** The Sydney Morning Herald  
**Author:** Stan Choe (AP)  
**URL:** https://www.smh.com.au/business/markets/asx-set-to-tumble-as-wall-street-falls-oil-surges-higher-20260309-p5o8ld.html  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** US, Australia, global  
**Tags:** oil, stock-market, stagflation, iran-war, hormuz, asx

## tl;dr

Oil shot to highest since 2023; weak US jobs report (employers cut more jobs than created) and oil above $90 sent stocks lower—Wall Street's worst week since October. S&P 500 -1.3%, Dow -453 (0.9%), Nasdaq -1.6%; ASX futures -156 pts (-1.8%). "Stagflation" fears: weak economy + high inflation = Fed has no good tool for both. Brent +8.5% to $92.69 (briefly >$94); US crude breached $90, +12.2% to $90.90. War expanded to areas critical for oil/gas; Strait of Hormuz (~fifth of world oil) key; UAE and Kuwait reducing production; Iraq holding back (storage full); Saudi shut biggest refinery; Qatar closed world's largest LNG export plant after drone attacks. Trump's strait insurance plan had little market effect. Analysts: $100 oil sustained could be too much for global economy. Trump signalled "unconditional surrender" for Iran, ruling out negotiations. Russell 2000 -2.3%; high fuel-cost firms led losses (Old Dominion, Carnival, Southwest). Treasury yields wavered. Europe down; Hong Kong +1.7%; South Korea volatile (historic plunge then rebound).
