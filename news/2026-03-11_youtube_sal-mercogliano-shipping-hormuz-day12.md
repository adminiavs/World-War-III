## Key Information

### Three Vessels Attacked in 24 Hours
- **OE Majesty** (container ship, laden) — hit by projectile, minor hull damage, 50nm NW of Dubai, crew safe
- **Mori Nari / Mayuree Naree** (Thai-flagged bulk carrier, in ballast) — struck by suspected unmanned surface vessel (USV), severe hull damage port side aft near rudder, engine room fire swept through superstructure. **Three crew members missing, presumed dead.** Crew abandoned ship via freefall lifeboat and life rafts. Rescued by Omani patrol boat.
- **Star Gwenneth** (Chinese-owned bulk carrier, in ballast) — hull damage from unknown projectile, 70nm NW of Dubai, crew safe, vessel afloat. **Chinese-owned vessel being hit undermines assumption China has guaranteed safe passage.**

- **13 confirmed vessel strikes** since war began

- Historical daily average: **138 transits/day**

### US Navy Privately Admits Escorts "Too Dangerous"
- Reuters (Jonathan Saul / gCaptain): Behind closed doors, **US Navy telling shipping industry escorts are too dangerous for now**
- Three shipping industry sources confirm Navy said it is "unable to provide escorts for the time being"
- Directly contradicts Trump's public statements that US "prepared to provide naval escorts whenever needed"
- Aramco CEO: "catastrophic consequences for world oil market" if disruption continues
- Adele Bakawan (European Institute for Middle East/North Africa Studies): "Neither France, the United States, an international coalition, or anybody is in a position to secure the Strait of Hormuz"
- US Navy has ~8 Arleigh Burke destroyers + 3 Littoral Combat Ships with Lincoln battle group — **not enough for escort missions** along full Kuwait-to-Hormuz route
- In 1980s tanker war, US had 600-ship Navy with mix of destroyers, cruisers, frigates, helicopter carriers; no longer has that capacity

### Port of Salalah (Oman) Attacked
- Fuel storage tanks struck by drones/UAS
- Port operations **suspended**
- Dramatic imagery: oil tanks ablaze on hillside, fires in harbor, bulk carrier docked nearby, tug spraying water for heat protection
- Significant: this is a commercial port on the Indian Ocean side (outside the Gulf), extending the threat zone

### CENTCOM Warning on Iranian Ports
- CENTCOM statement warning civilians to avoid port facilities in Iran where naval forces operate
- Claims Iranian naval vessels/military equipment within civilian ports could "strip those locations of protected civilian status under international law"

### Saudi Arabia Pipeline Pivot
- Saudi Aramco ramping up east-to-west pipeline to divert crude from Persian Gulf to Red Sea port of **Yanbu**
- Pipeline max capacity: ~8M bbl/day (Persian Gulf exports ~20M bbl/day total)
- **27 VLCCs sailing for Yanbu** (Windward AI data)
- MSC expanding Dragon and Jade container services from west coast Saudi Arabia
- Bab el-Mandeb strait becomes critical chokepoint for redirected traffic

### Insurance vs. Physical Security
- War risk insurance issue **largely resolved** through commercial means + US government supplements
- **Physical security (attacks) is now the primary barrier** keeping ships locked in place — not insurance

### Global Ripple Effects
- **Coal demand surging**: Chesapeake Bay anchorage full of empty bulkers waiting to load coal at Baltimore as coal replaces LNG in Asian power generation
- **Maersk emergency bunker surcharge**: $200/20ft container, $400/40ft container (Federal Maritime Commission monitoring)
- **60 million barrels of Russian crude** redirected to India after US greenlights Russian imports — Russia "one of the big beneficiaries" of US attack on Iran
- Russian oil filling void of Saudi, Omani, Qatari, Bahraini, Kuwaiti, Iraqi oil — not just Iranian
- Extended loss of Qatar LNG could curtail Asian power generation with knock-on effects across industries
- Global grain trade at future risk due to fertilizer shortages caused by energy price inflation
