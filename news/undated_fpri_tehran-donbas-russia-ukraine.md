## Full story

Analysis (from alternative sources): The U.S.-Israeli campaign against Iran in early 2026 may reshape Ukraine negotiations. Resources and attention diverted to the Middle East could reduce Ukraine's priority; weakening Iran's energy leverage might increase economic pressure on Russia and narrow Moscow's position, potentially enabling more flexible talks. China may adopt a more pragmatic stance on global energy if Western influence in the Gulf strengthens. Russia has relied on Iran as part of its sanctions-era survival strategy; Iran's domestic crisis (protests, thousands killed) and the Western strikes threaten that partnership; Russia is reportedly providing technical and security assistance to Iran. Militarily, Russia's dependence on Iranian supply has diminished: Russia now produces Iranian-type drones domestically as "Geran" (about 90% in Russia).
