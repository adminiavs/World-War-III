# Long War Analysis: Ground Troops Discussed, Ukraine/Pacific Impact

**Sources:** The Spectator, Washington Institute, Invesco, Responsible Statecraft, Mick Ryan  
**Date:** March 1-10, 2026  
**Type:** Analysis compilation  

---

## Ground troops being discussed

**The Spectator (Mar 7):**
"Behind the scenes, President Donald Trump has discussed the possibility of sending a limited number of US ground troops into Iran to secure strategic sites such as nuclear facilities and key infrastructure after the fighting."

**Washington Institute (Mar 6):**
Tasks like "removing enriched uranium or verifying that other sites do not pose a proliferation risk likely cannot be done through strikes alone."

## US military buildup (Responsible Statecraft)

- US troops in region: ~34,000 to ~50,000 since Oct 2023
- Three carrier strike groups deployed
- Six B-2 bombers (30% of fleet) at Diego Garcia
- ~13,500 US forces in Kuwait

## Four stated US objectives (Washington Institute)

1. Destroying Iran's navy
2. Destroying Iran's missile capabilities  
3. Preventing nuclear weapons
4. Preventing proxy support

"Regime change may not be explicit goal... but arguably the one on which clearest progress has been made."

## Impact on Ukraine (Mick Ryan)

- Munitions/interceptors diverted to Middle East
- Intelligence assets prioritized for Iran
- Diplomatic bandwidth consumed
- Russia may exploit gaps
- Ukraine "may fall to bottom of American priorities even faster than forecast"

## Impact on Pacific (Mick Ryan)

- Two carriers diverted from Indo-Pacific
- "Signals to every Pacific ally that American commitments remain hostage to Middle Eastern contingencies"
- China benefiting without direct involvement

## Saudi back-channel with Iran

The Spectator: "Saudi Arabia has opened urgent back-channel talks with Iran in an effort to contain the war."

## Key quote

Washington Institute: "The conundrum is that while US and Israel are clearly winning in military terms, if they stopped fighting today they would be judged to have lost."
