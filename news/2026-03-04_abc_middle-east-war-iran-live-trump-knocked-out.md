## Full story

Benjamin Netanyahu has said the US and Israel will continue to strike Iran "with force" as the war in the Middle East continues.

The Israeli Prime Minister spoke after US President Donald Trump told Tehran's remaining power base that it was "too late" to negotiate.

Meanwhile, Israeli strikes are continuing in Lebanon and the US embassy in Saudi Arabia has warned of a drone and missile threat from Iran.

**Today so far (3h ago, Tue 3 Mar 2026 8:25pm)**

If you're just tuning in, here's a quick rundown of what's happened this morning:

* US President **Donald Trump** gave an optimistic assessment that "just about everything" in Iran's navy and air force has been "knocked out" since the US-Israeli operation began on Saturday
* **Israeli** Prime Minister **Benjamin Netanyahu** says war against **Iran** may take **"some time", but not years**, as it continues to attack "with force"
* Israel **continues to target areas in Iran**, including airports, according to Iranian media
* It is also **striking places in Lebanon it says are Hezbollah infrastructure**, with the group promising to fight and claiming to have downed a drone
* **Iran is firing back**, with Israel saying strikes have hit an area in the country's centre
* Trump said the **US Navy would begin escorting tankers through the Strait of Hormuz if necessary,** to protect them from Iranian attacks
* The **death toll in Iran has reached 787, according to the local Red Crescent.** In **Lebanon, it is 40**, according to the Ministry of Health. In **Israel it has reached at least 11**, according to officials. **Eight have been killed in the Gulf** and **six US soldiers have died**

**Trump administration holds classified briefings on Iran (15m ago)**

US Congress was briefed on the situation in Iran today.

The briefings were classified, so we don't have much information on what was said.

**Israel launches fresh wave of strikes on Iran (29m ago)**

The Israeli military says it has launched another broad wave of strikes on Iran.

"The Air Force has now begun a wave of extensive strikes against launch sites, defense systems, and additional infrastructure of the Iranian terror regime," the Israeli Air Force said in a social media post.

**Pentagon identifies four of the six soldiers killed (Key Event, 39m ago)**

The **Pentagon** has identified four of the six US military personnel that were killed in an Iranian drone strike on Sunday.

The soldiers are 35-year-old Captain **Cody Khork**, 42-year-old Sergeant 1st Class **Noah Tietjens**, 39-year-old Sergeant 1st Class **Nicole Amor**, and 20-year-old Sergeant **Declan Coady**.

All four soldiers were a part of the 103rd Sustainment Command which is an Army Reserve unit based out of Iowa.

The Pentagon has not yet been able to identify the other two soldiers that were killed.

**Mojtaba Khamenei reportedly appointed as Iran's new supreme leader (Key Event, 49m ago)**

There are reports coming out of Iran that **Mojtaba Khamenei** has been appointed as the new leader of the **Islamic Republic**.

Iran International, citing information received from the **Assembly of Experts**, said the former supreme leader **Ali Khamenei's son,** Mojtaba, has been appointed.

It has been reported the decision has been made hurriedly, under pressure from Iran's **Revolutionary Guard**.

**Trump favours insider for supreme leader successor (58m ago)**

If the exiled son of Iran's former shah, **Reza Pahlavi,** was hoping for an endorsement from US President **Donald Trump** for his return to Iran he'll be disappointed.

The president described the exiled Iranian as a "very nice person" and acknowledged in comments at the White House that "some people like him".

But Trump said he would favour someone in Iran.

> "Somebody that's there, that's currently popular, if there is such a person."

The president suggested the US may soon run out of preferred candidates because of the success of its attacks on the Iranian leadership.

> "Most of the people we had in mind are dead … pretty soon we're not going to know anybody."

There was also a startling moment when the US president appeared to articulate his worst fears.

> "I guess the worst case would be we do this, and then somebody takes over who's as bad as the previous person, right?"

The comments came as a stark contrast to the bravado otherwise on display from the White House about its **Operation Epic Fury**.

Donald Trump was sitting next to the visiting German Chancellor **Friedrich Merz,** who had said the two men would talk about "the day after".

It seems much about what comes next is still a very open question.

**No reason for Australians to panic-buy petrol, Farrell says (1h ago)**

Trade Minister **Don Farrell** says we are living in an age of unpredictability when asked whether there has been any indication from the US on an end to the war in the Middle East.

The shutdown of the **Strait of Hormuz** has sparked concerns over soaring oil prices and supply chain disruption.

But Farrell says Australians shouldn't be worried.

> "We have the largest reserves of airline fuels, petrol and diesel, that we have had in the last 15 years," he told **ABC News Breakfast.**
>
> "There is absolutely no reason at all for people to be panic-buying."

**First flight from Dubai to Sydney departs (Key Event, 1h ago)**

A flight from Dubai to Sydney has departed the United Arab Emirates, according to flight tracking data.

It's the first flight to depart the UAE for Australia since airspace was closed and travel was halted due to the conflict in the Middle East.

Foreign Minister **Penny Wong** told **ABC's AM** this morning the flight would only take off if the circumstances were safe.

**More than 24,000 Australians in Middle East, Wong describes challenge as 'consular crisis' (1h ago)**

Foreign Minister **Penny Wong** has just dialled into ABC's AM, where she has been asked about the latest advice to Australians in the Middle East.

> "Obviously, this is a perilous and unpredictable time in the Middle East, and we are very conscious of how challenging, how distressing this is for Australians who are in the Middle East and for friends and family overseas," she says.

She says most of the Australians in the region are in the UAE.

"This is a consular crisis that dwarfs … any that Australia has had to deal with in terms of the numbers of people."

**Witness footage of attack on US consulate in Dubai (1h ago)**

Earlier we reported an **Iranian drone** had slammed into a parking lot outside the **US consulate in Dubai**, sparking a small fire.

Although the pace of Iranian missile and drone strikes has slowed, Tuesday's near-miss shows that Iran is still able to get munitions past American interceptors.

US Secretary of State **Marco Rubio** told reporters that all of the consulate personnel in Dubai had been accounted for.

**Two Kuwait army members killed in Iranian attacks (1h ago)**

Two **Kuwaiti army** personnel were killed on Monday, local time, as a result of Iran's attacks against the country, the government media office has said.

Six US military personnel were killed in Kuwait in attacks from Iran on Sunday, local time.

**Hezbollah says it has targeted Israeli bases and tanks (1h ago)**

Lebanese militant group **Hezbollah** said it has targeted several **Israeli military bases** and tanks in response to Israeli strikes on the group's strongholds in Lebanon.

In separate statements, Hezbollah on Tuesday claimed responsibility for 11 attacks on Israel, saying it targeted at least five Israeli tanks, three of them in Lebanese territory using guided missiles and "appropriate weapons".

The group also said it used attack drones and rocket salvos to target several bases in **northern Israel** and the **Golan Heights**.

Additionally, it claimed to have downed an Israeli drone over the southern city of **Nabatiyeh**.

**Blasts heard over Jerusalem, Tel Aviv (2h ago)**

Israel's **Home Front Command** had issued an advance warning to take shelter from a wave of incoming missiles.

Blasts have now been heard over **Jerusalem,** and there are reports of munitions debris falling in **Tel Aviv.**

**Iraq downs drone near Baghdad Airport (2h ago)**

Iraq has downed a drone in the vicinity of **Baghdad Airport,** according to the country's state news agency.

No deaths or material losses were reported.

**The Islamic Resistance in Iraq** says it has carried out 27 attacks against "enemy" bases in the country since dawn on Tuesday.

Separately, Qatar's defence ministry says Iran launched two missiles toward its territory, with one targeting the US-run Al Udeid air base.

**Analysis: Washington's ever-evolving rationale for war (2h ago)**

Donald Trump's claims that the US essentially forced Israel to take action on Iran, because he assessed Iran was about to strike Israel, directly contradicted his own **Secretary of State.**

**Marco Rubio's** suggestion just a day ago that Israel forced America's hand because it was about to attack Iran, thus putting US interests at risk, raised a lot of eyebrows.

That scenario didn't exactly portray the world's superpower in a flattering light and would only have fuelled criticisms that Washington was taking its marching orders from **Israeli leader Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu.**

Donald Trump's comments were an attempt to counter that narrative.

> "If anything, I might have forced Israel's hand," the US President said.

Senior Trump administration officials have now rushed to back them up.

One official, speaking on condition of anonymity, said the negotiations, led by **Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner**, over Iran's nuclear program were not going well.

"They were hiding things," the official said.

> "It all smelled fishy."

**Marco Rubio** has now attempted to walk back his comments, suggesting they had been taking out of context, but the trouble is it all adds to the ever-growing sense that the administration hasn't quite got its story straight as to why it took action when it did.

**Iran's Assembly of Experts building flattened by air strikes (Key Event, 2h ago)**

The building housing **Iran's Assembly of Experts** was flattened by an air attack in the city of **Qom,** south of Tehran, Iranian news agencies said.

The Assembly of Experts is tasked with choosing a leader to replace supreme leader Ayatollah **Ali Khamenei.**

Iran's state news agencies reported the building was destroyed in a strike on Tuesday, March 3, local time.

US President **Donald Trump** said senior Iranian officials had died on Tuesday, without giving details.

It was not immediately clear if anyone died in the attack.

**Nuclear watchdog says it had no evidence Iran was building bomb but there was cause for concern (Key Event, 3h ago)**

The International Atomic Energy Agency had **no evidence of Iran building a nuclear bomb,** the agency's director-general, **Rafael Mariano Grossi,** says.

But he added that it was **cause for serious concern** that Iran had stockpiled near-weapons-grade enriched uranium and refused to grant IAEA inspectors access.

Grossi said unless Iran assisted the IAEA in resolving outstanding problems, the agency would not be in a position to provide assurance that the country's nuclear program was peaceful.
