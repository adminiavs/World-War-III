# Iran has 'betrayed us' but all sides must de-escalate, says Qatar PM

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Source:** Sky News (UK)  
**URL:** https://news.sky.com/story/iran-has-betrayed-us-but-all-sides-must-de-escalate-says-qatar-pm-13517019  
**Author:** Yalda Hakim (Lead world news presenter)  
**Content type:** news (exclusive interview)  
**Region/country:** Qatar, Gulf  
**Tags:** qatar, gulf-states, de-escalation, hormuz, diplomacy, civilian-infrastructure, gas-supply, betrayal, iran-gulf-relations

## tl;dr

Sky News exclusive interview with Qatar PM Sheikh Mohammed bin Abdulrahman Al Thani (Sun 8 Mar 2026). Qatar feels "a big sense of betrayal" -- Iran attacked Gulf countries just one hour after the war started, despite Qatar's neutrality and its long-standing diplomatic relationship with Tehran. PM: Iran attacks on Gulf states are "a dangerous miscalculation" and their justifications are "completely rejected." 25% of Iranian strikes are targeting civilian facilities (airports, water utilities, gas infrastructure). PM calls for de-escalation from all sides including the US; insists diplomacy is "the only viable path." Qatar supplies roughly 20% of the world's gas and is one of the world's largest fertiliser producers -- disruption has global consequences. "They are our neighbours -- it's our destiny." PM's anger was striking to interviewer given Qatar's historically neutral/mediating role with Tehran. Qatar diplomatic channel to Iran now visibly strained.

## Key quotes

- "It is a big sense of betrayal. Just an hour after the start of the war, Qatar and other Gulf countries have been attacked. We made clear that we were not going to take part in any wars against our neighbours."
- "All the attacks on the Gulf countries -- we never expected this from our neighbour."
- "We have always tried to preserve a good relationship with Iran, but the justifications and pretexts they are using are completely rejected."
- "The miscalculation by the Iranians to attack Gulf countries has destroyed everything."
- "Twenty-five percent of the attacks are targeting civilian facilities. What has this got to do with the war? What do they want to achieve?"
- "We continue to seek de-escalation. They are our neighbours -- it's our destiny."
