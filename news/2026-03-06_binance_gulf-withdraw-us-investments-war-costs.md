## Full story

Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait and Qatar are discussing pulling back from U.S. and other investments as the toll from the regional war with Iran mounts (Financial Times, cited by Kobeissi Letter and others). Factors: mounting budget strain from falling revenue in energy, shipping and tourism; Iran has launched hundreds of drones at the UAE alone; QatarEnergy halted production after drone attacks; FDI in the region faces uncertainty. Gulf officials also complained they were not given adequate notice of the initial U.S.-Israeli attack and that the U.S. ignored warnings about regional consequences; one said interceptor stockpiles were rapidly depleting. UAE is separately considering freezing Iranian assets (WSJ/CNBC). Binance Square post itself could not be retrieved (JS/verification). Over $2 trillion in U.S. investments may be at risk.
