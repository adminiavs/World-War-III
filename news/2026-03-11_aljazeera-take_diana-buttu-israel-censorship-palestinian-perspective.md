## Israeli Public Support for War

- **81% to 93%** of Jewish Israeli public supports the war
- Buttu: "I've never seen people yearning for a war as much as these statistics are showing us"
- Polls show Israelis say they can **"withstand war for about a month"**
- Palestinian citizens of Israel (2 million people) **oppose the war** but their views are not covered

## Israel's Censorship Apparatus

### Formal Censorship Office
- Israel's press office is literally called the **"censorship office"**
- Foreign journalists must submit writings/reporting to government office for review
- Government can grant or deny press passes
- Press passes can be revoked if Israel doesn't like your reporting
- Some journalists have been **deported**

### Current Restrictions
- Not allowed to film strike sites
- Cannot show "sensitive" locations (but "sensitive" is undefined)
- June 2024: Israel "went after journalists" who tried to cover where Iranian missiles hit
- New legislation to **bar media outlets** including Al Jazeera

### No Foreign Journalists in Gaza
- "There are no foreign journalists that are allowed in Gaza even to this current day"
- Relying entirely on Palestinian journalists "who have been doing the work beyond all others through all this and suffering genocide"

### CNN Example
- CNN journalist said live on air: **"The Israeli government doesn't want us to show that"** (regarding an interceptor)
- CNN runs on-screen graphic: "CNN operates in Iran only with government permission"
- Buttu: Same applies to Israel — "in order to be a journalist covering Israel, you actually have to get permission by the Israeli authorities"
- Other networks (Al Jazeera, Arabic-language networks) have been showing what CNN wouldn't
- "They take the censorship rules and rather than try to push them... they somehow capitulate"

## Military Bases Throughout Israel

Buttu describes Haifa:
> "When looking at Israel, you have to just recognize that it is one big military base."

From her house in Haifa:
- Military base on one slope of the hill
- Another military base at the bottom of the hill
- Looking out window toward sea: another military base on the left
- Looking from balcony to the right: another military base
- "The place is entirely surrounded by military bases"

**Israeli equivalent of Pentagon:**
- Located in **heart of Tel Aviv** — commercial and residential district
- "That is literally located in the heart of Tel Aviv"
- Netanyahu went to its rooftop on Day 1 to proclaim he'd "yearned for this for 40 years"

This raises questions about "sensitive" designations:
> "Imagine if a field is hit. Is it that they've hit a sensitive area? How is it defined? Because so much of the country is just surrounded by military bases."

## Damage Assessment

### June 2024 War Damage
- Government estimate: **$3 billion** in damage
- Buttu: "Not at all accurate" — doesn't account for all damage to buildings, infrastructure, homes, cars
- In Haifa alone, places hit in June "still have not been repaired"
- People "were repairing it themselves"
- Some businesses "still not open"

### Current Situation
- Constant phone alerts for incoming missiles
- New warnings: **"We can't really predict all of the missiles that are going to come in"**

## Palestinian Citizens of Israel

### Population and Status
- 2 million Palestinian citizens of Israel
- Treated as a "fifth column"
- Israeli ministers have said "the only reason they remain is because David Ben-Gurion didn't finish the job in 1948" (the ethnic cleansing)

### Shelter Disparity
- **11,350 public shelters** throughout Israel
- Only **37** in Arab areas
- Palestinians sometimes **barred from entering public shelters** in Jewish areas
- Spanish journalist Laura de Chiclana reported from Arab quarter of Haifa that residents had to either pay "tens of thousands of dollars" for private shelters or try to go to Jewish areas — where they weren't being allowed in

### Ignored Perspective
> "When you look at the ways in which people are reporting on this war, there hasn't really been a lot of input from Palestinians who are Palestinians in Israel, who are some of the people who are perhaps going to be the most affected by this."

> "Very few people are asking this community, what is your day-to-day like and what does the future mean for you?"
