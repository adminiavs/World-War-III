## Key Points

### Strait of Hormuz
- **Effectively closed** — 20% of world's oil and natural gas normally passes through
- Shipping "ground to a halt"
- ~1/3 of world's fertilizer supply also transits Hormuz

### United States
- **Gas prices up 14%** in 9 days — ~$3.40/gallon
- **US economy lost ~92,000 jobs** in Q4 2025
- Fed was considering rate cuts but may push back due to inflation uncertainty
- **Midterm elections** on horizon — "fate of Congress may be decided by health of economy"
- Trump: oil price rise is "a very small price to pay for safety and peace"
- Rebecca Christie: "Quite ironic that he has out of his own discretion started a war that will make affordability worse, not better"

### Europe
- Imports virtually all fossil fuels — vulnerable to sudden price shifts
- Already suffered 2022 energy crisis from Russia's Ukraine war
- **Putin threatening to cut supply:** "Perhaps it would be more advantageous for us to stop supplying the European market right now, move to the emerging markets"
- ECB may turn hawkish; rate hikes possible
- Heading into summer = some leeway on heating
- Incomplete progress on energy security since 2022

### Asia — "Greatest impact"
- Takes **86% of crude oil and LNG** that passes through Hormuz
- **China:** 40% of oil imports from Gulf; ordered refineries to stop diesel/petrol exports; has stockpiles but "taking no chances"
- **South Korea:** Capped domestic fuel prices for first time in ~30 years
- **India:** Secured 30-day US waiver to import Russian oil; building reserves
- **Taiwan:** Only ~15 days of strategic LNG reserves (from Qatar) — "red line"
- **Japan:** May hike rates sooner than expected; huge oil dependence; risk of JGB yield spiral
- **Southeast Asia, Thailand:** "Terrible"
- **Malaysia:** Exception (has own reserves)

### Stagflation Risk
- **Stagflation = high inflation + slow growth** — could happen on global scale
- Freya Beamish: "The buffers between the US economy and recession have now worn much thinner. Any kind of shock at this stage can push us towards that recession."
- If central banks hike rates in response to cost-push inflation, "could really precipitate a move towards recession"

### China
- Growth target revised to **4.5-5%** — may have been last-minute change due to crisis
- Export data strong (22% increase) but external demand now uncertain
- "Inflation is not the issue for China... the impact on external growth is very concerning"

### Recovery timeline
- Even if war ends now, fuel prices won't return to January lows quickly
- Gulf states have cut production as storage fills; "takes time to bring output back"
- Risk of renewed conflict increases shipping costs (risk premium)
