# IDF strikes IRGC aerospace headquarters linked to satellite, missile program

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Source:** The Jerusalem Post  
**Author:** Yonah Jeremy Bob  
**URL:** https://www.jpost.com/defense-and-tech/article-889267  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** Israel, Iran  
**Tags:** idf, irgc, aerospace, satellite, khayyam, icbm, missile-defense

## tl;dr

IDF attacked Iran's Aerospace Headquarters (IRGC) on 8 March, used for launching satellites and potentially dual-use for ICBM/nuclear delivery. Site linked to 2022 Khayyam satellite launch (Russian Soyuz, Baikonur); Israeli officials worried Khayyam and Russia-Iran space cooperation could boost ICBM capability and improve Tehran's monitoring of Israeli/regional targets, and reduce Israeli intelligence penetration. Washington Post (2022) reported Russia would provide Iran advanced satellite for tracking Middle East military targets. Dec 2025 Tehran launched three domestic satellites from Russian site as Netanyahu headed to meet Trump. Post–12 Day War (June) Iran's nuclear program "in shambles" so satellite significance was open question; threats remain: ICBM progress if other nuclear work advances, conventional ballistic threat to US/Europe, surveillance vs Israeli satellite advantage. Israel has achieved surprise against Iran in current war; attack may remove or delay potential ICBM. Unclear why site not hit earlier in war.
