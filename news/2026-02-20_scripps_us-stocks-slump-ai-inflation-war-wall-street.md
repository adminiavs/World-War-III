**Source:** Scripps News

## tl;dr

US stocks sank Friday (Feb 20, 2026): S&P 500 -0.4%, Dow -521 pts (-1.1%), Nasdaq -0.9%; second losing month in last 10. Drivers: companies seen as AI losers sold off; wholesale inflation 2.9% (vs 1.6% expected), complicating Fed rate cuts; oil up 2.8% (US crude $67.02) on US–Iran tensions. Block (Jack Dorsey) cutting workforce by nearly half (4,000+ of 10,000+) citing AI tools; stock jumped 16.8%. Salesforce, Apollo, Blue Owl, Nvidia fell on AI disruption fears. Netflix +13.8% after walking away from Warner bid. US military has gathered massive fleet in Middle East; conflict could disrupt oil flow. (AP via Scripps.)

## Full story

U.S. stocks sank Friday as Wall Street kept punishing companies that could become losers in the AI revolution. A surprisingly discouraging update on inflation (wholesale 2.9% vs 1.6% expected) also hurt the market, while oil prices climbed on worries about tensions between the United States and Iran. The S&P 500 fell 0.4%, Dow dropped 521 points (1.1%), Nasdaq sank 0.9%; just its second losing month in the last 10. Block (Cash App, Square) announced it is cutting its workforce by nearly half after Chair Jack Dorsey said "intelligence tools have changed what it means to build and run a company"; stock jumped 16.8%. Fears about AI disruption hit Salesforce, Apollo Global Management, Blue Owl, Nvidia. Oil rose 2.8% (benchmark US crude $67.02); the U.S. military has gathered a massive fleet in the Middle East and a conflict could disrupt the global flow of oil. (Scripps News, AP; Feb 20, 2026.)
