# Tehran: Toxic Air and Acid Rain from Oil Depot Strikes

## Source
- Drop Site News (11 Mar 2026)

## First-Person Accounts

Saturday March 7 oil depot strikes hit Shahran, Aghdasieh, Shahr-e-Ray (Tehran), and Karaj. Massive fires raged through night.

Saghar (24, northeastern Tehran near Aghdasieh depot): House shook worse than earthquake. Windows shattered. Mother thrown by shockwave. Nine hours later, apartment coated in soot. White refrigerator entirely black. "Our fingernails were caked in chemical grime, and our lungs were burning just from breathing inside our own living room."

Sina (42, central Tehran): "When I woke up the house was so dark I assumed it was heavily overcast." Smoke had blackened entire sky. Brief rain turned everything greasy and black. Two days later chest still heavy, struggling to breathe.

Sara (36, Ekbatan): Five-minute walk left breathing "incredibly heavy." Hours later hands inflamed with hives. "Despite the blue sky, it felt like acid had been poured down our throats."

## Official Statements

Red Crescent warning: Explosions spread toxic hydrocarbon compounds and sulfur and nitrogen oxides. Any precipitation causes acid rain capable of chemical burns and lung damage.

WHO chief Tedros: Damage to petroleum facilities risks contaminating food, water and air. Rain laden with oil reported falling in parts of country.

Iran Department of Environment declared strikes violation of human rights citing Geneva Conventions.

## Mass Displacement

Tens of thousands fled Tehran to Ramsar and Mazandaran on Caspian coast. Nine million Tehran area residents affected.
