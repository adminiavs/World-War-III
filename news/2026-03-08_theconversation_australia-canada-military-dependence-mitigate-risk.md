# Australia can't easily reduce its military dependence on the US, but with Canada, we can mitigate risk

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Source:** The Conversation (Australia)

**Author:** John Blaxland (Professor, Strategic and Defence Studies Centre, Australian National University)  
**Content type:** analysis  
**Region/country:** Australia, Canada  
**Tags:** australia, canada, carney, middle-powers, us-alliance, aukus, pine-gap, strategic-cousins, dependence

## tl;dr

Carney warned the Australian parliament the post-war global order is "breaking down" and middle powers risk subordination due to integration with great powers. Blaxland compares Australia and Canada as "strategic cousins": both Five Eyes, UN founders, close US allies; Australia has "fear of abandonment," Canada "fear of entrapment" and sovereignty (long border, Trump's "51st state" humiliation, tariffs). Australia cannot easily replace the US: boutique defence force relies on US hardware, software, interoperability; AUKUS, Pine Gap, submarine rotation at Stirling; alternative kit from South Korea, Japan, Sweden, Germany are all US allies; full independence could cost 4-5% of GDP or more. US values Australia for Indo-Pacific deterrence; weaponising the relationship would harm US interests. Mitigation: work more closely with neighbours (Pacific Maritime Security Program, Pacific Policing Initiative, Indonesia); proposed "regional maritime cooperation forum" with NZ, Singapore, Indonesia, Malaysia. Carney-Albanese joint statement outlines expanding cooperation with Canada (critical minerals, defence, institutions). Australia must manage US alliance while bolstering other arrangements; working with Canada helps be a more confident, self-reliant regional leader.
