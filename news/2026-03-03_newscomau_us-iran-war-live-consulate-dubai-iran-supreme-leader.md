## Full story

Fire has engulfed a US consulate after a drone strike, while Iran confirms it has elected its new Supreme Leader. Follow live.

Welcome to our live coverage of the conflict between the US and Israel and Iran.

A drone attack has caused a fire near the US consulate in Dubai, a day after the US embassy in Riyadh was hit by an Iranian strike.

"The last update I had, with seconds before getting before these cameras, was that a drone unfortunately struck a parking lot adjacent to the Chancery building, and then set off a fire in that place," US Secretary of State Marco Rubio told reporters this morning.

It comes as Iran elects its new Supreme Leader, who will replace the slain Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.

Earlier, it emerged the Israeli Air Force "flattened" the building where Tehran's senior clerics had gathered to select the new leader. The Assembly of Experts, a body made up of 88 members, were allegedly together in the city of Qom when an air strike hit the building, just as they were counting the votes to appoint Khamenei's successor.

Donald Trump told reporters at a press conference earlier that "most of the people" his administration had in mind to replace Khamenei had now been taken out.

"Well, most of the people we had in mind are dead," the President said.

"So, you know, we had some in mind from that group that is … dead, and now we have another group. They may be dead also, based on reports."

Mr Trump earlier said it is now "too late" for Iran to negotiate with Washington.

**Iran elects new Supreme Leader (9:38 PM Mar 03, 2026)**

The son of Iran's Ayatollah Ali Khamenei was selected to replace him as supreme leader, according to a report in Iranian opposition media.

Mojtaba Khamenei was tapped to assume control of the country by Iran's Assembly of experts, Iranian International reported.

The report was widely picked up by Israeli media, but has not been confirmed by Iranian state mouthpieces.

Mojtaba was at first reported to be among the 40 top lieutenants killed during the Saturday strike that took out Iran's highest-ranking cleric.

**Extraordinary footage after US consulate struck (7:56 PM Mar 03, 2026)**

A drone attack caused a fire near the US consulate in Dubai on Tuesday, a government statement said, a day after the US embassy in Riyadh was hit by an Iranian strike.

"Dubai authorities have confirmed that a fire resulting from a drone-related incident near the US Consulate has been successfully contained," the official Dubai Media Office posted on X.

Residents told AFP they heard a bang, and one said she then saw the blaze. Police cordoned off roads and shooed away passersby trying to see the damage.

US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said all personnel at the embassy have been accounted for, and no injuries have been reported.

**Four of the six US soldiers killed identified**

The US Pentagon has identified four of the six US soldiers killed in an Iranian drone strike on Sunday.

Captain Cody Khork, 35; Sergeant 1st Class Noah Tietjens, 42; Sergeant 1st Class Nicole Amor, 39; and Sergeant Declan Coady, 20 were at a temporary tactical operations centre at Kuwait's Port of Shuaiba when it was hit. A source familiar with the situation told CNN there was no siren or warning ahead of the attack to give the troops time to take cover in a bunker.

The identities of the two other service members killed have not been release.

**Israel launches 'broad wave' of strikes**

Israel's military said it has launched a "broad wave of strikes" on targets in Iran, after the Islamic republic launched three separate missile barrages at Israeli territory in recent hours.

The military said the targets of its latest strikes include "launch sites, aerial defence systems, and additional infrastructure".

**Syria reinforces borders with Lebanon, Iraq**

As the conflict between the US, Israel and Iran spreads further afield, Syria has reinforced its borders with Lebanon and Iraq.

The nation's defence ministry said it had increased the number of troops at both borders to "protect and secure them" and keep the war from spilling over into its territory.

"The deployed units belong to the Border Guard forces and reconnaissance battalions, tasked with monitoring border activity and combating smuggling," the Syrian army said.

**Canada supports US action 'with regret'**

Canada's Prime Minister Mark Carney has voiced his support for the US-Israeli attacks on Iran, albeit "with regret, because the current conflict is another example of the failure of the international order".

Mr Carney, who is in Sydney at the moment as part of an official visit to Australia, urged "all parties" in the conflict to respect the international rules of engagement.

"Canada reaffirms that international law binds all belligerents," Mr Carney told reporters.

"We implore all parties, including the United States and Israel, to respect the rules of international engagement.

**First flight from UAE to Aus takes off (10:33 PM Mar 03, 2026)**

An Emirates flight carrying stranded Australians out of the Middle East has taken off from Dubai International Airport.

The plane from Dubai to Sydney is the first to Australia since the UAE halted air travel and closed much of its airspace in the wake of the US-Israeli attack on Iran. Flight EK414 is scheduled to arrive in Sydney late tonight.

Foreign Minister Penny Wong told ABC Radio the flight would not take off unless airspace over the Middle East was deemed safe.

The amount of Australians in the Middle East – an estimated 115,000, most of them concentrated in the UAE – "is a consular crisis that dwarfs … any that Australia has had to deal with in terms of the numbers of people", Ms Wong said.

**Stranded Aussies warned of $77k fine**

Australians stranded in the United Arab Emirates (UAE) have been warned about posting unverified conflict footage could lead to $77,000 in fines and even imprisonment, as the region continues to face a barrage of missiles coming from Iran.

The UAE Public Prosecution released a statement warning anyone who post or repost footage from "unknown sources" will face legal accountability including Dh200,000 ($77,700) fine and imprisonment of not less than one year.

"Information is a responsibility, and spreading rumours is a crime," the UAE Public Prosecution shared in a statement on X on March 2.

**Hezbollah targets Israeli bases, tanks**

Lebanese militant group Hezbollah has targeted several Israeli military bases and tanks in response to Israeli strikes on the group's strongholds in Lebanon, including the south Beirut suburbs.

Israel continues to carry out successive air raids, particularly on Beirut's southern suburbs and the south of the country, after issuing evacuation warnings to residents, while Lebanese authorities on Tuesday recorded the displacement of more than 58,000 people from areas hit by the strikes.

Israel announced on Tuesday morning local time it had begun a new round of "simultaneous strikes in Tehran and Beirut".

It announced later that day that it hit "approximately 60" targets "belonging to the Hezbollah and Hamas terrorist organisations".

**Commander of Iran's Quds Force killed**

The commander of Iran's Quds Force – one of the branches of its Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps – has been killed in a strike on Tehran, the Israel Defense Forces (IDF) has said.

"Fighter jets struck in the heart of Tehran, targeting Daoud Ali Zadeh, a senior official in the Quds Force and commander of the Lebanon Corps," IDF spokesperson Brigadier General Effie Defrin said.

"He attempted to attack Israel from Lebanese territory, and we struck and eliminated him on Iranian soil."

Mr Zadeh was "the highest-ranking Iranian commander responsible for Lebanon … guiding Hezbollah and other proxies' firepower capabilities".
