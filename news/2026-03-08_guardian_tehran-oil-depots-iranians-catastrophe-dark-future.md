# Dark like our future: Iranians describe scenes of catastrophe after Tehran oil depots bombed

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Source:** The Guardian (World)

**Author:** Deepa Parent  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** Iran, Tehran  
**Tags:** tehran, oil-depots, civilian-impact, toxic-rain, humanitarian, eyewitness

## tl;dr

Tehran residents describe catastrophe after US-Israeli strikes on four oil depots and a petroleum site. Thick black smoke, soot, toxic air; six killed, 20 wounded at one site. Red Crescent warned of toxic acid rain; people advised to stay indoors and wear masks. Activist Negin: apocalyptic, shortness of breath, burning eyes and throat; crime against humanity. Restaurant owner Mehdi: fear like Covid, soot everywhere, we are on our own. Mehnaz fled; gas rationed, long queues; dark like our futures. Prices up; inhalers expensive; gasoline five-litre limits. UK doctor Kordasti warned of airway and heart risks. Residents say regime left people on their own; water from dams at risk. When the birds abandon you, you are truly on your own.
