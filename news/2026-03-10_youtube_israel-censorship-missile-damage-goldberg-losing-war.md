## Key Points

### Israeli casualties and damage reporting
- Israel's state-run health ministry: 13 killed in Iranian strikes
- State-run military claims most missiles intercepted
- **Very little verifiable footage** of damage within Israel
- What footage exists appears "relatively superficial" and filmed "long after" strikes
- "Almost something calm about it" — no panic, no upset, nobody crying
- **Contrast with 2024-2025:** In previous Iranian attacks, verifiable footage of missiles over Israeli airspace was readily available; in 2026 "that's a lot more difficult"
- Social media flooded with AI-generated content making verification harder

### Media censorship measures
- **France 24 report:** IDF banned live broadcast of Israeli skyline during alarms; filming of air defense interceptions now forbidden (was allowed during June 2025 war); filming impacts near security sites banned
- **Ben-Gvir (far-right Minister of National Security):** Vowed "severity and zero tolerance" against international media violating censorship; police dispatched; detentions, arrests made
- Joint statement with Communications Minister: "Anyone who endangers Israel's citizens in the name of 'journalistic reporting' will face a determined and tough police force"

### Ory Goldberg (Israeli academic) statement
> "The level of damage is not remotely close to what Israel is doing in Iran and in Lebanon. The issue is that the Israeli government heavily censors any news regarding casualties and deaths as well as actual missile hits. We also get regular lies about the number of missile launchers Iran and Hezbollah have and about Israeli success in taking such launchers out of action. Over the past few days, missiles and rockets have hit Israel with regularity that is being officially denied. Alarms have sounded in the midst of rockets hitting. Advanced warnings are often given without alarms sounding — placing responsibility for sheltering on citizen shoulders. Information about hits is disseminated through unofficial channels. There are clearly hits on any number of strategic sites. We get no official information."

### Analysis: Why censor?
- **Fear of losing:** "It speaks to a tremendous fear on the part of the Israeli political class... that they're losing on their own terms at least — they're not meeting the objectives they set out with"
- **Global mood shift:** "They know that the mood in the rest of the world has completely changed towards them"
- **Identity crisis:** Israel used to "grandstand on its militarism" — was part of popularity when they were seen as "good guys protecting Western interests"; that framing has "completely shifted"
- **Domestic concealment:** More telling that they're withholding information from their own public, not just international audiences
- **Not squeamishness:** Israeli public "lives in perpetual state of war and many support it" — so censorship is not about protecting sensibilities

### Predicted consequences

- Will "speed up the rate in which Israeli society begins to fracture" — contradictions around apartheid and genocide will be exposed

### Other observations
