## Full story

US President Donald Trump has fired Secretary of Homeland Security Kristi Noem after mounting criticism over her leadership. Trump announced on Truth Social on Thursday, two days after Noem was grilled on Capitol Hill. Noem will take a new role as Special Envoy for the Shield of the Americas, a new security initiative in the Western Hemisphere to be announced Saturday. Trump said Oklahoma senator Markwayne Mullin will replace her; Mullin can serve as acting secretary while his nomination is pending. Noem was under pressure over immigration: she described two civilians shot dead by ICE agents in Minnesota (Renee Good and Alex Pretti) as engaged in domestic terrorism; video evidence did not support those claims. She also faced scrutiny over a 220 million dollar ad campaign featuring her urging people in the country illegally to leave voluntarily; Noem said Trump was aware in advance but Trump told Reuters he did not sign off on it. She also faced criticism over FEMA emergency funding and disaster response. Noem is the first cabinet secretary to leave during Trump's second term.
