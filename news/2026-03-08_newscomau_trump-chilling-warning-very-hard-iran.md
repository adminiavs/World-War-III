## Full story

Welcome to our live coverage of the US and Israel’s conflict with Iran and its proxies.

Israel and the US have pounded Iran in a fresh wave of air strikes, with one setting a Tehran airport ablaze, after US President Donald Trump warned the country “will be hit very hard” on Saturday (Sunday AET).

“Today Iran will be hit very hard! Under serious consideration for complete destruction and certain death, because of Iran’s bad behaviour, are areas and groups of people that were not considered for targeting up until this moment in time,” Mr Trump wrote on Truth Social as the US forces’ “Operation Epic Fury” enters its second week.

Soon after, footage shared on social media captured a massive fireball and plumes of black smoke billowing from Mehrabad International Airport, which housed the headquarters for Iran’s national carrier.

Meanwhile, Iran launched waves of missile and drone attacks on its Gulf neighbours hosting US forces and vowed no surrender despite threats from Mr Trump to widen the conflict.

In a statement, Iran’s Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi said Mr Trump had underestimated Iran’s “capabilities, determination and intent”.

Dubai’s main airport, a key global transport hub, was forced to briefly close on Saturday amid the strikes.

The barrage came despite Iran’s president apologising to neighbouring countries for its attacks, claiming they would no longer be targeted unless strikes were launched from their territory.

Attacks were reported on Saturday in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar and Kuwait, with the Emirates saying it was targeted with 16 ballistic missiles and more than 120 drones.

The conflict has since widened to Lebanon, as well as Cyprus, Turkey and Azerbaijan, and reached as far as waters off Sri Lanka where US forces sank an Iranian warship with a torpedo.

'Maybe we will': Trump floats boots on the ground in Iran

Harrison Christian

Donald Trump has suggested the US could send ground troops into Iran to secure the country’s stockpiles of enriched uranium, as part of efforts to dismantle Tehran’s nuclear program.

“At some point maybe we will. That would be a great thing,” the US president told reporters during a briefing aboard Air Force One on Sunday AEDT.

“We haven’t gone after it but it’s something we could do later,” he added.

Critics of the Iran war, launched a week ago, have said airstrikes alone will not be enough to achieve Trump’s stated goals of destroying Iran’s military capacity and preventing it from developing a nuclear bomb.

Asked about the use of ground troops in general, Trump did not rule it out, saying: “Could there be? Possibly, for a very good reason. It’d have to be a very good reason.”

“And I would say if we ever did that, they would be so decimated that they wouldn’t be able to fight at the ground level,” he said.

Trump blames Iran for deadly strike on girls' school

Harrison Christian

Donald Trump has blamed Iran for what the country’s authorities say was a deadly strike on a girls’ school on day one of the war, killing more than 150 people.

“We think it was done by Iran. Because they are very inaccurate, as you know, with their munitions. They have no accuracy whatsoever,” Trump told reporters aboard Air Force One on Sunday AEDT.

Iran has blamed Washington for the strike in the southern county of Minab last Saturday, while the US and Israel have not claimed responsibility, and US officials say it remains under investigation.

Iran’s Revolutionary Guards said on Friday local time they had targeted a US base in the UAE that they alleged had been used as a launchpad for the strike.

“Al-Dhafra air base, belonging to American terrorists in the region, was targeted using drones and precision missiles,” the Guards said in a statement broadcast on state TV. 

The Pentagon has confirmed it is investigating, while Secretary of State Marco Rubio has said the US would “not deliberately target a school”.

The New York Times newspaper reported that US military statements indicating forces were attacking naval targets near the Strait of Hormuz, where a Revolutionary Guards’ base is located, “suggest they were most likely to have carried out the strike.”

An analysis of social media posts from the time of the attack, as well as photos and videos from witnesses, indicated that the school had been struck at the same time as Guards’ naval base sites, the Times said.

Tiny island could be the key to Iran war

Harrison Christian

A historian has revealed what he believes is Donald Trump’s ultimate goal in the war: the capture of a tiny island off the coast of Iran.

Kharg Island, according to Guy Laron from Hebrew University, is a “tiny strip of land” in the northern Persian Gulf, one hour by boat from the Iranian shore. It houses oil terminals through which Iran exports 90 per cent of its crude oil.

Despite heavy international sanctions, oil remains the primary driver of Iran’s economy.

China is the leading customer for Iranian crude oil, accounting for about 90 percent of total oil exports, and circumventing US sanctions by using a “shadow fleet” of tankers.

Drone hits luxury skyscraper in Dubai

Harrison Christian

Footage shows smoke rising from Dubai’s famous 23 Marina tower after an Iranian drone attack.

'We don't need them': Trump's swipe at UK prime minister

World's busiest airport briefly closes after strike

Jack Evans

Dubai International Airport, recognised globally as the world’s busiest hub for international travel, experienced significant disruptions on Saturday after air defence systems intercepted an unidentified object over the facility.

The UAE said its air defences intercepted 15 missiles and 119 drones on Saturday morning, and video footage showed one projectile crashing at the airport.

The Dubai Media Office confirmed that operations were temporarily halted as a precautionary measure to ensure the safety of passengers, airport personnel, and airline crews.

Emirates, the Middle East’s largest carrier, immediately suspended all departures and arrivals, strongly advising travellers to avoid the airport entirely.

Trump vows to 'take care' of Cuba after Iran

Harrison Christian

Donald Trump has said Cuba is in its “last moments of life” and vowed to “take care” of the Caribbean nation, even as the Iran war enters its second week with no end in sight.

Speaking to South American leaders in Florida on Sunday AEDT, Trump said the US was “taking out tremendous amounts of oil” from Venezuela after the capture of dictator Nicolas Maduro and hinted that Cuba was next on his agenda.

Trump said the US administration had been working closely with the new president of Venezuela, Delcy Rodriguez, and, she was “doing a great job working with us”.

“This week we have formally recognized the Venezuelan government … We have also just reached a historic gold deal that’s called the gold deal with Venezuela, to allow our two countries to work together to facilitate the sale of Venezuelan gold and other minerals,” he said.
