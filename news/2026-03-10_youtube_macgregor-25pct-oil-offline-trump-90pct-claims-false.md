## Key Points

### Trump's claims vs. reality
- **Trump claimed:** Iranian missile capability "down to about 10%, maybe less"; 90% decline in missile launchers; 83% drop in drone launches
- **Macgregor:** "I don't think that we're having the effect that he's describing... these kinds of statistics really aren't terribly meaningful."
- Iran now announcing ballistic missiles carry minimum **1,000 lb payload up to 2,000 lb** — "not skimping in any way"
- Iran shooting down "all of the terrestrial ISR platforms" — manned and unmanned surveillance aircraft
- **Eastern Iran untouched:** Underground "missile cities" out of range of US weapons

### Global oil crisis — the numbers
- **25% of world's oil now offline** — "not for a few days or weeks. In some cases, it's indefinite."
- **Gulf nations slashed output by 6.7 million barrels/day** — 33% of regional production
- Storage tanks full; ships can't transit; production must be shut down
- **35% of world's fertilizer** no longer being shipped

### Regional energy storage crisis
- **Europe:** Entered March 2026 with ~46 billion cubic meters LNG (vs 60 billion in 2025)
- By Feb 28, LNG storage fell **below 30% capacity**
- **China/India:** Each depends on 50% of oil from Gulf
- **China's strategic reserve:** 104-115 days of import cover, but US shut down two primary discounted sources (Venezuela, Iran)

### Economic consequences for US
- **Inflation coming:** Electricity costs will rise, industrial goods more expensive
- **Monetary policy constrained:** Fed can't cut interest rates in current environment
- **Timeline:** "May not be felt by mid-April, but by the time summer hits, it's going to get ugly"
- Natural gas fuels 40% of US power; higher gas prices = immediate utility bill increases

### Insurance and Hormuz
- **Ships cannot be insured** to transit Strait of Hormuz
- Trump complained ship captains are "gutless" for not forcing through
- Macgregor: "Anything that tries to go through those straits that the Iranians don't want to go through will in fact be sunk. They have extensive weapon systems, missiles dug into the cliffs."

### The real demands are Israeli
- Macgregor: "The demands that President Trump has been making are not his. They never were. They are Israeli demands."
- **Israeli demands:** No enrichment of any kind; dismantle ballistic missiles; no regional allies
- "The Iranians are not going to do that. They're not going to surrender."
- Iran's position: "Better to die standing up than live on your knees."

### Cost of war
- **$5.4 billion in first 24-36 hours**
- Kosovo air campaign (78 days, 1999): $4 billion total
- "$50 billion as a supplement may not be enough"

### Jack Keane's argument (countered)
- Keane: "Are we really saying we cannot accept several weeks of oil prices being higher... to take Iran off the map as a predator?"
- Macgregor: "Can we destroy Iran? Yes... You can destroy everything. If it doesn't move, if it's a building, if it sits still, you can strike it."
- "Will that cause the state to collapse? I don't think so."
- **"How does Iran win? It survives."**

### Macgregor's conclusion
- "We're making enemies everywhere at a very high rate of speed."
- "The damage has just begun... Eventually, it'll hit. The guy that comes home at night and says, 'I'm going down to the 7-Eleven, get a six-pack of beer'... his life is going to be disrupted."
- "It's a terrible goal. It doesn't make military sense. It's not politically useful to us or economically useful to anybody."
