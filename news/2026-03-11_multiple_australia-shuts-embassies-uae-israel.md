# Australia Shuts Embassies in UAE and Israel

## Sources
- Al Arabiya (11 Mar 2026)
- Caliber.az (11 Mar 2026)
- Straits Times (11 Mar 2026)

## Key Facts

Foreign Minister Penny Wong confirmed Australia has physically closed embassies in Abu Dhabi and Tel Aviv, and consulate in Dubai.

"At least nine cities where Australian embassies and consulates are located have been hit by missile strikes and drone attacks."

"Iran's reprisal attacks continue at scale and at a depth that we have not seen before."

Conflict "likely to intensify and continue in the near term."

## Actions Taken

- Abu Dhabi embassy: physically closed
- Tel Aviv embassy: physically closed  
- Dubai consulate: physically closed
- Dependants of diplomatic officials in UAE and Israel directed to leave

## Australian Citizens

- 3,200+ Australians returned from Middle East on 23 commercial flights
- Approximately 115,000 citizens were in region when conflict began
- Limited commercial flights resuming; consular efforts expanding

## Wong Statement to Parliament

"The dangerous and destabilising attacks by Iran put civilian lives at risk, including Australian lives, and we continue to support Australians affected as limited commercial flights resume and we expand consular efforts."

## Context

Australia has deployed 85 personnel, E-7A Wedgetail, and air-to-air missiles to UAE for "collective self-defence." Wong continues to deny Australia is "at war" while diplomatic missions close and citizens evacuate.

Pattern: Wong simultaneously says Australia not at war, while closing three diplomatic missions due to missile/drone attacks on nine cities where Australian embassies are located.
