## Core Argument: America Resembles 18th Century Britain

### Britain's Two Economies (18th Century)
- **Domestic economy**: growing 1-2% per year
- **Overseas economy**: growing 25-40% per year (trade, colonies, extraction)
- The overseas economy was where ambitious people wanted to be
- British population was **not needed** for overseas accumulation
- Political exclusion of nearly everybody was the **prerequisite** for this system
- Only property-owning men had political voice
- Britain was a "very successful state" — but success didn't require including the population

### America's Equivalent Today
- Production moving out of America
- AI displacing labor sectors
- "Political assault on labor" and rights claimed from the state
- America **doesn't need its population** strategically the way it did in 1950s-60s
- Population increasingly "shut out of main drivers of the economy"
- Short-term strategy: "conceal the reality from Americans so they don't really know what's going on"
- Long-term: pressure to withdraw political rights population has enjoyed

> "The interests of the state are diverging from the interests of the people."

## Correlation: Industrialization = Democracy; De-industrialization = ?

### The Western Exception (1820-1974)
- In 1800: being rich in Africa, Asia, or Europe was "pretty much the same thing" — class mattered, not location
- 1820-1974: "almost a straight line" of West pulling away from everyone else
- By end of period: "better to be poor in the West than rich elsewhere"
- This 150-year period = "golden period of Western liberalism"
- Peak equality, democratic institutions = mid-1970s
- Also = peak Western advantage over rest of world

### What Changed in 1974?
- Asia started climbing back (Hong Kong, Taiwan, South Korea, then China)
- Western liberalism began its decline precisely when Asia began its rise
- "The astonishing achievements we associate with the Western state from the 19th and 20th centuries have in common this weird Asian absence"
- Bottom 50% of Western countries found themselves in "direct labor competition with Asia"
- Value of their labor dropping
- "We're not really part of this national project anymore"

### The Implication
> "There is a very very close correlation between industrialization on a massive scale in the West and democracy. De-industrialization, artificial intelligence, and all that has a very different relationship... that's the world we're entering into now."

## The Nation-State System as American Empire

### British vs American Empire
- British Empire: "reduced and usurped national governments"
- American Empire: "multiplied and backed them"
- "Where the Roman Empire's administrative unit was the province, the American Empire's was the independent nation state"

### Same Purpose, Different Mechanism
- Both empires do the same work: **transfer resources from poor to rich parts of world**
- America doesn't occupy vast swathes — it imposes capitalist laws through external coercion
- Plants gunboats, sends threats, infiltrates systems, applies tariffs
- Gets its policies legislated in foreign sovereign states

### Post-1980s Completion
- Washington Consensus, WTO (1995), bilateral investment treaties
- "Sovereign governments doing the work of this empire"
- Policing piracy of American goods, ensuring access to mining resources
- Massively increased efficiency of resource flow into industrial system

## China Outflanking the System

### How China Bypassed American Rules
- Doesn't have same commitment to nation-state as fundamental unit
- Does deals with warlords, factions, non-state actors
- Sends companies where American corporations can't function
- More efficient extraction, fewer legal/political burdens
- Told countries: "We're not those guys. We won't dictate your laws, your foreign policy."
- Bartered: highways, police training, equipment for resources and immunity

### America's Response
> "America is now an equivalent power to China or Russia. It is no longer in some other layer of the global system. It is in the same layer. Doing the same kinds of things."

> "America is having to catch up with China and do things that it hasn't done for decades, such as simply contemplate conquering places or seizing their oil on the high seas. These are very 18th century things."

Trump wanting Greenland and Canada = "clear signs" America is now an equivalent power, not the superpower above the system.

## The Religious Nature of the State

### The State as "Mortal God" (Hobbes)
- 18th century: philosophers reinvented the state as a quasi-god
- Religious allegiance previously given to God redirected to state
- "The state is all good. The state is all powerful. The state is all knowing."
- Hobbes and others were "in their origins theologians"

### Islamist Critique (Sayyid Qutb)
- Egyptian theologian (1940s-1965, executed)
- Argued that giving religious allegiance to the state is **idolatrous**
- "The state is not a godhead. There is only one sovereign — God."
- "We have to dismantle all these man-made institutions that we're being asked to treat as godlike objects."
- Dasgupta: "The most significant alternative conception of political and social organization in the world today... more than communism, Islamist critiques of liberalism and of the nation state are enduring problems for this system."

### European Far Right Says Similar Things
- Charles Maurras (1890s France): called for return to Catholic theocratic state, monarchy
- Critique of state as "iconoclastic object that does not respect lineage, religion, tradition, folk cultures"
- Intellectual ancestor of Le Pen
- "The European far right's critique of capitalism dovetails massively with that of radical Islam"
- "They're drawing on really similar sources"

## What Comes After?

### Leading States Aren't Going Anywhere
- "They are the political apparatus of the capitalist system"
- While capitalism exists, states will exist to administer corporations, laws, labor

### But the System is Fragmenting
- Competition between imperial states: China, Russia, US, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Turkey
- "Much more normal than the phase we've been in until quite recently"
- No single superpower representing "the system" with an ideology
- "We might lose that sense" of being part of a coherent values-based system

### Liberalism's Crisis
- "Liberalism has no means of recovering its former prestige"
- It arose with the nation state; functioned when West was much richer
- "Starts breaking down when those conditions disappear"
- Need intellectual renaissance "on the scale of the Enlightenment"
- Rethink equality, democracy, human rights — where will they be institutionalized?
