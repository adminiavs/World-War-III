# China halts refinery exports, cuts jet fuel supply to Australia

**Date:** 13 March 2026
**Source:** news.com.au / PerthNow / AFR / Reuters
**URL:** https://www.news.com.au/world/asia/china-halts-refinery-exports-cuts-jet-fuel-supply-to-australia/news-story/9cde7858fc941aa46fb9663f083344c5

---

## tl;dr

China has ordered its refineries to halt fuel exports in response to the Iran war, directly threatening Australia's jet fuel supply. Chinese authorities instructed refineries not to load fuel cargoes that had not cleared customs by March 11, 2026. Australian refineries account for 32% of Australia's jet fuel imports. At least two cargoes destined for Australia have been affected. March deliveries are expected to proceed, but April shipments are being closely monitored. The halt reflects China's own supply crisis: China sources roughly half its crude from the Middle East, and major refineries (Zhejiang Petrochemical, Fujian Refining) have already cut production by up to 20%. Domestic Chinese fuel prices: diesel +13.5%, gasoline +11%. Broader impact on Asia expected from April as other suppliers (South Korea, Singapore, Malaysia, Japan) also face refinery cuts.

---

## Why this matters

Australia is almost entirely dependent on overseas jet fuel. Australia has no significant domestic crude oil refining capacity -- the last major domestic refinery closed in 2015. With the Strait of Hormuz disrupted and Middle Eastern supply chains broken, Australia has been relying heavily on Asian suppliers, particularly Chinese refineries. Now that source is also drying up.

This is the chain Chomsky described twenty years ago: the oil shock feeds into a regional supply crisis, which cascades across multiple fuel types and reaches countries far from the war zone.

The immediate risk: April jet fuel deliveries. March shipments cleared customs before the cut-off and are expected to arrive. April is the question. If the Strait remains closed and Chinese refineries remain constrained, Australia faces a jet fuel shortage on top of the existing diesel and petrol crisis.

## Key facts

- **China's share of Australian jet fuel imports (2025):** 32%
- **Deadline:** Chinese authorities told refineries not to load cargoes that had not cleared customs by March 11, 2026
- **Cargoes affected:** At least two destined for Australia
- **Chinese refinery cuts:** Major plants (Zhejiang Petrochemical, Fujian Refining) cutting production up to 20%
- **Why China is affected:** China sources ~50% of its crude oil from the Middle East; the Iran war has disrupted that supply chain
- **Chinese domestic price rises:** Diesel +13.5%, gasoline +11%
- **Expected broader impact:** Supply disruption to roll across Asia from April, as South Korea, Singapore, Malaysia, and Japan also face refinery pressure
