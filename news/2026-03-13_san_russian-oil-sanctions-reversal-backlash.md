# The plan behind Russian oil sanctions and why a reversal faces backlash

**Date:** 13 March 2026
**Source:** Straight Arrow News (SAN)
**URL:** https://san.com/cc/the-plan-behind-russian-oil-sanctions-and-why-a-reversal-faces-backlash/

---

## tl;dr

The US Treasury temporarily lifted sanctions on Russian oil (oil already at sea, 30 days until 11 April) to dampen high oil prices from the Iran war. Brent was still above $102/barrel after the move — “the market kind of shrugged off the announcement.” MIT energy economist Catherine Wolfram (former Treasury, helped design the Russian price cap): the administration “gave all of those gains away”; Russia has ~100 million barrels at sea and Urals has gained $30+/barrel since the war began (~$150M extra per day). EU Council President Costa: “very concerning,” impacts European security. Macron: Hormuz closure is “in no way” a reason to lift sanctions. US Democrats criticised; Sen. Kim: “Putin and Xi are the happiest people on Earth.” Sen. Grassley (R): “wrong move” that “prolongs suffering in Ukraine.” CFR’s Fishman: “effectively the destruction of the oil sanctions on Russia” if the relief becomes permanent.

**Impact on predictions:** Chomsky’s “Russia benefits” and “oil shock” both confirmed — US is trading sanctions integrity for short-term oil relief and facing allied backlash. Fits “backfire” (Paul) and “creative destruction” (Kovalik) narratives.
