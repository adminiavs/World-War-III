## tl;dr

US launched Section 301 (Trade Act 1974) investigations into unfair trade practices — structural excess capacity and production in manufacturing — with China squarely in the crosshairs among a dozen trading partners, less than three weeks before Trump-Xi summit in Beijing (31 Mar–2 Apr). Analysts: Trump's negotiating position weakened by Iran war; US needs credible tariff threat; summit unlikely to yield major breakthrough; both sides seeking to maintain fragile truce. Iran war has risked China's energy supplies and complicated Beijing's calculus; China sent special envoy to region to mediate, urged ceasefire. Supreme Court struck down Trump's "reciprocal" tariffs last month, curtailing his tariff leverage; Section 301 allows levies without Congress. CEO delegation accompanying Trump to Beijing appears to be fading.
