## tl;dr

After late-February 2026 US-Israeli strikes and effective closure of the Strait of Hormuz, **jet fuel** (not just crude or LNG) is the most severely dislocated derivative: crack spreads doubled, Asian jet cracks to $52/bbl, European outright prices at 28-month highs. ~18–20% of global jet fuel exports transit Hormuz; Europe sources 25–50% from Gulf (Kuwait top). Jet A-1 cannot be substituted with diesel (freeze point -47°C); inventories and refining are structurally brittle. **Valuation (Damodaran-style):** 50% jet fuel spike ≈ $125B annualized extra costs globally; Gulf refinery asset devaluation ≈ $200B in medium-disruption scenario; prolonged 90+ days → combined stakes $500B–$1T (aviation, infrastructure, inflation). **System dynamics:** Reinforcing loops (disruption → inventory down → prices up → hoarding) dominate; balancing loop (demand destruction) lags 4–8 weeks; “slow to save” (refineries throttle to avoid damage) tightens product further. China/Asian refineries (e.g. Zhejiang Petrochemical 200k bpd unit shut) cutting runs 20–30%. **Paper vs physical:** Treasury futures intervention (Bessent) cannot reopen Hormuz or restore tanker insurance; physical levers (naval escorts, war-risk insurance, SPR) determine outcome. ## Impact on Australia predictions

- **Strengthens** existing Australia fuel-crisis predictions. Jet fuel is the globally most fragile link; Australia’s 32% jet fuel imports from China (and China’s own Gulf feedstock squeeze and export halt) sit inside this structural squeeze. The piece explains *why* jet fuel dislocates first and longest (no diesel substitution, lower buffers, Gulf-dependent).
- **Magnitude:** $125B+ annualized jet fuel cost shock and $200B Gulf refinery devaluation imply aviation and refined-product stress can be worse and longer-lasting than a simple “oil spike” narrative. Supports “fuel crisis will deepen,” “rationing likely,” and “secondary supply crises beyond Hormuz” (China/Asian refining).
- **No contradiction.** Paper (Treasury) tools “smooth speculative premium” but “cannot create barrels”; aligns with Australia’s physical dependence on imported refined fuel and limited strategic reserves. System-dynamics “reinforcing loops dominate” supports predictions that the crisis will not resolve quickly without physical resolution of Hormuz/tanker flows.
