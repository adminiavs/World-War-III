# Iran oil exports climb to pre-war highs despite Hormuz closure

**Date:** 12 March 2026
**Source:** The New Arab
**URL:** https://www.newarab.com/news/iran-oil-exports-climb-pre-war-highs-despite-hormuz-closure

---

## tl;dr

Kpler data: seven tankers carried Iranian oil out of the region since 28 February. Iran exported an average of 2.1 million barrels per day (5–10 March), higher than the 2 million in February. Non-Iranian vessels remain blocked from the Strait (~20 million barrels/day removed from global supply). Iran is using the Strait closure to block Arab Gulf producers while continuing its own exports via shadow tankers, mostly to China. “Iran’s control of the strait has handed it a vital financial lifeline … and is allowing it to undercut Washington’s efforts to throttle the country’s revenues.” IEA called the loss of Gulf energy the “largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market.”

**Impact on predictions:** Iran is not “closed” — it is selectively open for itself and (per other stories) for selected partners (e.g. India). Asymmetric leverage (Jiang): Iran keeps revenue while denying it to US allies and the global market. Contradicts any narrative that Iran is purely “losing” economically.
