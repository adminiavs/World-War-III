## Full story

The ABC heard from Iranians inside Iran via voice notes during intermittent connection. Many celebrated and prepared for "freedom day" after Khamenei was killed; a Tehran emergency room was "like a party" with patients drinking to celebrate. Others felt fear and anxiety. Journalist Bahman Ahmadi Amouee said a significant portion of those killed were ordinary people, especially girls at educational and medical centres; "day by day their anger and rage at this war is growing." Plain-clothes and IRGC cracked down on street celebrations; some younger IRGC discreetly urged people to beep car horns. Parisa (Tehran) described shock waves and destroyed streets (Niloufar Square, Narmak). Evin prisoners were moved to an unknown location. Mixed emotions: hope the regime falls and Reza Pahlavi returns versus fear of protracted war and more civilian loss.
