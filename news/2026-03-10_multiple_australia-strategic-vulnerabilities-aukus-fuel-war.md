## Key Points

### AUKUS Concerns in Wartime Context

**Source: The Conversation (Hugh White, ANU) - 9 March 2026**

- "At a time when diplomatic realities, strategic imperatives and political expedience all suggest we should gently but firmly distance ourselves from Trump's America, AUKUS ties us tighter than ever"
- Three Australian AUKUS trainees were on the US fast-attack submarine that sunk the Iranian warship IRIS Dena
- Pentagon's AUKUS review (completed before Albanese's October visit) raised concerns but conclusions not publicly disclosed
- Key unresolved problems:
  1. US has no Virginia-class subs to spare (shipyards delivered 4 of 11 planned between 2019-2023)
  2. Defence spending insufficient (A$365 billion over 30 years but only growing to 2.3% GDP by mid-2030s)
  3. No clear commitment from Canberra on whether Australia would support US in war with China
- Former Biden official Ely Ratner (Feb 2026): "need for very serious and deep alliance conversations about our expectations around roles and missions" — implying these conversations haven't happened
- White argues: "AUKUS is a perfect symbol of... an attempt by America to get Australia to pay to bolster US military power"
- Context: Canadian PM Mark Carney at Davos and Australian Parliament urged middle powers to stop depending on Washington

### Fuel Security Crisis

**Source: Australian Financial Review - 10 March 2026**

- Australia imports over 90% of refined oil products (petrol and diesel)
- Only approximately one month of spare fuel reserves
- Tehran's vow to maintain Strait of Hormuz blockade "if attacks continue" directly threatens Australian supply
- AFR editorial: "current policy settings may have worked during a more benign era of globalisation. But just-in-time supply chains risk arriving too little and too late"
- Calls for Albanese government to confront fuel security vulnerabilities

### Wong Denies Australia "At War" Despite Military Deployment

**Source: 7NEWS Sunrise - 10 March 2026**

- Foreign Minister Penny Wong: "We are not taking offensive action against Iran. We're not providing boots on the ground to go into Iran"
- Australia has deployed:
  - 85 ADF personnel
  - E-7A Wedgetail reconnaissance aircraft
  - Medium-range air-to-air missiles
  - To UAE to "assist defensive capabilities"
- Wong: "What we are providing is a capability that helps the Gulf countries defend themselves against the attacks Iran is engaging in"
- Wong rejected suggestion Australia is "already at war" — described operation as "collective self-defence"
- 115,000 Australians currently in Middle East region, including ~24,000 in UAE at start of conflict
- Wong noted Australia was "already the target of an Iranian attack" before conflict (leading to ambassador expulsion)
- National terrorism threat level remains at "probable" — unchanged after deployment
