## Full story

The Pierburg plant in Berlin is halting the production of automotive components and will begin manufacturing casings for 155-mm artillery shells. The IG Metall Berlin reported on this. The plant owner, defense group Rheinmetall, made the decision amid a crisis in the automotive industry and rising demand for ammunition. Instead of producing engine pumps and valves, the facility will machine steel shell bodies. Management has already transferred nearly 300 employees to the newly created defense division, Rheinmetall Waffe Munition GmbH. The plant will become part of the company global production network for 155-mm artillery shells. Rheinmetall plans to reach 1.1 million shells by the end of 2026, roughly 10 times pre-war capacity of about 100,000 units. The main hub remains Unterluss (Lower Saxony). The Spanish asset Expal adds about 300,000 rounds per year. New plants in Hungary (Varpalota) and Lithuania are expected in 2026. The Berlin Pierburg plant will function as a specialized machining facility; steel billets are processed into projectile bodies and transported by rail to Unterluss or other European facilities for final assembly.
