## Full story

**Setup.** Pretend March 2027. Trump goes on TV and announces **Operation Iranian Freedom**: full-scale US invasion of Iran with Israel, Saudi Arabia as main partners, plus UK, Australia, UAE, Poland. He gives five reasons for the war.

**Reason 1.** For the past year violent protests in Iran (religious, political, ethnic). Iranians are sick of the Ayatollah and the dictatorship; they want democracy and freedom. In response the IRGC are killing thousands. Iran is on the brink of civil war. The US has an obligation to protect the people of Iran; they are “praying for freedom and democracy”; the world’s greatest democracy must give them democracy.

**Reason 2.** US and Israeli intelligence have found that for two years Iran has been working very hard on a nuclear bomb. Iran is one month away from having three nuclear bombs so that New York City, San Francisco, and Los Angeles could be destroyed. To prevent this, we must strike first to stop Iran’s nuclear program.

**Reason 3.** Iranian proxies (Houthis, Hezbollah, others) have been disrupting shipping in the Red Sea and Strait of Hormuz. 40% of the world’s oil passes through that region, mainly to East Asia (China, Japan, South Korea). Global prosperity is at stake. We must go to war to protect shipping lanes and ensure oil reaches those countries. The US as world hegemon has a responsibility to protect global prosperity.

**Reason 4.** Iran has been attacking allies Saudi Arabia and Israel. Hezbollah violently attacking Israel, killing dozens of innocent Israelis. Houthis attacking Saudi oil fields, damaging the economy. We have an obligation to protect our allies and friends in the Middle East.

**Reason 5.** We know for a fact the IRGC is a sponsor of terrorism. Last week gunmen in a mall killed 107 people. Our intelligence says it was most definitely an Iranian operation. So for these five reasons we have decided to invade Iran.

**Why it will succeed (Trump).** Don’t worry: we have many allies (UK, Israel, Saudi, etc.). Our special forces are already in the country looking for air bases and missiles; we can knock them out at the start. We have been in contact with Iranian opposition groups; they want to overthrow the regime, and when we help them they will install democracy. Our military is the greatest; in 1991 we defeated Saddam Hussein in 100 hours in less than three weeks. We will defeat Iran in only two weeks. We will protect democracy, freedom, and global prosperity.

**Scenario.** Operation launched. USS Gerald R. Ford ($13B supercarrier) in Strait of Hormuz to keep shipping safe. America establishes air supremacy quickly (full control of the skies). Massive invasion force to southern Iran: 100,000 US troops land, maybe 200,000 Saudi, total 200,000 to half a million. They have a foothold in the south and are about to strike Tehran in the north. **Question: at this point, who has won the war?** **Answer: Iran.** (Trick question.)

**Why the US has already lost (traditional military doctrine).** Three principles: mass forces, avoid encirclement, protect supply lines.

(1) **Encirclement.** Iran is all mountains; it’s a fortress. To get troops in you have to airdrop them. Once they’re in, you can’t get them out. They are encircled.

(2) **Failed to mass forces.** Iran has a population of 90 million. You need at least 3–4 million soldiers to have any chance of occupying the country. They have 100,000. You can’t move tanks through mountains; they’re not designed for mountain warfare. So they can’t mass forces and they’re encircled.

(3) **No supply lines (biggest problem).** They cannot be resupplied. To resupply, airplanes have to drop ammunition and food. But Iran is mountains; you can’t see what’s on the ground. A random person with a rocket launcher can shoot down a helicopter (as in Afghanistan against the Soviets). Drones can take down aircraft. So these troops are trapped. They’re not really soldiers; they’re **hostages**: too many to get out, not enough to strike Tehran, can’t resupply, encircled by Iranian forces.

**Why won’t Iranians revolt?** The American hope is that once US troops are in, resistance to the regime will rise and the Iranian army will mutiny. Why won’t that happen?

- **Iranians hate America.** They remember the Shah from 1953 to 1979. The American-supported Shah ran a brutal police state; everyone in Iran revolted in 1979.
- **Iraq.** America destroyed Iraq from 2003 to 2011. America said it would bring freedom, democracy, prosperity. Instead it destroyed the country. American soldiers ran into houses at night, pointed guns at children, destroyed homes, arrested men for no reason. Iranians saw this on TV and heard from neighbors. They know that if America comes, it’s to destroy the country, not to bring freedom or prosperity.
- **Civilization and independence.** Iranians see themselves as part of a great civilization; they value freedom and independence and won’t submit to a foreign conqueror.
- **Religion.** Iranians are religious; they believe America is Satan and have a religious duty to fight. So most will resist; only a minority might side with America.

**Why do Americans think Iranians will rise up?** They need to justify the invasion. One criticism will be that you need on the order of 4 million troops to invade Iran. They don’t have 4 million; they have at most about 1 million total worldwide, about 60,000 in the Middle East. So they can’t field enough soldiers. The only way to explain why the invasion will succeed is to say that once we invade, the people of Iran will support us. So it’s not based on truth; it’s an explanation for why the invasion will “succeed.”
