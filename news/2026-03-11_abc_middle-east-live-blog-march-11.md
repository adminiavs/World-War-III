## tl;dr

ABC live blog Day 11: Hegseth announces "most intense" day of strikes; Iran launches new missile salvo at Tel Aviv and US targets using Fattah/Emad/Khaibar strategic missiles; Iran parliamentary speaker Qalibaf says "eye for an eye" and "if they start a war on infrastructure, we will undoubtedly target infrastructure"; 7 Iranian women's footballers now granted asylum in Australia (5 players + 1 player + 1 staff confirmed by Tony Burke); Pentagon confirms ~140 US wounded (108 returned to duty, 8 severe); Trump claims 10 mine-laying vessels destroyed in Hormuz; Energy Secretary Chris Wright deleted false post claiming Navy escorted tanker through Hormuz (Leavitt confirmed no escorts yet); HMS Dragon departs Portsmouth for Eastern Mediterranean; Canada increases security at US/Israeli consulates after Toronto shooting; UAE/Israel/Iran exchange missile fire; Wong says war will continue "for some time."

## Key developments

### Iranian women's footballers asylum
- 7 total now granted asylum in Australia
- 5 players granted humanitarian visas Monday night
- Home Affairs Minister Tony Burke confirmed 1 additional player + 1 support staff granted visas Tuesday
- Team labelled "traitors" by Iranian state media for refusing to sing national anthem
- At least one refused to board flight at last minute
- Remaining team flew to Kuala Lumpur
- Burke: "They were given a choice... no rushing, no pressure"

### Military operations
- Hegseth: Tuesday "most intense" day of strikes since war began
- Netanyahu: "not done yet"
- IDF launched extensive wave of strikes on Tehran targeting "key infrastructure"
- Iran returned fire with missiles toward Israel (intercepted)
- UAE defense systems responded to missile threats
- Iran's parliamentary speaker Qalibaf: "eye for an eye," will target infrastructure if attacked

### Strait of Hormuz confusion
- Trump called on Iran to remove mines (while saying US has no reports of mines being placed)
- Trump claims 10 inactive mine-laying vessels destroyed
- Energy Secretary Chris Wright posted (then deleted) claim that Navy escorted tanker through Hormuz
- Leavitt confirmed: Navy has NOT escorted any vessel through Hormuz yet
- Iran Revolutionary Guards called Wright's post a "complete lie"

### Casualties
- Pentagon: ~140 US service members wounded over 10 days
- 108 returned to duty; 8 severely injured
- Israel allocating special war budget of ~40 billion shekels ($16 billion)

### Other
- HMS Dragon (Type 45 destroyer) departs UK for Eastern Mediterranean
- Canada increasing security at US/Israeli consulates after Toronto consulate shooting
- Iranians crossing into Turkey at Kapikoy border gate
- Iran UN envoy: March 7 fuel storage attacks caused "highly acidic" black rain
- Wong: war will continue "for some time" before diplomatic offramp
