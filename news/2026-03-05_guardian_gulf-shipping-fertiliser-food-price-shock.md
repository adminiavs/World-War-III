## Full story

The global fertiliser supply chain could face significant disruption if the effective closure by Iran of the Strait of Hormuz persists. Between a quarter and a third of global trade in fertiliser raw materials passes through the strait, as well as a fifth of seaborne crude and gas. The closure is affecting ammonia and nitrogen transport. Roughly half of global food production depends on synthetic nitrogen; without it yields fall and prices of bread, pasta, potatoes and animal feed rise. Iran is the fourth-largest global urea exporter. Fossil gas is 60–80% of nitrogen fertiliser production cost; Qatar closed its largest gas facility after a drone attack. Egyptian urea prices (benchmark) up more than 25% (CRU). Middle East is source of ~45% of global sulphur. UK meets ~40% of nitrogen fertiliser needs domestically. Tom Bradshaw (NFU): immediate price volatility, too early to say UK medium-term impact. Yara CEO Svein Tore Holsether: input prices rising, crop prices not; very big burden on farmers unless government steps up. A container ship was hit in the strait on Wednesday (engine room fire).
