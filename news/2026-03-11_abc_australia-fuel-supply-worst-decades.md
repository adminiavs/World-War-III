# Australian Fuel Supply: Worst in Decades

## Source
- ABC Rural (11 Mar 2026)

## Key Facts

Dozens of docked fishing trawlers and vessels stranded. Distributors describe shortages as "worst they have seen."

Retailers rationing fuel or limiting to emergencies. Some towns, farmers, and transport companies cut off completely.

Economists warn petrol prices could rise 40 cents per litre in weeks.

## First-Person Accounts

Danny Kreutzer (Westlink Petroleum, Queensland): "I am 59 years old. I have been in this business with my dad since I was 14 and I have never seen it as bad as what it is right now." 90 percent reduction in supplies. Lost 405,000 litres per day. 37 of 38 trucks at standstill.

Gretta Barton (Bartranz Petroleum): Wholesale price spikes "worst I have seen, times by 100." On average week distributes 1 million litres; now can only pick up 50,000 litres per day. Clients include farmers, transport companies, fishing trawlers.

Jake Turner (WA Wheatbelt farmer): Less than 500 litres left. Tractors use nearly 200 litres per hour. Ordered 20,000 litres two weeks ago; told delivery at least two weeks away. "We are 10 days in and we are feeling the impact. We have turned a farming operation off from front to back."

Pavo Walker (Walker Seafoods, Mooloolaba): "We have been informed that the three major fishing wharves at Mooloolaba have no fuel for us." Boats require 20,000-30,000 litres at a time. "We have 50 staff relying on the business."

Noel Cattell (veteran fisherman, 50+ years): "This time I am really worried. It is looking grim. If the fuel price goes up to anywhere from $2.50 to $3 a litre, we are pretty well out of business."

## Impact on Food Supply

Suppliers say farmers and fishing operations are days from grinding to a halt. Easter food supplies at risk.

Walker: "This week's haul could be the last catch for the foreseeable future."

Barton: "Farmers will stand to lose hundreds of thousands of dollars, transport companies will go under."

## Trucking Concerns

Debbie Gostelow (Far North Queensland trucking): "If we take these jobs, does it mean that we get 1,000 kilometres from home and not be able to fill up again?" Some servos stopped filling trucks to brim. "Heaven help us if we have got a full load of cattle on."

## Government Response

Energy Minister Bowen met industry representatives. "Every single contract is being honoured... This is not a supply problem." Big spike in spot market orders causing supply issues.

ACCC investigating fuel prices and sales. Watchdog wrote to companies requesting information on reserves and prices. Deputy chair Keogh wants to understand if prices "out of kilter with international trends."

## Context

Australia imports over 90 percent of refined oil products. Only approximately one month of reserves. Officials warn against panic buying. York service station in WA Wheatbelt limiting customers to 50 litres at a time.
