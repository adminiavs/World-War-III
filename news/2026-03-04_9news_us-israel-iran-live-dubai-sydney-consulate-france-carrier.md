## Full story

Hello, and welcome to our continuing coverage of the conflict in the Middle East.

Israel has been unrelenting with "simultaneous strikes in Tehran and Beirut" targeting Iranian military sites and the Iran-backed group Hezbollah.

Overnight, Israeli troops intensified their push in Lebanon.

President Donald Trump says Iran is seeking talks with the US, but indicated the opportunity for negotiations has passed. The president has also railed against NATO allies Spain and the UK.

US special envoy Steve Witkoff claimed Iran previously boasted of having enough enriched uranium to build almost a dozen nuclear bombs.

**Pentagon names four US soldiers killed (23:52)**

The Pentagon has identified four of the six US armed service members who were killed in an Iranian drone strike on Sunday.

The soldiers are Captain Cody Khork, 35; Sergeant First Class Noah Tietjens, 42; Sergeant First Class Nicole Amor, 39; and Sergeant Declan Coady, 20.

All four were assigned to the 103rd Sustainment Command, a US Army Reserve sustainment unit out of Iowa.

The two other soldiers who were killed in the strike on Sunday have not yet been identified.

**Stranded Americans say they 'just feel like a sitting duck' (By CNN 23:36)**

Americans stranded in the Middle East as the war in the region escalates are voicing their frustrations and fears as the Trump administration seeks to reassure them that they are working to get them home.

"I feel just like a sitting duck," one stranded American told CNN Tuesday morning (today AEDT).

"There is widespread frustration," said an American currently in the United Arab Emirates who is trying to leave.

"Do we head to the airport? Do we stay sheltered in place?

"The directive of the US government is completely unclear. They are saying to shelter in place but also to seek out commercial flights and there was no advance warning that a war was about to break out."

"It would be hilarious if it wasn't so frustrating," said Forrest Harrington, a US citizen stuck in Dubai.

He was only meant to have a layover there on Saturday and was returning to the US from a work trip in India.

"The Department of State tells me to evacuate, but there's no way to do so," said Katie, an American stuck in Bahrain.

"I cannot believe there is no support for US citizens stranded when the US started this conflict."

**ASX falls by more than 1 per cent (23:31)**

The Australian sharemarket has slumped 1.4 per cent today amid fears about the spiralling Middle East conflict that sent the oil price rocketing.

In early trading, the S&P/ASX 200 fell 129.80 points to 9847.50, the *Australian Financial Review* reports.

Wall Street fell by two per cent overnight.

**Players flee court at pro tennis tournament (23:28)**

Professional tennis players have sprinted from the courts at an ATP Challenger event in the UAE after play was suddenly stopped amid a security alert sparked by the conflict.

Footage posted online showed Belarus player Daniil Ostapenkov and Japan's Hayato Matsuoka running from the court midway through their qualifying match in the city of Fujairah.

Officials later cancelled the tournament.

**Flight to Sydney departs Dubai (22:52)**

A flight from Dubai to Sydney carrying Australians from the Middle East has just taken off from Dubai International Airport.

The Emirates aircraft is now airborne, according to flight tracking site flightradar24.

It is the first Australia-bound flight to leave the UAE since commercial airspaces were largely closed after the US and Israel launched strikes on Iran.

The flight is due to land at Sydney Airport at 10.30pm.

**Iran was no imminent nuclear threat, says UN watchdog chief (22:38)**

Iran was not days or weeks away from having a nuclear weapon, the head of the UN nuclear watchdog, Rafael Grossi, has told CNN.

His remarks contradict a US claim that Iran posed an imminent nuclear threat.

Quizzed on CNN today if "the Iranians were days or weeks away from building a bomb," Rafael Grossi said: "No."

He said US strikes on Iran last June inflicted lasting damage to the nation's nuclear program,

"We never had information indicating that there was a structured, systematic program to build or to construct a nuclear weapon," Grossi said.

**France sending aircraft carrier to Middle East (22:25)**

French President Emmanuel Macron has said he ordered the aircraft carrier *Charles de Gaulle* to move from the Baltic Sea to the Mediterranean. He said it will be escorted by its air wing and frigates.

In a pre-recorded speech on French TV, Macron added that Rafale fighter jets, air-defence systems, and airborne radar systems have been deployed over the past few hours in the Middle East.

Britain is sending a warship and helicopters to Cyprus after a drone hit a UK base on the Mediterranean island.

UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer said the government is deploying helicopters with counter-drone capabilities and the air-defence destroyer HMS Dragon to the region.

It comes after an Iran-made drone hit RAF Akrotiri base over the weekend, causing minor damage and no injuries.

**Satellite photos show wrecked presidential compound (21:54)**

Before-and-after images published by US-based satellite company Vantor show the domed roof of Iran's presidential complex destroyed, seeming to confirm Israel's earlier claims of a strike.

Numerous munitions were dropped on what Israel's military said was among the most heavily secured sites in Tehran.

Iranian officials and state media have not yet acknowledged the damage or reported casualties from the strike.
