# For the Iran war, US public expects allies like Australia to send troops, new poll reveals

**Date:** 13 March 2026
**Source:** Australian Financial Review
**URL:** https://www.afr.com/world/north-america/trump-may-ask-allies-for-help-in-iran-voters-say-that-s-a-good-move-20260313-p5oa2d

---

## tl;dr

YouGov poll of 1,000 US voters (conducted for Bondi Partners, an Australian strategic advisory firm): 44% of Americans say allies who publicly back the Iran military operation should also send their own troops. Two-thirds of Republican voters agree. Only 19% disagree. This directly contradicts the Albanese government's position that Australia can verbally endorse the war while limiting its involvement to "defensive" measures. It puts the Australian government on a collision course with Washington -- which has already been pressuring allies to contribute more, and where Trump is now deploying 5,000 extra Marines and openly floating whether allies should contribute ground troops.

---

## Key findings

| Group | "Allies who back the war should send troops" |
|-------|----------------------------------------------|
| All Americans | 44% agree / 19% disagree / 27% neutral / 10% unsure |
| Republican voters | ~66% agree |

**What this means for Australia:**

Australia publicly backed the war on Day 1 -- PM Albanese, FM Wong, and Defence Minister Marles all issued a joint statement supporting the strikes. Under the view held by 44% of Americans (and two-thirds of Republican voters), that means Australia should be contributing troops.

The Albanese government has been trying to navigate between Washington and Australian public opinion, which is deeply opposed to sending ground troops. But this poll shows that the "diplomatic endorsement without military commitment" position Australia is holding is politically unsustainable in Washington.

**Background:** Trump may formally ask allied nations to contribute to the war effort. If he does, Australia's position -- "we support the US, we've sent a Wedgetail and some missiles, but no ground troops" -- may come under direct pressure.

Jessica Gardner (AFR Washington correspondent): the poll "raises tough questions about how the Albanese government manages the US relationship during the war in Iran."
