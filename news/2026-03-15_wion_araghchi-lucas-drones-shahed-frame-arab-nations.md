## Full story

Iranian Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi has denied that Iran is responsible for strikes on civilian and residential areas in regional countries. He said Iran's military operations are strictly limited to US bases and interests—legitimate defensive responses—and suggested the US and Israel are conducting attacks on civilian targets in Arab countries to incite instability and damage diplomatic ties between Tehran and its neighbours.

Araghchi claimed the US has developed a drone system called "LUCAS" that is being used to attack targets in Arab countries while deceptively casting blame on Iran. He said the LUCAS drone is designed to be nearly identical to Iran's Shahed series so regional states cannot distinguish between them. He proposed a joint fact-finding committee with regional nations to investigate the attacks and determine responsibility.

LUCAS (Low-cost Uncrewed Combat Attack System) is a US one-way attack drone; US officials have acknowledged it was reverse-engineered from captured Iranian Shahed-136 drones. It costs roughly $35,000 per unit and was used for the first time in combat in the current war with Iran; Centcom chief Admiral Brad Cooper described it as a Shahed "made better" and "fired right back at Iran." The US has branded the Iranian framing claim a "lie"; Centcom stated Iran has launched "thousands of drones AND missiles" at its neighbours and that US attack drones only target Iranian military capabilities.
