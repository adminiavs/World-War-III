---

## tl;dr

Opinion piece from Tasmania's major daily newspaper. The author (Wooley) captures the public mood in Australia as the country's military involvement escalates -- "that sinking feeling we're at war again." Draws on the pattern of past conflicts (Iraq, Afghanistan) and the sense that Australia is being drawn in once more without a genuine national debate or parliamentary vote. Tasmania, with its strong anti-war tradition, serves as a useful barometer of mainstream public discomfort with the deployment. The title itself reflects a widely shared feeling among Australians who have watched the country's involvement escalate step by step.

---

## Context

The Mercury is Tasmania's dominant newspaper and reflects the views of regional and rural Australia more than the metro press. The fact that this "sinking feeling" opinion is being published in Hobart -- a city whose diesel ran dry before the war even started (20 February) -- reflects the geographic reach of the war's impact. Tasmania is a long way from Tehran. But it is not insulated from what is happening.

The piece reflects the trajectory described by Guardian columnist Zoe Daniel ("already mission creep, without an exit strategy") and the concerns raised by Greens senator David Shoebridge -- that Australia has been signed into the war "by deception and stealth," step by step, without a parliamentary vote, without public consent, and without an exit strategy.
