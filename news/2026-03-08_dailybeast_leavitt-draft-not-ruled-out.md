## Full story

Karoline Leavitt did not dismiss the notion that President Donald Trump could reinstate the draft for his war on Iran. Speaking with Fox News host Maria Bartiromo on Sunday, the White House press secretary said that mandatory conscription for military service was not "off of the table."

Bartiromo said: "Mothers out there are worried that we're going to have a draft, that they're going to see their sons and daughters get involved in this. What do you want to say about the president's plans for troops on the ground?"

Leavitt responded: "President Trump wisely does not remove options off of the table. I know a lot of politicians like to do that quickly. But the president, as commander-in-chief, wants to continue to assess the success of the military operation. It's not part of the current plan right now, but the president, again, wisely keeps his options on the table."

A military draft has not happened in the United States since the Vietnam War, though men aged 18 to 25 are required to register with the Selective Service System.

Leavitt's statements quickly drew criticism. Marjorie Taylor Greene: "How about the answer is NO DRAFT AND NO BOOTS ON THE GROUND because we campaigned on NO MORE FOREIGN WARS OR REGIME CHANGE! Liars every single one of them! Not my son, over my dead body!" Greene added: "Send Lindsey Graham, Mark Levin, and Laura Loomer and ALL the murderous blood thirsty maniacs that support this America LAST WAR."

Progressive group Call to Activism: "Translation: they won't even promise your kids aren't next." Geopolitical commentator Mats Nilsson: "Once the Congressman's and Ivy League parent's children get drafted it'll be a whole other ballgame."

Eight American military personnel have died since strikes began Feb 28. Two additional service members died beyond the six killed in the March 1 Kuwait drone strike.

Trump said on March 1: "There will likely be more. That's the way it is."
