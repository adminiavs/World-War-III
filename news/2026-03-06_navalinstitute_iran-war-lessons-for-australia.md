## Full story

The United States' and Israel's military operations against Iran highlight the importance of the ADF's long-range strike and power projection and reinforce the need for nuclear-powered conventionally armed submarines and an expanded surface fleet. Both are being addressed (AUKUS; 2024 Fleet Review outcome of 26 ships; August 2025 decision to acquire 11 upgraded Japanese Mogami-class frigates). The campaign in Iran illustrates the importance of traditional hard power: carrier strike groups applying naval air power and long-range land attack missiles against Iranian targets while staying beyond Iran's reach. Australia is not likely to invest in carriers and Middle East geography differs from Australia's primary interest, but the ability of surface ships to conduct precision strike is essential for the strategy of denial underpinning the 2023 Defence Strategic Review and 2024 National Defence Strategy. Power projection is now much more relevant. At the ASPI 2025 Defence Conference, CDF Admiral David Johnston said Australia may finally need to be reconsidered as a homeland from which we conduct combat operations, affecting northern infrastructure and supply chains; we might need to operate from this country. That reinforces the importance of northern Australia and that geography no longer protects Australia as China develops power projection (e.g. China's two 2025 naval deployments: Type 055 Renhai circumnavigation and Type 075 Yushen amphibious, signalling blue-water operations and threat to sea lanes). In confronting a challenge like China, the RAN and ADF should project power forward from the sea-air gap; the gap should be seen as a main rear area from which to project across multi-domain operations and deny an adversary the chance to project against Australia. As US naval forces project power against Iran, the RAN with nuclear submarines as the tip of the spear, supported from air, space, cyberspace and littoral, should project effect decisively before a threat is employed against Australia: shoot the archer before he releases the arrow. That demands power projection of the type, if not the magnitude, arrayed against Iran. Work remains on how different capabilities contribute; the ADF must aspire to long-range air power and precision strike, advanced autonomous systems, and resilient C2ISR. Sea power alone is not sufficient, but a larger and more capable navy (advanced surface combatants and nuclear submarines) allows sustained forward presence vital for deterrence by denial. The region is maritime and archipelagic; the forces Australia is developing will allow impactful projection along island chains, defence of sea lanes, and working with allies to constrain China's coercive power projection. Malcolm Davis, senior analyst, ASPI Defence Strategy program. ANI / The Strategist, 6 Mar 2026.
