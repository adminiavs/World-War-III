## Full article

Since Israel and the United States joined forces in a war against Iran on February 28, the region's Christians -- already a shrinking minority -- have been caught in the crossfire.

**Southern Lebanon:** On March 9, an Israeli tank strike in the Christian village of Qlayaa killed Maronite Father Pierre al-Rahi. He had refused IDF evacuation orders and was tending to wounded from an earlier strike when the IDF struck again. Father al-Rahi the day before: "We carry nothing but kindness, goodness, love and prayer. We chose to stay under danger because these are our homes. We won't leave them for anyone who wants to come in and use them and occupy them."

Many Lebanese Christians blamed both Israel and Hezbollah.

**Pope Leo** expressed his "profound sorrow" for victims of the Middle East attacks, "for the many innocent people, including numerous children, and for those who were helping them, such as Father Pierre al-Rahi."

**Israel:** Auxiliary Bishop Rafic Nahra (Nazareth): "We are not separate. We feel the effects of war like everyone." Many Christian families have emigrated from Israel. A Catholic Filipina caregiver was killed in an Iranian air strike.

**West Bank/Jerusalem:** Yousef Barakat (Notre Dame of Jerusalem Center): "Every two or three years we have a crisis." He has told his son in Texas not to return. "In the West Bank, I'd say 70-80% of the Christians are thinking of leaving." Unless the situation improves, "in a few decades, there could be no more than a few Christian families here."
