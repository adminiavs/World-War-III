## Full story

Donald Trump said only Iran's unconditional surrender will bring an end to the offensive launched seven days ago, as the US and Israel carried out some of the heaviest bombardments so far. There will be no deal with Iran except UNCONDITIONAL SURRENDER, Trump wrote on Truth Social on Friday, when US strategic bombers were in action and intensive Israeli strikes in Lebanon forced more than a million to flee. Israeli and US officials threatened further escalation as Iran retaliated with more attacks across the Middle East. Pete Hegseth said US firepower was about to surge dramatically. Eyal Zamir (IDF chief of staff) said Israel was moving to a new phase that would further dismantle the regime and its military capabilities; we have additional surprises ahead which I do not intend to disclose. Tehran launched missiles and drones at Kuwait, Qatar, Saudi Arabia and Bahrain (US bases and civilian infrastructure including oil pipelines). Fewer missiles at Israel than in first days. IRGC said it had fired missiles toward Tel Aviv and promised new initiatives and weapons. Witnesses: latest airstrikes in Iran particularly intense; explosions around Kermanshah (missile bases). Internet coverage in Iran reduced to about 1% (NetBlocks). In Lebanon, Israeli army issued largest most sweeping displacement orders; Dahiyeh (600,000+) urged to evacuate; by Friday area ghost town, rubble and fires. Red Cross: many fled on foot with nothing but clothes, no clear sense where to go. UN rights office: devastating impact before our eyes; demand swift investigations into fatal Israeli strikes Lebanon to determine if they violate international law; blanket displacement orders risk prohibited forced displacement. Lebanon health ministry: 217 killed, 798 wounded. Hegseth pushed back on criticism, denying US and Israel caused regional instability. Brad Cooper: B-2 stealth bombers dropped dozens of 2,000lb penetrator bombs on deeply buried ballistic missile launchers; large Iranian naval vessel used as drone launch platform hit and possibly sunk. Reuters: US military investigators believe US likely responsible for strike on girls school (100+ students). Volker Turk (UN rights chief): world urgently needs steps to contain and extinguish this blaze; instead more inflammatory rhetoric, more bombings, more destruction. Guardian 6 Mar 2026, Jason Burke.
