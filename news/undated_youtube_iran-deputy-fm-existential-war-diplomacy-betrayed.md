## Full story

Dr. Seyed Katib Zadeh (Deputy Foreign Minister of Iran and President of the Institute of Political and International Studies) spoke at an event (Heat; moderator Dr. Rushna). He framed the conflict as an existential war: the US and Israel want to put an end to the existence of Iran; Iran is fighting a heroic nationalistic war to push back the aggressors and has no option. He said the administration does not understand the essence of diplomacy, dialogue and compromise (give and take, not dictate), and that President Trump is demanding a change in Iran's leadership and a voice in that while he cannot appoint even the mayor of New York.

What changed this time: A week before the attack Iran was in Geneva with the mediation of the Omani foreign minister and the presence of the IAEA head (Grossi). They nailed down a practical agreement; the Omani foreign minister said an agreement was within reach and everything was ready. They identified Monday to go to Vienna and write it down. They left the room; the American negotiator said it was very promising and they would hand over their written proposal on Monday. They arrived in Tehran and the aggression started. So they invited diplomacy while building up militarily and then attacked; there is no real understanding of why they started. His view: power politics, greater Israel, and the Israeli prime minister's desire to impose hegemony and change the Middle East forever; they are carpet bombing Iran out of frustration. They betrayed diplomacy for the second time.

Mediation: Traditional mediators (Oman, Qatar) and improving relations with Saudi Arabia and Turkey had brought things to the same page; then the Americans and Israelis started the invasion and sabotaged it. In the first minute of the attack the Americans intentionally bombed a primary school and killed around 200 small children (girls, ages 7 to 11). So we were on the same page when they started this atrocity.

US presence: America has decided to put an end to Iran's existence; Israel has promised this for decades (greater Israel). Iran's response is to put an end to the presence of the United States in the area around Iran. They have multiple bases, assets, long-range radars, command and control, the Fifth Fleet in Bahrain (75% of US marine activities there), all targeting Iran. Iran is only hitting US bases and US assets and those sheltered in residential areas; there has been no Arab neighbour casualty from Iranian attacks. Two American soldiers were killed by Iran when operating from a residential area.

Regime narrative: The term regime is used to demonize and delegitimize Iran. If it were just a regime, assassinating the head of state would have caused collapse. Iran is institutionalized (constitution, division of powers). They unlawfully assassinated the standing leader but the country continues. He rejected the claim they are fighting for the people of Iran; they are carpet bombing and taking revenge for the 1979 revolution. In January they sent thousands of internet terminals and terror cells to misuse peaceful protests; now millions are rallying in Iran because of the invasion. He cited the US Secretary of War (Hegseth) saying there is no rule of engagement, they do not want to be politically correct, and no stupid international organization norms would apply; they just want to win the war.

Expanding scope: When the US attacks from those countries (e.g. 5,000 km radar in Doha, Fifth Fleet in Bahrain), those are not for fishing; they are launching from the surrounding area. Iran has no option but to hit wherever the attacks originate. So it is an existential war; the equilibrium is they want to end Iran's existence and Iran's response is to end their presence in the region. If they wanted coexistence the rules would be different. He said regional countries know this is Israel's war; they dragged America in; from Saudi to Oman everyone knows and is upset. Iran is trying to avoid spillover; he cited false flag operations by Mossad (refineries, Cyprus); UK PM said Cyprus did not originate from Iran; Tucker Carlson on Mossad groups captured in Saudi Arabia and Qatar for false flags.

Strait of Hormuz: Iran has not closed the Strait. If they close it they will announce it. Their declarations are precise and honest. The main cause of no tankers is insurance companies stopping coverage, not an Iranian closure. They have said no US/Israeli flag or those helping them may pass; they have not closed it until further notice and are an anchor of stability. Tanker attacks: one or two incidents; one involved a flag not permitted; others false flag (Aramco, Cyprus); they are investigating and have shared information with those who asked.

Geopolitical realignment: Two parallel paths. One is the Israeli path of maximum pressure and greater Israel, with America joining to change Iran's political structure. The other is inclusion: parallel to the Geneva nuclear talks (which involved only two US attendees, Steve Vitkov and Jared Kushner, no nuclear experts), there was a separate unannounced track between Iran, Oman, Qatar and other Persian Gulf states for an inclusive regional dialogue. Americans and Israelis spoiled it; he believes Israelis knew it was happening and rushed to drag everyone into war. Oman and the GCC know; the moment the atrocity stops is the moment for the region to move to a new security and inclusive arrangement.

Way out: Iran has no strategy or goal in this war except to resist. The other side started the aggression; the ball is in their court. If they stop the aggression today, Iran is defending not attacking. Iran is Persia, a country of chess not American football; they are not bullying. Diplomacy down the road is the only option for every country. He doubts this administration understands the essence of diplomacy and compromise (give and take, not dictate). Thank you to Dr. Katib Zadeh; conversation ended on that note. YouTube, 2026.
