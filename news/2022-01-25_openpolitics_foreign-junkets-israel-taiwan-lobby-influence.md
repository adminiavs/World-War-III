ASPI, which receives funding from foreign governments and arms manufacturers, also funded Tim Watts' trip to Taiwan and Andrew Hastie's trip to Israel. The Henry Jackson Society (a neoconservative think tank supporting interventionist foreign policy like the Iraq invasion) funded Hastie's London trip. Key detail: BESA Center, the Israeli think tank that co-hosted with ASPI, published a paper opposing the Obama administration's efforts to destroy ISIS, arguing a weakened but functioning ISIS would undermine Iran. US government trips were modest by comparison. Parliamentary delegations do not need to be disclosed, meaning the data significantly understates actual overseas travel. Richard Marles (now Deputy PM and Defence Minister) received travel from the International Institute for Strategic Leadership Dialogue for the Israel trip.

## Full story

If you want a free overseas holiday it pays to be an elected official. But it may not be costless.

At least 39 federal MPs and senators, one in six parliamentarians, have enjoyed junkets since the last election, with Taiwan and Israel the most popular destinations based on an analysis of the interest registers.

**Taiwan trips (2019-2020):**

Eleven visited Taiwan in 2019-20 as guests of the national government, which paid for international flights, ground transfers, meals, drinks, and accommodation. Taiwanese taxpayers even stumped up for two MPs to bring their partners. Recipients included Eric Abetz, Slade Brockman, David Fawcett, Steve Georganas, Chris Hayes (with spouse), Ted O'Brien, Graham Perrett, Tony Sheldon, David Smith (with spouse), Maria Vamvakinou, and Rick Wilson.

The Australian Strategic Policy Institute (ASPI), which receives funding from foreign governments and arms manufacturers, paid for Tim Watts, the Shadow Assistant Minister for Cyber Security, to attend the Australia-Taiwan Cyber and Technology Dialogue in late 2019.

Senator Eric Abetz, following his 2019 visit, called for Australia to defend Taiwan in the event of a war with China. Ted O'Brien pushed the Morrison government to pursue a free trade agreement with Taiwan and accused the CCP of trying to divide Australian business and the federal government.

**Israel trips (late 2019):**

Eleven MPs and senators visited Israel in late 2019:

- AIJAC funded trips for 6 Liberals: Eric Abetz, Katie Allen, Alex Antic, Andrew Hastie (via Rambam Israel Fellowship), David Van, Bert van Manen.
- International Institute for Strategic Leadership Dialogue: Kimberley Kitching, Richard Marles, James Stevens.
- Israel Allies Foundation: Andrew Wallace.
- ASPI: Andrew Hastie (ASPI-BESA 2019 Beersheba Dialogue in Israel). Note: BESA Center, a strategic studies think tank based in Tel Aviv, published a paper opposing the Obama administration's efforts to destroy Islamic State (ISIS), arguing that a weakened but functioning ISIS would undermine Iran's ambitions to be a regional hegemon. During Trump's presidency BESA published glowing articles on his foreign policy.

**Other notable trips:**

- Henry Jackson Society (HJS), a neoconservative think tank that supports interventionist foreign policy like the illegal invasion of Iraq, funded Andrew Hastie's business class flights to London in 2019. HJS was denounced by its own co-founder Matthew Jamison for demonising Muslims and running an anti-Chinese propaganda campaign.
- Kimberley Kitching received hospitality from Hermitage Capital Management (Bill Browder's firm) in London in 2021 for receiving a Magnitsky award. In her acceptance speech she thanked her "Wolverine" buddies Andrew Hastie and Senator James Paterson.
- Coalition for Conservation hosted three MPs at COP26 in Glasgow.
- US International Visitors Leadership Program covered only domestic US travel for five MPs/senators.
- Australian American Leadership Dialogue hosted Andrew Hastie and Anthony Albanese in Honolulu; Qantas upgraded Albanese to business class for the return flight.

**Key structural point:** Parliamentary delegations do not need to be disclosed because they fall outside the definition of "sponsored travel." This means the data significantly understates the true extent of overseas travel. Ministerial travel is excluded for the same reason.
