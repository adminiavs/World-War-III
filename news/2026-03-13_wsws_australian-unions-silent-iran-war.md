# Australian unions silent on Labor-backed US-Israel war on Iran

**Date:** 13 March 2026
**Source:** World Socialist Web Site (WSWS)
**URL:** https://www.wsws.org/en/articles/2026/03/13/behj-m13.html

---

## tl;dr

The ACTU and virtually every Australian union have not acknowledged the war, let alone opposed it. By Day 14: at least 1,255 dead including 200 children, 12,000+ wounded. Three Australian military personnel were on the US submarine that sank the Iranian frigate IRIS Dena (140 people killed; 87 bodies recovered, 32 rescued). The Albanese government has committed aircraft, missiles, and troops to the Gulf. Only two unions -- the Maritime Union of Australia (MUA) and the Australian Manufacturing Workers Union (AMWU) -- have issued any statement, and both are criticised here as inadequate and contradictory. The WSWS calls for rank-and-file committees independent of the union bureaucracy and the Labor Party, and frames the silence as a deliberate act of class betrayal.

---

## Perspective note

The WSWS is the publication of the Socialist Equality Party (International Committee of the Fourth International). It applies a Marxist, class-based analysis. Its perspective is explicitly anti-war, anti-Labor, and hostile to the trade union bureaucracy. It is archived here not as a neutral source but because it provides the most detailed documentation of the union response (or lack thereof) to the war, and because it raises questions about Australian labour movement silence that mainstream outlets have not covered.

---

## Key facts documented

- **ACTU:** No statement on the Iran war.
- **Most Australian unions:** No acknowledgment of the war.
- **MUA (Maritime Union of Australia):** Statement on March 3 "opposing" the war but not naming Labor; calling on government to "condemn this war" (which the government is actively fighting); since news of Australian involvement in IRIS Dena sinking broke, MUA has shifted language to "escalating geopolitical tensions"; now calling for a "Maritime Strategic Fleet" (essentially a government-subsidised scheme for major shipping companies)
- **AMWU (Australian Manufacturing Workers Union):** "We will not allow our labour to be weaponised for wars of aggression" -- but no call for industrial action; instead, appeals to the government to "use every diplomatic lever"
- **No strikes called.** No shipments blocked. ZIM (Israeli shipping line) is still being loaded and unloaded by MUA members every week.
- **Australia's direct involvement:** Three Australian military personnel were on the US submarine that sank IRIS Dena off Sri Lanka. The WSWS describes this as "concrete expression" of Labor complicity.
- **Australia's deployment:** Government announced aircraft, missiles, and troops to the Gulf (March 10). WSWS: "Further confirmation of Labor's complicity in this illegal war."

## Key quote

"The silence of the Australian trade union bureaucracy on the US-led criminal war against Iran, almost two weeks on, is deafening. At least 1,255 people, including 200 children, are dead and in excess of 12,000 are wounded, as the US intensifies its attacks, vowing to obliterate a country of more than 90 million people."

"The Australian ruling elite, with the federal Labor government as its spearhead, is fully committed to the drive of US imperialism to restore its hegemony. This was concretely expressed in the presence of three Australian military personnel on the US submarine that sank unarmed Iranian frigate IRIS Dena, killing at least 140 people."

"Not a single union has opposed the Australian state's role in the carnage, let alone called for industrial action to halt the supply of war materials."
