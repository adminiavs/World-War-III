## Key Points

### Internet blackout
- **NetBlocks:** Iran connectivity at just **1% of ordinary levels**
- Prepaid phone cards to call abroad selling for ~$50, last only 5-7 minutes
- Difficult to coordinate rescue efforts, reach loved ones, get information out

### Regime threats
- **Mass text messages** warning people not to protest
- Anyone who comes to street labeled as "Israeli soldiers" and "direct target"
- **MP Salar Velayatmadar (former IRGC commander):** Warning to anyone watching Iran International or speaking "a word that aligns with the enemy" — **"Your death sentence has been issued"**

### Starlink in Iran
- Estimated **~50,000 Starlink terminals** in Iran
- "According to the Iranian law, having a Starlink is equivalent to running espionage operation for Israel"
- Terminals being moved around for security; people turn them on/off
- Played crucial role getting videos out during January crackdown

### On the ground
- Checkpoints everywhere with military vehicles
- Families crossing Turkey border to be with relatives
- Some crossing back INTO Iran because they can't reach family otherwise
- Only independent news: Iran International (London-based, Farsi, changes signals to prevent jamming)

### Amir Rashidi (Miaan Group):
> "They want to project power... They want to protect itself from cyberattacks [and] control the narrative internally."
