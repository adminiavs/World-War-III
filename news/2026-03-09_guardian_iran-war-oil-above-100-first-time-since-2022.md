# Iran war drives oil prices above 100 a barrel for first time since 2022

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Source:** The Guardian (Business)  
**URL:** https://www.theguardian.com/business/2026/mar/09/iran-war-drives-oil-price-above-100-a-barrel-for-first-time-since-2022  
**Author:** Jillian Ambrose (Energy correspondent)  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** Global  
**Tags:** oil-prices, brent, wti, 100-dollar, hormuz, trump, qatar-150, kuwait, supply-deficit

## tl;dr

Global oil surged past $100 a barrel for the first time since 2022 as Middle East conflict wipes 20m barrels from the market each day. Brent jumped 16.6% to $108.10; WTI rose 19.6% to $108.72. Trump on social media: surge is a very small price to pay for U.S.A., and World, Safety and Peace; short term; will drop rapidly when destruction of Iran nuclear threat is over. Kuwait national oil company announced precautionary cut. Qatar energy minister predicted if war continued unabated all Gulf exporters would shut production within weeks and oil would rise to $150. Oil storage in Saudi, UAE, Kuwait near limits; major oilfields may need to shut if crude cannot be exported via Hormuz. Hundreds of tankers halted after IRGC threatened to set ablaze any vessel; strait carries a fifth of world oil and LNG. CSIS Seigle: 20m barrel/day deficit with no sign of relief; Trump demand for unconditional surrender very unlikely; disregard for oil prices is not a bluff. White House countermeasures (Red Sea reroute, emergency reserves, shipping insurance) would not offset loss of 20m b/d. Prices up two-thirds from start of year (from just above $60).
