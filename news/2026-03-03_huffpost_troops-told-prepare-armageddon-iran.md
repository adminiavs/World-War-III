## Full story

For some U.S. military commanders, the emerging war in Iran is part of a biblical plan to bring about the end of the world as we know it, according to complaints filed by over 100 service members.

The Military Religious Freedom Foundation has received a litany of complaints about religious ideology seeping into military orders since the U.S. and Israel began bombing Iran, independent journalist Jon Larsen first reported.

Mikey Weinstein, founder and president of MRFF, said the organisation started getting calls in the wee hours of Saturday morning from people saying their commanders were "jubilant" and telling people "Don't worry, it's all part of God's plan." Weinstein said the "metric promised" in the Bible's Book of Revelation is horrifying: "They are promised a 200-mile-long river that is four-and-a-half feet deep filled with nothing but the blood that their weaponized version of Jesus will spill at the Battle of Armageddon."

One non-commissioned officer filed a complaint on behalf of himself and 15 other troops. The NCO reported that a commander told them to tell fellow troops that the war in Iran was "all part of God's divine plan," citing the Book of Revelation and Armageddon and the "imminent" return of Jesus Christ. The commander allegedly said President Donald Trump "has been anointed by Jesus to light the signal fire in Iran to cause Armageddon and mark his return to Earth." The complaint said the messaging was "destroying morale and unit cohesion" and that commanders were flagrantly violating their oaths to uphold the Constitution. Service members also reported being invited to Bible studies at commanders' homes to "discuss how this was all part of the plan and it's all being lived out in the Book of Revelation and Christian eschatology."

Weinstein noted that under the military criminal code, insubordination is a felony, so subordinates cannot easily push back when proselytised by a superior. Harrison Mann, a 13-year Army veteran now with Win Without War, said the politicisation of the military makes it harder for soldiers to speak out and may give subordinate leaders "tacit approval to start imposing their own religious beliefs on others." He urged public support for service members who speak up and pressure on Congress to hold the administration accountable.
