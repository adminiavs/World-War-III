## Full story

Israel has called on Australia to "wake up" and join the war in Iran as it enters its sixth day and hostilities spill across the Middle East.

Israeli Deputy Foreign Minister Sharren Haskel told 7NEWS it's time for Australia to participate in the strikes launched by the US and Israel on Iran. "The war is now escalating on all fronts. So my message to Australia is to wake up," she said. "I would expect Australia, with its long history of defending humanity and freedom throughout many wars throughout our history, to actually join forces as well." Haskel insisted for Australians worried about the impact of the war on fuel and gas prices, trade, travel and security that the conflict would be worth it in the long run. "They need to understand what could have come in the future, which would have been far more dangerous, which would have been much more economically costly as well," she said.

Haskel added that if the son of Iran's former Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, Mojtaba Khamenei, were to become his successor, Israel would have "no choice" but to target him as well. Asked "Will Israel assassinate him too?" she said: "Look, anyone who would threaten the annihilation of the state of Israel will be eliminated. We don't have any other choice."

**War widens**

Iran launched its large Khorramshahr-4 missiles in an attack targeting Israel; the Guard said the missiles had a 1-ton warhead and can be multiple warhead; Israel said Iran used cluster munitions. The Guard claimed attacks in Bahrain, Kuwait and the United Arab Emirates. Qatar's Defence Ministry reported a missile attack late Thursday morning. Qatar condemned the attempted Iranian missile attack against Turkey and the drone attack on an airport in Azerbaijan, calling them "dangerous, aggressive escalation and a blatant violation to the nations' sovereignty." Azerbaijan's Foreign Ministry said Iranian drones hit Nakhchivan International Airport; Azerbaijan said it was "preparing the necessary response." Iran's general staff denied launching a drone toward Azerbaijan. The UAE defence ministry said it repelled six missiles and 131 drones on Thursday and hundreds since the start of the war.

In Iran, the IDF said it carried out airstrikes overnight against targets in Tehran including ballistic missile infrastructure, underground storage, launch sites and long-range launchers. Israel also called all residents of Beirut's southern suburbs to evacuate "immediately" to "save your lives and evacuate your homes immediately," signalling plans for heavy bombardment. Israel had already issued a blanket evacuation warning for southern Lebanon south of the Litani River.

**Sri Lanka and US submarine**

A Sri Lankan minister revealed another Iranian vessel is in its waters after a US submarine sank a warship near its coast. Government spokesman Nalinda Jayatissa confirmed the presence of the second Iranian ship in parliament but did not provide further details. He said the government was making an "intervention to minimise loss of lives and to safeguard the regional peace." US Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth confirmed an American submarine sank an Iranian warship with a torpedo in international waters, killing at least 87 people and wounding 32 others. The strike is the first such attack on an enemy vessel since World War II, Hegseth said. "The Iranian navy rests at the bottom of the Persian Gulf," he told reporters.
