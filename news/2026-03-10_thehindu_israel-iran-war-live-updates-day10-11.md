## Key developments

### Casualties and damage
- Pentagon: ~140 U.S. service members wounded; 108 returned to duty, 8 severely injured
- Iran UN envoy: ~10,000 civilian sites destroyed (65 schools, 8,000 homes), 1,300+ civilians killed
- Lebanon: 570 killed, 1,444 wounded since March 2; 759,300 displaced
- Four Iranian diplomats killed in March 8 Beirut Ramada strike
- Seven U.S. troops KIA total (Sgt. Benjamin Pennington latest)

### Strait of Hormuz
- U.S. destroyed 16 Iranian mine-laying vessels
- Trump: "TWENTY TIMES HARDER" if Iran stops oil flow
- Navy tells shipping Hormuz escorts "not possible now"
- Iran IRGC: will block oil exports to hostile side until war ends
- Gulf energy official: stopping war is only way to reopen Hormuz
- Aramco CEO: ~80 million barrels disrupted; consequences could be "catastrophic"

### Military operations
- Hegseth: March 10 "most intense day" of U.S. strikes on Iran
- Israel begins new wave of strikes on Tehran
- Iran launches missiles at Israeli cities using Fattah, Emad, Khaibar strategic missiles
- IRGC targeted U.S. base in Iraq's Kurdistan (Al-Harir Air Base)
- UAE Ruwais refinery (world's 4th largest single-site) shut as precaution after drone attack
- Kerman Airport in southern Iran damaged

### Diplomacy
