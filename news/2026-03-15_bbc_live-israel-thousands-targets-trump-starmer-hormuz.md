## Full story

Israel says it still has thousands of targets to hit in Iran; strikes expanded to western and central Iran. Iranian FM Araghchi told CBS Tehran has not asked the US for a ceasefire and does not see reason to talk to Americans after they attacked during negotiations. Trump had said Iran wanted a deal but terms were not good enough. Trump and UK PM Keir Starmer spoke about the importance of reopening the Strait of Hormuz to end disruption to global shipping.

US branded Iranian claims that US/Israel are using LUCAS drones (Shahed lookalikes) to attack Arab nations and blame Iran as a "lie." Centcom stated Iran has launched "thousands of drones AND missiles" at neighbours and US attack drones only target Iranian military capabilities.

Baghdad International Airport hit by five rockets at 19:00 local time; five injured (engineer and four staff); launcher in vehicle seized west of Baghdad. UN peacekeepers fired upon at three bases in Lebanon (Yatar, Dayr Kifa, Qallaqiyah). IDF said it eliminated Muhammad Majid Abdul Salam Tawfiq Zaydan (described as Palestinian "terrorist" for Iran) in Lebanon strike Friday. US-Italian base Ali Al Salem in Kuwait struck by drone; shelter with Italian Task Force Air remotely piloted aircraft destroyed; personnel safe.

Trump economic adviser Kevin Hassett (CBS): US has spent $12bn; "we've got what we need"; energy prices the big problem; "boom in oil production" when Iran stops being disruptive; "global economy big positive shock" when war over; still briefed 4–6 weeks from start, "ahead of schedule." Energy Secretary Chris Wright (ABC/NBC): US knew there would be temporary interruption of Hormuz flows; contingency plans with allies including Saudi positioning 100M+ barrels outside Middle East; strait currently not safe; goal to reopen and bring down gas prices; "Americans will feel it for a few more weeks"; "likely time-frame" for war to end is few weeks.

IEA: oil from 400M barrel emergency release will soon start flowing; immediately in Asia-Oceania, from end March in Americas and Europe; release double the post-Ukraine 2022 record. Oil price nearly a quarter higher than when war began. NetBlocks: key Iranian telecom network AS12880 connectivity collapsed; government near-total internet blackout for 16 days. Fifth member of Iranian women's football team withdrew asylum in Australia (activists/IRNA say captain Zahra Ghanbari); only two of seven who sought sanctuary remain.

Trump threatened to open Hormuz "one way or another" and called on China, France, Japan, South Korea, UK and others to send warships; Araghchi said strait closed only to US and Israeli ships/tankers and Tehran "open to countries who want to talk" about safe passage.
