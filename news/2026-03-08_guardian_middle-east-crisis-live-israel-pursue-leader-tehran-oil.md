## Full story

Live blog coverage from 7-8 March 2026. Key developments:

**Israel and successor threat:** Israel warned it would "pursue every successor" to killed supreme leader Ali Khamenei. The body in charge of selecting a new supreme leader said it had reached a decision, although the name was not immediately announced.

**Tehran strikes:** Overnight US and Israeli strikes hit five oil sites around Tehran; an Iranian official said the five facilities were damaged but fire was brought under control. Plumes of black smoke billowed over the city. The Iranian Red Crescent Society warned the public to avoid toxic acid rain after Israel struck oil storage depots.

**US casualties:** A seventh US military service member died from wounds sustained during Iran's initial counter-attack (attack on US troops in Saudi Arabia, 1 March). US Central Command: "This is the seventh service member killed in action during Operation Epic Fury."

**Lebanon:** Lebanon's health ministry said the number killed from Israeli airstrikes in the past week had increased to 394, including many women and children. An Israeli airstrike hit a hotel in Beirut on Sunday, killing at least four people. Israel said two soldiers were killed in southern Lebanon.

**Iran civilian toll:** Iran's health ministry said US-Israeli airstrikes had killed 200 children and about 200 women since the war started and had damaged critical health infrastructure. Iran's parliament speaker Mohammad Baqer Qalibaf said on Sunday that Tehran is not seeking a ceasefire and that "aggressors" should be punished; if the enemy attacks from any country, Tehran will respond decisively.

**Iranian warship:** The Iranian army said at least 104 people were killed and 32 wounded in a US attack on an Iranian warship off Sri Lanka's coast last week. US Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth had confirmed that a US submarine sank an Iranian naval vessel in international waters. Survivors were transported to hospital in Sri Lanka.

**Gulf:** A wave of Iranian strikes hit Saudi Arabia, the UAE, Qatar, Bahrain and Kuwait. In Bahrain, authorities announced that a desalination plant had been damaged in an Iranian attack. Kuwait reported a huge fire at a tower after drone strikes and cut crude oil production. UAE foreign ministry said it was acting in self-defence "against brutal and unjustified Iranian aggression." Arab League chief said Iran was "reckless" to attack Gulf states. Iran's president Pezeshkian reportedly threatened to increase attacks on American targets; he had earlier apologised to Iran's neighbours and pledged to halt strikes on them unless an attack on Iran originated from their soil.

**Trump:** Donald Trump told ABC News that Iran's new leader "is not going to last long" if Iran does not get his approval first for the theocratic posting. "He's going to have to get approval from us." He would accept someone with ties to the old regime if they were a good leader. On gas prices: "I think it's fine. It's a little glitch." He indicated willingness to send US forces to secure Iran's enriched uranium stockpile (Natanz, Isfahan, Fordow). "Everything is on the table. Everything." He dismissed reports of MAGa base fractures: "It's more popular than ever. It's a very Maga thing what we're doing." Trump took a golf break Sunday morning as airstrikes continued.

**International:** China's foreign minister said the Iran war "should never have happened" and the world cannot return to the law of the jungle. Swiss defence minister Martin Pfister said the US and Israel have violated international law with their attacks on Iran. UK: Prime Minister Keir Starmer spoke to Trump and discussed military co-operation through US use of RAF bases "in support of the collective self-defence of partners"; Britain has given permission for defensive strikes but has not taken part in direct attacks. French president Emmanuel Macron spoke with Iranian president Masoud Pezeshkian; Macron called on Iran to halt strikes against other countries and to end the closure of the Strait of Hormuz; he reiterated the priority of safety and return of French nationals Cécile Kohler and Jacques Paris from the French Embassy.

**UK forces:** British forces successfully engaged a one-way attack drone fired from Iran towards Iraq; RAF Typhoons and F-35 jets continued defensive sorties; a Merlin helicopter is due in theatre.

**Energy and sanctions:** Trump administration officials defended the temporary easing of sanctions on Russian oil (30-day waiver for Indian purchases) to stabilize markets; gasoline national average $3.32/gallon (11% weekly increase), diesel $4.33 (15% spike). Energy secretary Chris Wright and UN ambassador Mike Waltz appeared on Sunday shows.
