## Analysis

The unprecedented number of senior civilian officials living on military bases represents a significant development in American political culture. While individual security concerns are legitimate, the scale raises questions about:

1. **Domestic political violence** — Escalating beyond protests to assassination (Kirk) and credible threats
2. **Blurring civilian-military boundaries** — Military becoming protector of officials from citizens
3. **"Praetorian guard" dynamic** — Military infrastructure shielding political appointees
4. **Cost and accountability** — Some paying rent, others not; taxpayer burden unclear
