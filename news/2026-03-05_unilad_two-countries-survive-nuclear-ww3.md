## Full story

Escalating involvement of nuclear powers has raised the prospect of World War Three. America and Israel are at war with Iran; the Taliban has begun open war with nuclear-armed Pakistan. Peer-reviewed work in Nature suggests a small nuclear exchange could put enough soot in the atmosphere to cause nuclear winter and devastate global agriculture. Annie Jacobsen, author of Nuclear War: A Scenario, drawing on scientific papers and defence experts, told Steven Bartlett's podcast that hundreds of millions would die in the blasts; around three billion might survive but in a world where places like Iowa and Ukraine are under snow for a decade and agriculture fails. Research indicated New Zealand and Australia are the only two countries that could sustain agriculture in such a scenario; ozone damage would make sunlight lethal elsewhere, forcing people to live underground except in those two. Jacobsen noted we can still act to prevent proliferation and war. John Erath, Senior Policy Director at the Center for Arms Control and Non-Proliferation, told Newsweek that no place is truly safe from fallout, contamination and prolonged radiation. UNILAD 5 Mar 2026, William Morgan.
