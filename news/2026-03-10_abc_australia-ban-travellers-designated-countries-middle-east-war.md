# Australia proposes laws to temporarily ban travellers from designated countries amid Middle East war

**Date:** 2026-03-10  
**Source:** ABC News (Australia)  
**URL:** https://www.abc.net.au/news/2026-03-10/new-laws-to-temporarily-ban-travellers-from-certain-countries/106437588  
**Author:** Stephanie Borys  
**Content type:** news  
**Region/country:** Australia  
**Tags:** australia, migration, visa-ban, asylum, middle-east-war, greens-criticism

## tl;dr

Australian government introduced legislation (only 10 days after war began) to temporarily ban some people from designated countries entering Australia on temporary visas for up to six months, citing the Middle East conflict and concerns over visa overstays and asylum claims. Home Affairs Minister Tony Burke can make "arrival control determination" with PM and Foreign Minister support; exemptions for parents of children in Australia, immediate family of citizens, and protection/humanitarian visa holders. Coalition backs in principle; Greens and Asylum Seeker Resource Centre call it "appalling" — "from backing illegal war to shutting door on civilians who have legal right to enter." Government has not named which countries.
