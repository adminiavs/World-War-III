## Full article

The Mirror can reveal that US special forces warplanes have been deployed from the UK to the Middle East alongside the wider Marine deployment.

**The aircraft:** Nine MC-130J Commando II planes recently landed at RAF Mildenhall in Suffolk. Four have since taken off for the Middle East. The MC-130J is a special operations variant of the C-130 transport -- faster, with higher flight capabilities and increased fuel efficiency. It can land on desert strips packed with troops and weaponry and is used for clandestine troop insertion.

**Why the UK matters:** Mildenhall hosts the only United States Air Force Special Operations Command presence in Europe. An intelligence analysis seen by the Mirror says:

- "Operations supported from this location have the potential to place the United Kingdom in an enabling role within wider contingency operations directed at Iran."
- The planes could be used for "reconnaissance of Iranian coastal infrastructure, assist with targeting maritime threats associated with the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps, support partner forces in the region or prepare the operational environment for potential escalation."
- "These missions are particularly sensitive because they are often conducted in a deniable manner, allowing governments to avoid formal acknowledgement of their involvement while still enabling strategic outcomes."
- "Iranian authorities may perceive British territory as indirectly facilitating hostile activity... Such perceptions increase the likelihood that Iran may respond through asymmetric retaliation targeting British interests."

This comes just days after British troops came under fire at their base in Erbil, northern Iraq.

**Kharg Island:** Sources suggest the US could seize Iran's Kharg Island (16 miles off the mainland), which would help free up Iran's grip on the Strait of Hormuz.

**Kurdish proxy option:** Reports indicate the US discussed special forces embedding within a large force of Kurdish troops entering Iran. Problem: Kurdish groups within Iran are not unified and may not be capable of unseating the regime.

**IRGC assessment (from intelligence document):** "The IRGC's operational philosophy emphasises indirect confrontation, allowing Iran to challenge stronger adversaries without exposing itself to the risks of large-scale conventional warfare. Within the Strait of Hormuz, the IRGC naval forces represent the most immediate maritime threat to commercial shipping. Their use of swarm tactics, small missile boats, drones and naval mines allows them to threaten large vessels and naval task groups with relatively limited resources."
