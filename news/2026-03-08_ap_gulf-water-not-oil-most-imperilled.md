# The Gulf resource most imperiled by war may be water, not oil

**Date:** 2026-03-08  
**Source:** AP News

## tl;dr

As missiles and drones hit energy and water infrastructure, analysts warn water may be more at risk than oil in the Gulf. Bahrain accused Iran of damaging a desalination plant; Iran said a US strike damaged its plant on Qeshm, cutting supply to 30 villages. Most Gulf drinking water comes from desalination (Kuwait ~90%, Oman ~86%, Saudi ~70%); hundreds of plants line the coast. 2010 CIA analysis: attacks on desalination could trigger national crises; 2008 cable warned Riyadh could "evacuate within a week" if Jubail plant or pipelines were seriously damaged. Iran racing to expand desalination but constrained by sanctions and cost. International humanitarian law prohibits targeting water indispensable to survival.
