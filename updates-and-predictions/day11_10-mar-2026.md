# Day 11 -- 10 March 2026

## Daily Update

- **Hegseth: "most intense day" of strikes since war began.** US and Israeli forces hit Iran with their heaviest bombardment yet. IDF launched new wave on Tehran targeting "key infrastructure of the Iranian terror regime." Iran returned fire with Fattah, Emad, and Khaibar strategic missiles at Tel Aviv and US targets. Iran parliamentary speaker Qalibaf: "eye for an eye — if they start a war on infrastructure, we will undoubtedly target infrastructure." IRGC: "it is we who will determine the end of the war." Netanyahu: "not done yet... we are breaking their bones." (ABC, Xinhua, The Hindu)
- **US destroys 16 Iranian mine-laying vessels near Hormuz.** Trump initially claimed 10 "inactive" vessels; US CENTCOM updated to 16. Trump: "I am pleased to report we have hit, and completely destroyed, 10 inactive mine laying boats and/or ships, with more to follow!" Warned "TWENTY TIMES HARDER" if Iran stops oil flow. IRGC Navy Commander Tangsiri: will use missiles and submarines to stop US fleet. Gulf energy official: "stopping the war is the only option" to reopen Hormuz. (The Hindu, AP)
- **Navy tells shipping industry Hormuz escorts "not possible now."** Despite Trump's earlier offer of convoy escorts, the Navy informed the shipping industry they cannot execute this. Energy Secretary Chris Wright posted — then deleted — a claim that the Navy had escorted a tanker; Leavitt confirmed it had not. Iran Revolutionary Guards called Wright's post a "complete lie." ~1,000+ vessels waiting to transit. (Reuters, ABC, CBS)
- **NIC pre-war assessment leaked: military intervention would not cause regime change.** AP: National Intelligence Council assessment, completed shortly before the Feb 28 strike, concluded neither limited airstrikes nor a prolonged campaign was likely to produce leadership change — even if current leadership was killed. No unified opposition coalition existed to take over. Mojtaba Khamenei's selection as supreme leader aligns precisely with this finding. Trump historically dismissive of intelligence community; Gabbard fired NIC acting chairperson after prior inconvenient assessment. (AP)
- **Tomahawk fragments in Minab school marked "Made in USA."** Iran displayed missile components with US DoD contract numbers, "SDL ANTENNA" (Tomahawk satellite link), and explicit US markings at the site where 160+ were killed, many of them children. NYT photographs authenticated the hardware; preliminary US intelligence assessment concluded it was "likely" a US strike. Trump: "I just don't know enough about it" and willing to live with investigation. Schumer: "Iran doesn't have Tomahawk missiles, Donald Trump! The claim is beyond asinine." Earlier denials — including suggesting Iran might also possess Tomahawks — now untenable. (Common Dreams, ABC, AP, NYT)
- **War costs: $5.6 billion in munitions in first two days alone.** Administration report to congressional committees. Three officials cited. Tomahawks ~$2.2M each; 2,000+ munitions fired, 5,000+ targets. Center for American Progress: ~$630M to assemble force before first shot fired; friendly fire (F-15s) ~$351M additional. GOP reportedly briefed with lower estimates nearer $2B. Members of Congress who will face funding request increasingly alarmed. (The Hill, Washington Post, Pentagon briefing)
- **Pentagon confirms ~140 US service members wounded over 10 days; 7 KIA total.** 108 returned to duty; 8 severely injured. Pentagon spokesman Sean Parnell in emailed statement. Latest KIA: Army Sgt. Benjamin Pennington, 26, died of wounds from 1 Mar attack on Prince Sultan Air Base, Saudi Arabia. (ABC, Reuters, The Hindu)
- **Australia confirms Wedgetail + air-to-air missiles to UAE; Labor internally fractured.** PM Albanese confirmed E-7A Wedgetail surveillance plane and medium-range air-to-air missiles to UAE; 85 personnel operational by end of week. "Not part of offensive action." Rationale: 115,000 Australians in Middle East, 24,000 in UAE. Simultaneously: Guardian reveals Labor left faction held "robust" debate (March 2) about government's Feb 28 endorsement; MPs questioned omission of any reference to international law; concerns raised with Wong. Labor Against War group circulating motion to NSW/Victoria branches condemning war as "illegal act of aggression"; targets July national conference. International law experts including UN special rapporteur Ben Saul argue attacks breach UN charter. (Guardian, ABC)

White House told DHS to hold it. DHS source: "any unclassified FOUO information going forward concerning Iran has to be reviewed by the White House before any dissemination." "Chilling effect on keeping state and local law enforcement informed." (Daily Beast)
- **Saudi Arabia in final stages of "huge deal" for Ukrainian interceptor tech.** Kyiv Independent: Saudi arms company signed deal for Ukrainian interceptor missiles; broader deal possibly finalized as soon as 11 Mar. Ukraine sending experts to Qatar, UAE, Saudi Arabia, and Jordan. Zelenskyy confirmed on WhatsApp Q&A. Context: Iran's Shahed swarms have depleted Gulf interceptor stocks; Ukraine has 4 years experience intercepting Russian Shaheds at ~$1,000 per unit. US "burning through" precision missiles vs $20,000 Shaheds at unsustainable cost ratio. (Kyiv Independent, ABC)

Trump appeared interested but "his team did nothing." Now Gulf states scrambling for exactly that tech. EU defence commissioner: US no longer capable of supplying allies with sufficient missiles. (New Republic)

Entire government built on one man's ego with no reality check. (iNews)

Leavitt: India has been a "good actor," previously stopped buying Russian oil; this is "short-term" to fill gap caused by "the Iranians." The Russian oil was "already at sea." (Bloomberg, The Hindu)

Brent briefly hit ~$87.80/bbl after spiking to $119.50 Monday, then falling on Trump's "very complete" comments. Trump waiving some oil sanctions to boost supply. ## Likely Next Stages (as of end of Day 11)

### What Day 11 changes in the trajectory

Two developments on Day 11 shift the probabilities more than any battlefield update:

**1. The NIC leak.** The US government's own intelligence community told the President before the war that it would not produce regime change. It was ignored. Mojtaba's selection now proves the assessment correct. This is politically catastrophic for any administration seeking a "mission accomplished" exit: the stated objective was never achievable, and there is now a documentary record proving the White House knew. This does not end the war but it does change the domestic political calculus — particularly when congressional funding is requested.

**2. "Made in USA" Tomahawk fragments at Minab.** Physical evidence displayed publicly. NYT photos authenticated. Preliminary US intel: "likely" US responsible. Trump's serial denials — including suggesting Iran might have Tomahawks — have now been punctured not by journalists but by missile parts. This is the moment in any major war when the information battle tilts against the government and becomes irreversible. Chomsky, Jiang, and the Vietnam credibility gap prediction all converge here.

### Three trajectories (updated from Day 10)

**1. Stalemate / declared victory — most likely (55%).** The NIC leak and Minab accountability problem actually increase the incentive for Trump to find a rapid exit frame — he needs to declare victory before the congressional funding debate and before the school strike investigation concludes. "Degradation objectives met," Hormuz partially reopens, oil falls from $90 to $75, and Trump claims he "won." Netanyahu continues operations at lower tempo independently. The Minab investigation is buried. This remains the path of least resistance but is now more urgently needed.

**2. Negotiated off-ramp — more plausible than Day 10 (30%).** Putin has "voiced ideas"; Witkoff visits Israel next week; Jaishankar active (India as back-channel); IEA emergency meeting is economic pressure on all parties. Iran's terms remain hard (permanent end to war, not ceasefire), but the "Made in USA" fragments and NIC leak give Washington political cover to negotiate without looking like capitulation — they can frame negotiation as "responsible response to new information." Still structurally difficult.

**3. Escalation — least likely but with assembling preconditions (15%).** Special forces for uranium sites remain on the table (Axios); the cost-pressure on Iran's Gulf attacks is intensifying; and if Hormuz stays closed another week while oil climbs back toward $110-120, Trump may feel forced to demonstrate decisive action. But the NIC assessment and the school strike problem make escalation politically toxic domestically. And the Minab story feeds directly into the casualty-gap problem: if the US is bombing schools and lying about it, ground troops create an even larger credibility surface.

### The signal to watch this week

**Will the US congressional funding request create a floor for debate?** The $5.6B two-day munitions figure is now in congressional hands. If opposition coalesces around that number — particularly after the NIC leak and Minab evidence — Trump faces either a funding fight or a political incentive to end the war before the vote. This is the structural path through which Ron Paul's "Congress abdicates war powers" prediction meets its first real test of reversal.

**Watch also:** Whether the Witkoff–Israel meeting produces any signal of divergence, and whether oil holds below $90 or climbs again as the "very complete" rhetoric fades against continued Iranian strikes.
