# Casualty reconciliation: US and coalition vs Iranian claims

This note tracks official and claimed casualty figures for Operation Epic Fury and Iranian retaliation. Use it to compare US CENTCOM counts, Iranian claims, and third-party or satellite-based estimates. See also: "Vietnam credibility gap" (Jiang, Chomsky) — pattern where official figures diverge from reality until truth becomes impossible to suppress.

## US military (Operation Epic Fury)

### First 24 hours (28 Feb – 1 Mar 2026)

- **US CENTCOM (Defense One, White House):** 3 KIA, 5 seriously wounded (Kuwait, Iranian missile; US Army Sustainment Command). Several minor shrapnel/concussion. 6 US F-15s shot down by Kuwaiti air defence; crew ejected safely.
- **Later cumulative:** 6 KIA from 1 Mar Kuwait drone strike; then 7th KIA (wounds from 1 Mar Saudi Arabia attack) announced by CENTCOM. Day 10 archive: "8–10" US official figure for Gulf/Bahrain casualties in one 24h window.

### Operation scale (context)

- 50,000 US troops; ~200 fighter aircraft; two carrier groups; 2,000 targets; 2,000+ munitions (RedState, Defense One, White House).
- Trump: conflict could last four to five weeks.

**Sources:** Defense One (first 24 hours by the numbers), White House (CENTCOM Commander Operation Epic Fury), RedState (2,000 targets), live5news (7th American killed).

## Iranian claims

### 24-hour window (early March 2026)

- **Press TV / Khatam al-Anbiya (7 Mar):** 220 US killed or wounded in 24 hours. Breakdown: 21 at US Navy Fifth Fleet, Bahrain; approximately 200 killed/wounded at Al Dhafra Air Base. Part of Operation True Promise 4.
- **EADaily (1 Mar):** 560 killed and wounded at US base in Bahrain (IRGC claim).

### Notes

- No independent or satellite-based verification of Iranian figures in this archive. Day 10 update cites "satellite imagery of destroyed bases cited by analysts as inconsistent with single-digit casualties" (YouTube analysis) — not a confirmed count.
- Iran’s 220 (and 560 Bahrain) are **claims**, not verified. US figures are **official** but may be partial or lag (e.g. 7th KIA announced later). The **gap** itself is the story for prediction tracking (credibility gap, domestic pressure).

**Sources:** Press TV (220 US forces killed or wounded 24 hours), EADaily (560 Bahrain), Day 10 daily update, YouTube transcript (220 dead, satellite inconsistency).
