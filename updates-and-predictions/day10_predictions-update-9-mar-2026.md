## What Happened on Day 10

**The big story was the markets, not the battlefield.** Oil hit **$119.50/bbl** in early Asian trading — highest since 2022 — then Trump told CBS from his Doral golf club that the war was "very complete, pretty much," Iran had "nothing left in a military sense," and the US was "very far ahead of schedule." S&P 500 and Nasdaq surged >1% after earlier being down 1.5%; WTI collapsed back below $86. The Dept of War had posted "We have Only Just Begun to Fight" and "no mercy" on X just hours before Trump spoke. Analysts: "the market is trading on hope... if it just turns out to be false hope we could reverse again tonight."

**On the ground, no de-escalation was visible:**

- **Mojtaba Khamenei** formally named third Supreme Leader. Israel: "anyone who poses a threat will be struck." Trump: he'll have to get US approval or "is not going to last long." IDF: ~1,900 IRGC soldiers and commanders killed since 28 Feb. Strikes continued — Tehran, Isfahan, southern Iran, 35 Hezbollah high-rises Beirut, 700+ Hezbollah sites in Lebanon.
- **UAE absorbed 1,400+ attacks** since war began; four civilians dead, 114 injured. UAE ambassador in Geneva: "being targeted in a very unwarranted manner"; will not join the war; bases not used against Iran; calls for return to negotiations. Two additional UAE servicemen killed — helicopter crashed on national duty.
- **Iran hit three AWS data centres** (two UAE, one Bahrain) — first deliberate airstrikes on data centres in any recorded conflict. Outages hit banking, payments, apps, enterprise software. Iran's Fars News said the Bahrain facility was targeted for its role supporting "enemy military and intelligence activities." US military uses AWS for some intelligence workloads including Anthropic's Claude.
- **Second Iranian ballistic missile intercepted over Turkiye** (Gaziantep) by NATO defences — second interception in the war. NATO has not invoked Article 5.
- **Pakistan** closed all schools through end of March; steep government austerity — 20% spending cut, cabinet unpaid two months, MPs 25% wage cut, no foreign trips, official iftars banned. Sharif: UAE, Qatar, Saudi, Kuwait together supply ~99% of Pakistan's LNG imports — all disrupted.
- **G7/IEA emergency meeting:** "ready to take necessary measures" including stockpile release — no actual release agreed. French FM: "we are not there yet." UK Chancellor Reeves called for "immediate de-escalation."
- **Australia confirmed** deploying an E-7A Wedgetail surveillance plane and medium-range air-to-air missiles to UAE; 85 personnel operational by end of week.
- **Lindsey Graham threatened Saudi Arabia** for refusing to join military operations; simultaneously warned Israel to be "cautious" about targeting oil infrastructure it will need post-war for reconstruction.
- **UK:** wholesale gas up ~25% Monday; Northern Ireland heating oil +81%; rural England prices doubled. Reeves held emergency G7 energy call.
- **Trump–Putin call:** one hour, "frank and businesslike." Putin "voiced a few ideas" for a quick political-diplomatic settlement after conversations with Gulf leaders and Pezeshkian.
- **Macron–Lebanon deadlock:** France trying to replicate the late-2024 Lebanon ceasefire; Israel has refused all French mediation. ~400 killed in Lebanon (83 children), 500,000+ displaced by 8 March.

## What Is Likely

### The Trump "very complete" signal — genuine or improvised?

Two possibilities. First: it is a genuine signal of an off-ramp being constructed, possibly via Putin's "ideas for political-diplomatic settlement," possibly via Oman. Markets treated it this way. Oil fell $30 in hours. Second: it is an improvised response to oil price panic — the same morning the DoW posted "no mercy," Hegseth said "this is only just the beginning," and the DoW posted "We have Only Just Begun to Fight." The signals are contradictory. Analysts explicitly noted: "if it just turns out to be false hope we could reverse again tonight."

The Asia Times (Emmott) argument cuts through this: **what Trump says matters less than what Netanyahu will accept.** Netanyahu has threatened to kill Mojtaba Khamenei. His stated goals would take many more weeks. Trump will find it hard to stop him from "going all the way."

### Three trajectories (updated from Day 9)

**1. Stalemate / "mowing the grass" — most likely.** Trump declares degradation objectives met in 2–3 more weeks. Israel continues semi-independently. Mojtaba Khamenei survives (or is assassinated and the cycle repeats). Hormuz partially reopens. Oil settles $90–110. No regime change. Economic damage severe and lasting. This is the path of least political resistance for Trump given the polls (59% disapprove), gas prices ($3.48/gallon), and the structural inability to escalate further without a ground invasion.

**2. Negotiated off-ramp — less likely than Day 9, but possible.** Putin's "ideas," Oman/Qatar back-channel, and the G7's escalating economic pressure create the conditions. Iran's terms are harder than western coverage conveys: Araghchi said not a ceasefire but a permanent end to war; Iran was burned by the June ceasefire. Any deal Trump can call a "win" would need to simultaneously look like total victory and meet Iran's conditions for a permanent end. Structurally difficult but not impossible for Trump, who reframed the North Korea stalemate as a win. Timeline: weeks to months.

**3. Ground escalation — least likely but no longer dismissible.** Special forces for uranium sites → casualties → sunk cost → more troops. The preconditions Jiang described are all now present. E6B Mercury deployed. If ground forces enter Iran and are engaged at scale, the nuclear question moves from theoretical to operational. Jiang's prediction: Trump threatens nukes, Putin blocks publicly and extracts maximum strategic leverage. This is the worst outcome and Jiang is the only analyst in the archive who has mapped it in detail.

### The clearest near-term signal to watch

Whether the oil market's trust in Trump's "very complete" holds through this week. If Iran continues strikes at day-10 pace and more Gulf infrastructure burns, the $119 spike returns and the pressure on Trump to either escalate (troops) or find a genuine exit (deal) becomes acute. The G7 has its finger on 1.8 billion barrels of strategic reserve — but French FM Lescure said on Monday: "we are not there yet." That statement cannot remain true for long if the war continues.

The Pakistan story may be the leading indicator nobody is watching: a nuclear state with no food and fuel sovereignty, closing its schools and cutting its cabinet's salaries, three weeks into a war it had no role in starting. The cascading secondary effects Chomsky, Paul, and Kovalik all warned about — the costs falling on countries and populations with no vote in the decision — are now visible and measurable in real time.
