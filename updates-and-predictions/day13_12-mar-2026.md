## Jiang and Chomsky Analysis — Day 13

### What Jiang would say

**The credibility collapse is now documented in government records.** Three simultaneous developments on Day 13 create an unprecedented paper trail:

1. **Pentagon investigation confirms school strike.** US military's own investigation contradicts the commander-in-chief's public statements. This isn't media speculation — it's the military documenting that Trump's "Iran bombed itself" suggestion was false.

2. **Intelligence agencies briefing Congress that regime won't collapse.** The classified reality directly contradicts the public justification. Officials are "afraid" to tell Trump the truth, creating documented evidence of a failing strategy known internally but denied publicly.

3. **Trump admitting he was "falling asleep" during war planning.** The commander-in-chief publicly acknowledged not paying attention to the planning of a war that has killed 175 children. This is not spin — it's his own words.

The structural gap between what the government knows internally and says publicly has rarely been this stark or well-documented. The question is whether any institution — Congress, courts, media, allies — will act on this information.

**The cyber dimension changes the cost calculation.** Stryker was a Fortune 500 medical device company. Iran demonstrated it can wipe 200,000+ devices across 79 countries overnight. If this capability is turned on financial services, power grids, or defense contractors, the economic warfare expands beyond oil prices. The US has extensive cyber capabilities but is also extensively vulnerable. Iran may have calculated that demonstrating capability creates deterrence — or it may be the opening of a new front.

**The "unconditional surrender" vs "permanent guarantee" positions are irreconcilable as stated.** But both may be maximalist opening positions. Watch for creative reframing: "permanent guarantee" could become "UN monitoring" or "regional security framework"; "unconditional surrender" could become "verified dismantlement" or "new leadership acceptable to international community." The question is whether either side's domestic politics permit retreat from stated positions.

### What Chomsky would say

**The school strike follows the historical pattern.** When civilian massacres are documented, the apparatus follows a predictable sequence: initial denial → claim enemy is responsible → investigation when denial fails → acknowledgment of "error" → assertion of good intentions → no accountability → continuation of policy. The school strike is now at stage 4 (acknowledgment of "error"). The pattern predicts stage 5-7 will follow: good intentions will be asserted, no one will face consequences, and operations will continue.

**The information control requirements have exceeded the apparatus's capacity.** Trump's "I don't know about it" when asked about his own military's investigation. Officials "afraid" to tell him the truth. G7 allies "confused" by contradictory signals. The propaganda model assumes coordinated messaging from centers of power — but the messaging is now contradictory *within* the administration. This doesn't mean the war stops; it means the justifications become increasingly incoherent while operations continue.

**The domestic dimension is escalating in parallel.** Queensland arrests for explaining a phrase. UAE charging tourists for filming. VA healthcare gutted while 140 troops wounded. Common Dreams documenting how war rhetoric enables election manipulation. The pattern: as the external conflict fails to achieve stated objectives, the domestic apparatus intensifies control to manage the narrative rather than adjust the policy.

**The cyber attack represents a new category of blowback.** Iran has historically projected power through proxies and missiles. Demonstrating cyber capability against a Fortune 500 company signals that retaliation can reach the American homeland without missiles. If this is the beginning of a sustained cyber campaign, the war's costs to American civilian infrastructure could exceed the military expenditure.

### Convergence

Both Jiang and Chomsky would note the **speed of documentation**. Day 13 is not just a worsening situation but a day when official documents, intelligence briefings, and Trump's own admissions created a record of:

- The war was launched based on flawed intelligence (school strike investigation)
- The war's objectives cannot be achieved (intelligence briefing to Congress)
- The commander-in-chief was not paying attention during planning (Trump admission)
- Officials are afraid to tell Trump the truth (NYT report)

This is the kind of documentary evidence that historically enables accountability — if institutions choose to act. The question is whether any institution will, or whether the pattern of documented failure followed by no consequences continues.
