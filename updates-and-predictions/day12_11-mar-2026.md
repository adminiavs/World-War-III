## Likely Next Stages (as of end of Day 12)

### What Day 12 changes in the trajectory

Three developments on Day 12 shift the probabilities structurally:

**1. The war has gone global.** Japan releasing stockpiled oil and capping gasoline prices. Europe approving the largest emergency oil reserve release in IEA history (400 million barrels). South Korea involved in IEA discussions while losing THAAD interceptors to the Gulf. Australia's ACCC convening emergency meetings as fuel supply hits "worst in decades." Three major economic blocs are simultaneously taking emergency measures — this is unprecedented outside of world wars. The economic emergency has crossed the threshold where allied governments can absorb it passively.

**2. The war aims are contradicted from inside the coalition.** Israeli defense analysts publicly admitting regime change is "most likely" unachievable. Democratic senators emerging from classified briefings to call the strategy "totally incoherent" with "no logical estimates" of duration. The NIC leak (Day 10) + Israeli analyst admissions (Day 12) + Democratic senators' classified information (Day 12) = the stated objectives are unachievable, and the evidence is coming from inside the house. Meanwhile Trump's "unconditional surrender" demand (March 6) remains the official US position — structurally incompatible with reality on the ground.

**3. The AI-targeting revelation creates a new accountability dimension.** Claude used for 1,000+ targets in 24 hours. Anthropic's CEO refused to remove safeguards for mass surveillance and autonomous weapons. Pentagon declared Anthropic a "supply-chain risk" but the military cannot operate without it. "No stupid rules of engagement." No evidence AI reduces civilian casualties. The school strike investigation is ongoing while the system that may have enabled it continues operating. This creates a legal and political exposure category that did not exist before this war.

### Three trajectories (updated from Day 11)

**1. Declared victory / stalemate — still most likely (45%, down from 55%).** The "unconditional surrender" demand makes a clean exit harder to frame — Trump cannot declare surrender achieved while Iran fires 20 missiles a day. But the emergency oil releases buy time economically, and the $50B funding request creates a congressional deadline. Israeli military wanting 2+ more weeks conflicts with Trump's incentive for rapid exit. The AI-targeting and school strike accountability problems compound over time, creating pressure to end before investigation concludes. The emergency reserves have a shelf life — once deployed, they create a countdown.

**2. Negotiated off-ramp — rising (30%, up from 25%).** China-Iran safe passage talks (Reuters) represent a potential template: Iran keeps Hormuz open for some traffic in exchange for economic concessions. Multiple back-channels active. Democratic senators' public warnings create political cover for negotiation. Iran's selective Hormuz strategy (open for China, closed for West) may itself be a negotiating lever. Key obstacle: Iran demands "permanent end" vs Trump demands "unconditional surrender." These are incompatible as stated, but both sides may be seeking face-saving exits. The NIC leak and Israeli admissions give Washington cover to redefine objectives downward.

**3. Escalation — risk increasing (25%, up from 15%).** Classified briefings suggest active ground troop planning. 48 THAAD interceptors repositioned to Gulf. Romania approving US forces. FBI domestic retaliation warnings. Iran's selective Hormuz strategy (blocking West, supplying China) creates a choice: accept Chinese-Iranian dominance of the strait or escalate to reopen it. If Hormuz remains closed to Western shipping for another week while China continues buying Iranian oil, Trump faces pressure to demonstrate capability. The AI system enables faster, higher-volume targeting which could intensify the air campaign before a ground decision is made.

### The signals to watch this week

**Will the $50B supplemental request trigger Congressional opposition?** The NIC leak, school strike evidence, Israeli analysts' admissions, classified briefing revelations, and $10B cost collectively create the strongest political foundation for opposition since the war began. If Democrats can force public hearings, the information environment shifts fundamentally.

**How long do emergency oil reserves last?** Japan's 15-day + 1-month release and Europe's 400M barrels are emergency buffers, not solutions. If the war extends beyond the reserves' coverage window, the economic crisis returns worse — because the buffer is gone. This creates a structural countdown that may be the most powerful force toward resolution.

**Does Iran's selective Hormuz strategy become a negotiating lever or a provocation?** Iran shipping 11.7M barrels to China while blocking everyone else is sophisticated economic warfare. If the US frames this as justification for escalation (to "reopen" the strait), the war widens. If it becomes a template for selective reopening, it could be a path to de-escalation.

**Watch South Korea.** If North Korea tests a missile during the THAAD absence, the Indo-Pacific crisis compounds the Middle East crisis. Lee Jae Myung's public reassurances are the kind of statement leaders make when they're not confident.
