## Key Themes

- **"What we say goes":** U.S. as outlaw state; single standard = subordination to power; treaties/norms don't restrain "outlaws" in U.S. discourse (Traub, NYT); Iraq invasion = supreme international crime; discipline of not saying what you know.
- **Pirate vs emperor (Augustine):** Molest seas vs molest world; same logic, scale differs.
- **Civil obedience:** Zinn: problem is civil obedience; disobedience = re-creating functioning democracy; Bolivia (Morales), Haiti (Aristide) as contrasts.
- **Media:** Double standard = single standard (Friel/Falk, *Record of the Paper*); UN Charter/international law absent from 70 Iraq editorials 9/11–March 2003; domestic surveillance vs international law.
- **History and memory:** "People who hold the clubs insist on historical amnesia"; people hit by clubs remember (Iran, Venezuela, Chile).

## Chapter 1 – What We Say Goes (Feb 2006)

- **Traub (NYT Mag):** Treaties don't restrain outlaws (Saddam); U.S. can and will act alone; diplomacy needs united front. Chomsky: U.S. is leading outlaw state; "what we say goes."
- **Pakistan Jan 2006:** 18 civilians killed in U.S. missile strike; NYT editorial: strikes "legitimately aimed" at Al Qaeda leaders; no limits on U.S. right to use force.
- **Nuremberg:** Aggression = "supreme international crime" containing "accumulated evil of the whole."
- **Friel & Falk,** *Record of the Paper:* 70 Iraq editorials, no "UN Charter" or "international law"; enemy violations = outrage, U.S. = as if it didn't happen.
- **King (1967 Riverside):** "Assume task of opposing government policy, especially in time of war"; U.S. was attacking, not attacked; "plain, outright aggression."
- **Zinn:** Problem is civil obedience.
- **Bolivia/Haiti:** Morales elected (indigenous); Aristide would have won 2004 if not expelled; "democracy" = do what we say.
- **Indigenous:** Entering political arena; control own resources; "Indian nation" ideas; threat to U.S.
- **King:** Honored for Alabama; condemned for poverty, war, Memphis strike, Poor People's March.

## Chapter 2 – Lebanon and the Middle East (Aug 2006)

- **Official story holes:** July 12 Hezbollah capture of soldiers; but U.S./Israel have no objection to capture of soldiers or kidnapping of civilians; Israel abducts civilians (e.g. two Gazans June 24); Gaza violence (40+ killed June, 170+ late June–July) preceded Lebanon; no call to invade Israel.
- **Hezbollah reasons:** Prisoner exchange; solidarity with Gaza (only meaningful Arab support for Palestinians).
- **Jan 2006:** Palestinians voted wrong way (Hamas); punishment: funds cut, atrocities, starvation; "democracy promotion."
- **West Bank:** "Convergence"/"withdrawal" = annexation, cantonization, water, settlements; Jordan Valley; illegal under UN, World Court.
- **Hamas:** Truce 1.5 years; Israel kept assassinations, no truce; Hamas for long-term truce, two-state if Israel commits to withdraw.
- **UN border record:** Hundreds of Israeli violations (overflights, sonic booms); no confirmed Hezbollah rocket May 2000–July 2006 except May 28, 2006 retaliation.
- **Hezbollah/Iran:** Don't regard Israel as legitimate; Nasrallah: accept what Palestinians accept (two-state); Khamenei: Iran accepts Arab League position, two-state on international border; only U.S. and Israel reject consensus.
- **Nasrallah:** Reasonable, pragmatic (Peck); Chomsky met opponents (Jumblatt, Mallat); no good answer to "does Lebanon deserve a deterrent?" Fifth invasion in 30 years; 1982 ~20,000 dead; deterrent = guerrilla war or U.S. commitment (unlikely).
- **1559:** Unique interference in internal affairs; Syrian withdrawal (cynical: Syria entered 1976 with U.S./Israel support to kill Palestinians); "U.S.–Israeli invasion" (planes, missiles, cluster munitions from U.S.; U.S. vetoed cease-fire).
- **Hersh:** U.S. closely involved in planning Israel's retaliation; Chomsky: no time for detailed planning; green light likely; one goal = remove Lebanese deterrent to U.S. attack on Iran.
- **Reinhart:** Sharon 2000 withdrawal then wait for incident to justify devastating attack on Lebanon/Syria; "new order" in Lebanon.
- **Varadarajan (Hindu):** IDF degraded by occupation, fighting guerrillas; Chomsky: Israel can still crush Syria; Hezbollah = guerrilla, population, "Hezbollah targets" = civilian society; Dershowitz: 80% support Hezbollah so all Lebanese legitimate targets.
- **Cedar Revolution:** Lebanese suspicion of U.S. higher than of Syria; Hezbollah = social services (health, education); U.S. wants commercial/financial center for wealthy; destroying secular nationalism created religious extremism.
- **U.S. and Islamic fundamentalism:** Strongest outside supporter since WWII; Saudi Arabia; Nasser threat; 1967 Israel eliminated secular Arab nationalism; Israel created Hamas by destroying PLO (negotiations); Afghanistan (jihadis); Zia, Reagan, Pakistan nukes, madrassas; Saddam secular, supported when useful.
- **Rumsfeld/Cheney/Wolfowitz:** Created Shiite-dominated Iraq, close to Iran; Maliki condemned Lebanon invasion; Dems boycotted him.
- **Iraq withdrawal:** Cannot leave Iraq sovereign; Vietnam analogy wrong; Iraq too valuable (oil, center of energy region); sovereign democratic Iraq = catastrophe; Shiite bloc (Iran, Iraq, Shiite Saudi) controlling oil, maybe turning East; China not intimidated; Hu Jintao insult (no state dinner), then Saudi visit, investment; "ultimate nightmare."
- **Kristol (Weekly Standard):** Strike Iranian nuke facilities. Chomsky: Iran offered negotiations 2003 (Khatami, clerical approval): all issues, two-state; Bush didn't reply, censured Swiss diplomat; U.S. refuses; Rice "shift" = negotiate if Iran concedes result first (stop enrichment) with gun to head; ElBaradei proposal (international control of fissile production); only Iran accepted (Larijani).
- **Korten (Great Turning):** Perfect storm = peak oil, climate, dollar collapse. Chomsky: Nuclear war threat more serious and imminent; McNamara "apocalypse soon"; dollar = mixed; peak oil could be blessing if it forces change; health care, 9/11 theories (diversion of energy from real crimes; no serious evidence; power centers may like it).

## Chapter 3 – Latin America (Sep 2006)

- **Thucydides:** Large nations do what they wish, small accept what they must. **Adam Smith:** "Principal architects" (merchants/manufacturers) make sure their interests "most particularly attended to," however grievous for others.
- **Chávez UN (Sep 20, 2006):** "Devil," sulfur; little attention to substance. **2005 speech:** New international economic order (UNCTAD, nonaligned 1970s); UN in south; energy consumption; millennium goals; UN Charter on force; 2006 speech: prolonged applause = views widely held; Europe polls: U.S. top threat to peace; "tinpot dictator" = elected, certified, media hostile, coup 2002 (Bush support), referendums won; Venezuelan support highest in Latin America; social programs.
- **Ridiculing Bush:** Helps Rove (elite liberals vs "regular guys").
- **Friedman on Chávez:** Oil to sway elections, populism to ditch. Chomsky: We influence elections (NED, State, Nicaragua ambassador "vote our way or we strangle you"); Friedman's economics = disaster (neoliberal rules = growth decline); China/India grew by violating rules; Chile Pinochet/Chicago Boys = worst collapse, state bailout, "Chicago road to socialism"; Codelco (Allende nationalization) not privatized; Argentina Kirchner violated IMF, recovery; India labs vs peasant suicides, rural flight (P. Sainath).
- **Democracy deficit:** Failed States; 2005 PIPA: public wanted inverse of federal budget (cut military, raise social); no paper reported; Waldman (Boston Globe): Democrats think issues matter, Republicans know imagery matters.
- **Elections:** Fraud (Florida 2000, Ohio 2004) less critical than no real election (two candidates, imagery, no positions); 5–10% House seats seriously contested; funding predicts outcome.
- **Venezuela history:** Gómez (Wilson kicked British out); Pérez Jiménez (Eisenhower medal); Chávez pre-coup = neoliberal disaster, riots; 2002 coup (U.S.); "if Iran had supported coup in U.S.?"
- **Iran:** 1953 coup, shah, Carter 1977 "love," 1979 coup attempt, Reagan/Saddam, hundreds of thousands dead, 1989 Iraqi nuke engineers to U.S. for training; half-century of torment.
- **Citgo heating oil:** Only one to help poor (Boston, South Bronx); condemned as buying influence; PetroCaribe; Operation Miracle (Cuban doctors, Jamaica); Telesur; new international information order (UNESCO), U.S. defunded UNESCO; Al Jazeera bombed (Kabul, Baghdad), U.S. tried to shut down; Qatar emir, Powell, "freedom of press."
- **Mercosur, Venezuela:** First real move to independence since conquest; 2002 coup, U.S. backed down (Latin American reaction); violence less effective; IMF (Argentina kicking out); Venezuela–China; integration (transport, elites to West); south–south (China in Latin America, Saudi, Iran; Venezuela diversify oil).
- **Mirror:** Why prolonged applause for Chávez? Why in polls is U.S. main threat? What have we done to Iran 50 years, Iraq now? 70% Iraqis want troops out in a year; Baghdad 2/3 immediately; U.S. = violence, sectarianism, permanent bases; Lebanon 90% see as U.S.–Israel war; cluster bombs, fuel tanks, oil spill, poisoned water (env minister); cultural zone Beirut flattened; Iraq worse ("stuff happens").

## Chapter 4 – United States Versus the Gospels (Dec 2006)

- **Pinochet obit (NYT):** Passing reference to U.S. role; U.S. undermined 1958, 1964; 1970 committed to overthrow; "hard track" (coup), "soft track" ("make economy scream" – Nixon to Helms); Chile = one of series of coups.
- **Villa Grimaldi:** Torture, doctors kept victims alive for next stage; doctors still practicing in Santiago; "culture of terror" (Hernández Pico); Chicago Boys ran economy into worst depression; 1982 state bailout; "first 9/11" (1973): per capita equivalent = White House bombed, president killed, 50–100k dead, 700k tortured, etc.
- **Passive voice:** "People were killed" not "We killed them."
- **Bachelet:** Woman, divorced, prisoner, father killed; Codelco 10% to army (law); Mapuche, Aymara, Quechua; Iquique 1907 massacre (miners, 1000+), "preventive war."
- **Ortega (Nicaragua):** Opportunist, pact with Alemán; second poorest; IMF rules, energy privatized, shortage; Costa Rica ~100% sustainable energy; water (Bechtel, Cochabamba).
- **Correa (Ecuador):** U.S. base; "If they let us put base in Miami we'll accept base in Ecuador."
- **Mexico/Calderón:** Split; wall, NAFTA 1994, Gatekeeper; expected migration from Mexican agriculture collapse.
- **Yunus/Grameen:** Microcredit to women; sensible, not panacea; "pure capitalism."
- **Benedict/liberation theology:** "Takes Gospels seriously" = crime; Constantine turned cross to empire; Church = rich and powerful; Reagan wars vs Church.
- **Iraq "opposition":** Opposition = occupation hasn't gone well; they want it to go well (like Russian soldier in Afghanistan vs Canadian in Iraq).
- **Afghanistan:** Chomsky: Afghan war = major war crime; we didn't know attacks from Afghanistan (Mueller, 8 months later: maybe hatched there, implemented in Gulf, Germany); bombing = hand over bin Laden or we bomb; Taliban gestures (handover if evidence) rejected; Abdul Haq, 1000 Afghans in Peshawar opposed bombing; now warlords, opium.
- **Walzer/Elshtain (just war):** No arguments; no opponents; Elshtain's reasons = falsehoods (Hegemony or Survival).
- **GI coffeehouses:** Sir! No Sir!, Soldiers in Revolt; support for soldiers, effective; renewal near Fort Drum.
- **Krieger/nuclear:** Nuclear states = criminal; NPT Article 6 (good-faith elimination); U.S. in lead violating; India deal tears NPT (Milhollin, Current History).

## Chapter 5 – Framework for Thinkable Thoughts (Jan 2007)

- **Fighting power now vs 1960s:** Easier now; many topics unmentionable then (Israel post-1967); now can discuss; solidarity movements didn't exist; anticorporate movement now conceivable.
- **Imperialism:** Now open; 1960s couldn't raise fundamental benevolence of U.S. policy.
- **Fissures:** Public vs power; both parties and business right of population; Iran = majority for diplomacy not confrontation; Iraq Study Group never mentions what Iraqis want (withdraw); same polls show 61% think attack on U.S. soldiers legitimate, majority want U.S. out; "adjust tactics so Iraqis don't see us as occupiers" vs "they want us out."
- **Unions:** Private sector crushed (Reagan: employers can use illegal measures); public steady; can be reversed.
- **Economy:** "United States" = territory (declining) vs owners (fine); trade deficit if count U.S. corporate production abroad as domestic would shrink; housing bubble; China holds ~$1T debt, props up U.S.
- **Oil in euros:** Advantage = don't buy international currency; diversification effect unclear.
- **Iran:** UN Charter Article 2 outlaws threat or use of force; U.S. accepted as outlaw; only criticism = might get us in trouble; Iran opening bank in Iraq = "interference" (like Russia protesting U.S. bank in Afghanistan).
- **Nixon/Cambodia:** Costless (defenseless); Iran = can blow region. Cambodia bombing (Congress barred, continued secretly); Clinton release: tonnage ~5x known; Kiernan; Khmer Rouge 10k to hundreds of thousands; "anything that flies, anything that moves" (Nixon/Kissinger) = genocide call, no comment.
- **Vietnam vs Iraq:** Different motives, character, end; Rich "parallels" = superficial; Vietnam ~1970 U.S. had won main aims (destroy country, inoculate region); can't do that in Iraq; why can't leave = Iraq too valuable.
- **Munich/Hitler/Iran:** Beck, Netanyahu "1938, Iran is Germany"; Chomsky: Munich welcomed by FDR; Iran couldn't defeat Iraq; military almost nonexistent; if Iran had nukes, only as deterrent (otherwise vaporized); Pakistan has leaked nukes.
- **Strike on Iran:** Could stir Shiite forces, Saudi Sunnis; doesn't take military power.
- **ElBaradei:** Sanctions = escalation; military attack "absolutely bonkers," would strengthen hard-liners, drive program underground.
- **Osirak 1981:** Didn't end Iraqi nuke program; initiated it (Nature: reactor not for weapons; after bombing Saddam went clandestine).
- **Pentagon contingency:** Take Khuzestan (Arab, oil), bomb rest to dust.
- **Polls (region):** Turkey, Saudi, Pakistan prefer nuclear-armed Iran to military action.
- **"Meddling":** Iran in Iraq = no irony; Vietnam: we bomb Chinese railroad in North Vietnam; China repairs = "interference"; insinuate we own world.
- **On Power and Ideology:** Debate within unspoken presuppositions = framework for thinkable thought; "right to win in Iraq" = unthinkable.
- **Threats and regime:** Harsher in Iran = good for U.S. (protest, secession); Azeris, Khuzestan; "Iranian-backed Hezbollah" (not "U.S.-backed moderates"); demonization; "you're supporting Ahmadinejad/Holocaust denier" = protect right to lie.
- **NPR:** Golan = "disputed" (annexation 1981, unanimous SC condemnation); wall = "security barrier" (annexation wall); Buergenthal (World Court): wall protecting settlers = ipso facto violation; Kollek tribute ("great unifier") vs did nothing for Palestinians; Juan Williams to Bush: "people praying for you, want to be with you" = commissar.
- **Iraq Study Group:** Say we'll inform Iraqis we don't intend permanent bases; don't say stop building them; embassy = city in Baghdad.
- **"Great are only great because we are on our knees."**

## Chapter 6 – Invasions and Evasions (Feb 2007)

- **Tinkerers vs overhaulers:** Both; tinkering can help (Boston Globe under Winship: first to call for Vietnam withdrawal, Central America coverage; reverted after Scharfenberg 1992); tinkering = preliminary to structural change when running into barriers.
- **Lippmann 1927:** World thinks of U.S. as empire except Americans; "civilizing work so reluctantly."
- **Porter:** Saltwater fallacy; territorial expansion = imperialism (settler colonialism).
- **Passive voice:** "People were killed" not "We killed them."
- **Chile 9/11 1973:** Per capita = White House bombed, president killed, 50–100k dead, 700k tortured, etc.
- **Polls (Harris 50% Iraq had WMD):** Kull: must have had reason for invasion; rather believe Cheney.
- **Israel lobby:** Chomsky reluctant; underestimates if = those seeking to influence for Israeli policies; main component = U.S. intellectual community and media; AIPAC doesn't write NYT editorials; post-1967 shift (Nasser destroyed, Dayan to Vietnam jokes, Ocean Hill–Brownsville, weapon vs New Left); Mearsheimer/Walt narrow to Jewish orgs; when interests conflict (Israel–China arms 2005) U.S. wins, lobby silent; "national interest" = Smith's principal architects; ExxonMobil, Lockheed, Intel, Buffett in Israel = fine.
- **Carter Palestine book:** Apartheid used in Ha'aretz, B'Tselem; error = 1982 invasion as reaction to PLO (fabrication; Israel elicited; Porath: invasion = war for West Bank); important = first mainstream to report U.S.–Israel 14 reservations eviscerated road map; three conditions (recognize, renounce violence, accept agreements): U.S. and Israel reject all three.

## Chapter 7 – Threats (Mar 2007)

- **UN climate report (Feb 2):** Unequivocal warming, very likely human; 11 of 12 years 1995–2006 in top 12 since 1850.
- **Nuclear:** Disaster always imminent; Bulletin doomsday clock 5 to midnight; Shultz, Kissinger; proliferation partly from U.S. belligerence; North Korea "misread" like Iraq; main = stocks in great powers; U.S. initiatives.
- **Avian flu:** Could leap to human; hundreds of millions at risk; infrastructure needed; not enough done.
- **Water:** Glaciers, desertification; poor suffer; World Bank studies.
- **Global governance:** Would need cooperative action; great powers won't concede sovereignty; depends on U.S. becoming functioning democracy; population might accept less sovereignty; majority for UN, even giving up veto (no whisper in elite).
- **ExxonMobil:** Supporting research to show flaws in climate science; CEOs have grandchildren; institutional role = short-term profit.
- **Auto companies:** Knew, didn't prepare; huge vehicles; eastern Congo worse than Darfur (Mamdani, LRB); Darfur = convenient (Arab perpetrator).
- **Iraq effect (Bergen et al.):** "Sevenfold increase in yearly rate of fatal jihadist attacks"; Rand database; little in mainstream.
- **Iran:** Short-term gain (shift from Iraq) may trap into attack; North Korea Sep 2005 agreement (dismantle for end hostility, reactor, normalization); days later U.S. froze assets, killed deal; now back to similar; China ASAT = predictable (missile defense = first-strike in adversary view); Putin Munich = Russia security (Gorbachev 1990, NATO not east; Clinton broke it); missile defense in E Europe "against Iran" = Russia sees first-strike; China at UN for space for peaceful use; U.S. blocks.
- **Media/Internet:** Flood of material, cult formation (9/11 style); need to know what to look for.
- **Archives:** Open better; classification = state vs population (Iran planning secret from Americans not Iran).
- **Community radio:** Where it exists, more organized (Boston atomized); Democracy Now!, Alternative Radio.
- **Draft/collapse:** Evidence slim; 1969 70% "wrong and immoral" not "mistake"; no collapse in 1960s or 1980s when activism high.
- **Manufacturing Consent (2002 ed.):** "Anti-communism" too narrow; now = "Islamic terrorism" (we're inciting); Gerges: jihadi leadership condemned bin Laden post-9/11; Bush reunited them; Scheuer: Bush = bin Laden's "only indispensable ally."
- **Huntington "clash":** As history ridiculous; Christianity more violent; Saudi = oldest ally; Indonesia (Suharto); Catholic Church (Reagan wars); some creating clash = bin Laden and Bush.
- **Orwell 1984:** We're not that society; very free; state has little power to coerce.

## Chapter 8 – What We Can Do (Mar 2007)

- **"What we say goes":** Bush I Feb 1991, "new world order," main principle; Powell to UN on Iraq: go along or be "irrelevant"; Bolton "no United Nations"; invasion against overwhelming opposition.
- **Vietnam:** Major war vs South; protest about North; South = what we say goes (triple scale by 1965); McNamara memoirs detail North, don't mention jet escalation South; Cambodia/Laos defenseless.
- **Herman:** U.S. aid flows to torturers (Schoultz 1981); Colombia leading recipient and worst rights; Israel/Egypt separate; Turkey 1990s; Colombia "drug war" = chemical warfare, displacement.
- **Grand Area:** WWII planning (State, CFR); 1939–43 Germany+U.S.; by 1943–44 Grand Area = as much as U.S. could dominate; liberal international order, U.S. corps access resources/markets; extended Monroe Doctrine.
- **Guantánamo:** Taken at gunpoint; Cuba refused payment (Castro); used for refugees then "outside law"; if not for torture why not New York?
- **Darfur:** Powell slow; convenient (Arab perpetrator); Congo worse; no proposals to act.
- **Israel borders:** Never defined; expanding with U.S. support; wall, settlements, water; "right to exist" = mid-1970s when Arabs/PLO had accepted 1949 borders; 1976 resolution (Syria, Jordan, Egypt, PLO) for Palestinian state in occupied territories; U.S. vetoed.
- **Eqbal Ahmad:** Take risks; love of people. Chomsky: risks here minimal vs world; privilege = "intellectual"; being decent.
- **Education:** Individuals can't; local (unions, workers' education); civil rights = wave King rode; same for Johnson, women's movement.
- **Failed States suggestions:** ICC, World Court, Kyoto, UN lead, Article 51 self-defense only, cut military and raise social spending.
- **Vida Urbana:** 34 years, Boston poor (Latino, black); many groups atomized; power succeeded in separating people.

## Predictions and Warnings

- **Iran strike:** Would strengthen hard-liners, drive program underground; could take Khuzestan, bomb rest; region prefers nuclear Iran to military action; blow up region.
- **Short-term thinking:** State/corporate focus on short-term (ExxonMobil, auto, Britain/Japan 1932); Iraq undertaken knowing terror and proliferation would rise; North Korea 2005 deal then U.S. undermined.
- **China/Russia:** Anti-missile in E Europe, space = arms race, accident risk.
- **Organizing:** Must be local, collective; community radio, unions; "organizers' paradise" but atomized.
