### 2. The "Plot" – Policy Papers and the "Superb Offer" Trap

- **Wesley Clark (cited):** "We're going to take out seven countries in five years, starting with Iraq, and then Syria, Lebanon, Libya, Somalia, Sudan and, finishing off, Iran."
- **Brookings "Which Path to Persia?" (2009):** Kovalik quotes Cartalucci: US policymakers pledged to propose then intentionally betray a "superb offer" so that "only a regime determined to acquire nuclear weapons and acquire them for the wrong reasons would turn it down." Then "the United States (or Israel) could portray its operations as taken in sorrow, not anger" and "the Iranians 'brought it on themselves' by refusing a very good deal."
- **The trap mechanism:** The US will intentionally offer a deal it knows Iran cannot accept—such as rewriting JCPOA conditions to be deliberately unacceptable. When Iran rejects it, the US and Israel will claim they are acting "in sorrow, not anger," using Iran's rejection as proof that Iran is acquiring nukes "for the wrong reasons." (Ch. 1)
- **Relevance to 2026:** Pre-war diplomacy (Oman, offers) and then attack mirrors the "superb offer" logic: present a deal, blame Iran for intransigence, then strike. Trump withdrew from the JCPOA and demanded conditions Iran could not accept; when Iran refused, war followed. Iran's position (e.g. Araghchi: only "permanent end to war" acceptable after prior ceasefire was "exploited") fits the narrative of "they refused a good deal."

Vilification of Iran (terrorism, nuclear threat) remained the public justification for the war.

### 4. Historical Context That Explains 2026

- **1953 coup:** CIA (Kermit Roosevelt) and UK overthrew Mossadegh, installed Shah; Iranians remember. "The United States has, through decades of meddling, helped make Iran what it is today."
- **Reagan and the hostages:** Reagan/CIA/Israel "derailed" Carter's efforts to free hostages; deal to hold them until after Reagan's inauguration; then Reagan encouraged Saddam to invade Iran (1980).
- **Iran-Iraq War:** US at times supported both sides; Saddam's chemical weapons from Germany and US knowledge/acquiescence; ~1 million dead. Iran views US as having waged war by proxy.
- **MEK:** US support for MEK (de-listed 2012 under Obama); MEK used for intel and pressure; Bolton addressed MEK in Paris 2017. MEK and Israeli-linked assassination of scientists (e.g. at Imamzadeh-Saleh, Tehran) cited in book.
- **Libya model:** Gaddafi gave up nukes, was then invaded and killed. "The only way a weaker state can protect itself against the United States is to have a nuclear deterrent" (Van Creveld, cited). Iran gave up enrichment under JCPOA and got no security; then Trump withdrew and war came.
- **Relevance to 2026:** Iranian refusal to trust US offers and insistence on "permanent end to war" (not just ceasefire) reflect this history. Decapitation (Khamenei killed) did not produce regime collapse; military had "pre-planned" contingencies (Arafi, IRIB).

### 7. No Real Nuclear Threat

- **IAEA and US NIE:** 2003 and 2007 IAEA concluded Iran did not intend to build a nuclear weapon; 16 US agencies in NIE stated with "high confidence" that if Iran ever had a weapons program, it halted it in fall 2003.
- **Khamenei fatwa:** Supreme Leader issued fatwa against production, stockpiling, and use of nuclear weapons as contrary to Islam.
- **Van Creveld (cited):** Iran would be "insane" not to try to develop nukes given US forces on all sides and Iraq precedent; but that is deterrent logic, not evidence of an active weapon program.
- **Relevance to 2026:** War was justified publicly as preventing a nuclear weapon; no weapon existed; Iran had complied with JCPOA until US withdrawal. Post-strike, Iran has threatened escalation (e.g. $200 oil) and introduced new systems (Etemad missile), but no nuclear use.

### 8. Path Forward (Conclusion) – What Kovalik Recommends

- **Recognize Iran as sovereign:** "Same rights as any country ... to exist and to exercise the type of government its people wish" (UN Charter).
- **Acknowledge US harm:** "It is the United States which has caused immensely more suffering to Iran than they have or even could cause to us." Acknowledging this "makes it much harder to vilify Iran."
- **No quick fix:** "There is no quick fix to Iran's problems, especially in the way of another Western-backed coup."
- **Iranian people decide:** "It is up to the Iranian people themselves to make political change—and not up to foreign governments." Sanctions and meddling "only bring more suffering and more government repression."
- **Tudeh Party (cited):** Against foreign intervention and US use of protests for regime change; "replace the current reactionary regime with another reactionary regime" is the US/Israeli aim.
- **Chomsky (cited):** Sanctions "hurt the people" and "undermine democracy and freedom"; US is "looking for an excuse" to impose pressure, which "make the situation more militarized."
- **Carter Doctrine obsolete:** US does not need Gulf oil for supply (plenty of oil; climate demands alternatives); real goal is "controlling the world's energy supplies" for profit. "Any rationale for the Carter Doctrine ... does not exist now."
- **Military threat overblown:** "The alleged military threat Iran poses to the United States and its partner Israel are greatly overblown, if not utterly nonexistent."
