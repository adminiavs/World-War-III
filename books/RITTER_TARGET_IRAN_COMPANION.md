## Main Arguments and Claims

1. **Iran crisis is "made in Israel."** Israeli focus on Iran (not Iraq); NCRI August 2002 briefing was Israeli intel; goal regime change in Tehran; U.S. follows Israeli red lines.
2. **Same playbook as Iraq.** Regime change disguised as disarmament; opposition groups (Chalabi/INC vs NCRI/MEK) used to feed intel; WMD narrative hyped; IAEA facts ignored when inconvenient.
3. **No evidence of Iranian nuclear weapons program.** IAEA found no evidence of weapons program; HEU/LEU traces explained by contamination from imported equipment; Iran had safeguard breaches (late declarations, Kalaye tests) but no proof of weaponization.
4. **U.S. policy is regime change.** Sanctions, referral to Security Council, and "all options on the table" serve removal of theocracy; EU incentives are tactical pause, not strategic change.
5. **Israel lobby drives U.S. toward war.** AIPAC and likeminded groups push Congress and public; Bolton described as "sixth Israeli diplomat" at UN; U.S. interests subordinated to Israeli preferences on Iran.
6. **War with Iran would be catastrophic.** Oil shock, Strait of Hormuz, attacks on Israel and U.S. forces in Iraq, Hezbollah/Hamas escalation, possible nuclear use; U.S. economy and standing would suffer severely.
7. **EU/Russia/China can restrain but are unreliable.** EU-3 diplomacy and Russian/Chinese vetoes slow referral; U.S. can still force confrontation; EU did not deliver on security guarantees to Iran (per Chomsky in other source).

## Predictions and Warnings

- **Path to war:** "The path that the United States is currently embarked on regarding Iran ... will inevitably lead to war" (prologue); "history repeat itself" (Iraq WMD false premise).
- **Worse than Iraq:** Such a course "will make even the historical mistake we made in Iraq pale by comparison."
- **Oil and economy:** Iranian oil off market plus Gulf disruption could push world to negative capacity; oil $150–200/bbl; hyperinflation; "economic rock bottom."
- **Military:** No Osirak-style single target; many dispersed sites; strike would escalate to regional war; ground invasion would bog down; risk of nuclear escalation.
- **Israel:** Iran would strike Israel with missiles; Hezbollah would rocket north Israel; Hamas/PLO front; Israeli casualties and economic damage severe.
- **Postscript (July 2006):** Israel–Hezbollah conflict framed as proxy U.S.–Iran war; NCRI/MEK job "done"; next step could be hyping Iran again for domestic U.S. politics.

## Technical and Treaty Content

- **NPT Article IV:** Right to peaceful nuclear energy and fuel cycle; Iran's legal right to enrich under safeguards.
- **IAEA Safeguards:** Declare nuclear material and facilities; Additional Protocol (93+2) for broader access and undeclared sites.
- **Enrichment:** Centrifuges (P-1 URENCO-type, P-2); uranium hexafluoride (UF6); LEU (e.g. 3.5%) for power, HEU (e.g. 90%+) for weapons; cascade stages.
- **Sites:** Natanz (PFEP, FEP), Arak (heavy water), Kalaye Electric (centrifuge components/tests), Esfahan (conversion), Lavizan-Shian (civil defense/WBCs), Parchin (military, disputed).
- **HEU/LEU traces:** Multiple origins (Russian 36%, Pakistani, indigenous 1.2%); Iran attributed to imported equipment; not fully resolved in book timeline.
- **Polonium-210:** Raised by IAEA (beryllium + polonium as neutron initiator); Iran said 1991 feasibility for RTGs; Ritter calls it red herring.
