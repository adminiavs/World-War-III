## Introduction (Barsamian) – Important Points

- **Geography:** Iran borders Gulf, Arabian Sea, Afghanistan, Pakistan, Iraq, Turkey, Caucasus, Caspian; "stupendous source of strategic power" (1945 State Dept).
- **1953 coup:** Eisenhower approved; Mossadegh nationalized oil; CIA (Kermit Roosevelt) and UK; shah restored; "Ajax"; 1979 revolution and hostage crisis cannot be understood without it; Huyser 1979 attempt.
- **Axis of evil (2002):** Iran had just helped against Taliban; Khatami damaged; Iran grouped with Iraq (which had waged war on Iran).
- **May 2003 Iranian offer (via Swiss):** Comprehensive: nuclear, Israel/Palestine, Hamas/Hezbollah, al-Qaeda; Bush admin did not reply; Swiss ambassador censured (Leverett, Unger/Vanity Fair).
- **NPT:** Iran is signatory and has right to enrich; U.S. demands halt before negotiations; Israel, India, Pakistan have nukes and are non-NPT; U.S. obliged to draw down arsenal.
- **Carriers:** Eisenhower Strike Group (Oct 2006), Stennis (Feb 2007) off Iran; "Give us what a negotiation would produce before the negotiations start."
- **EFPs:** U.S. accuses Iran of supplying EFPs in Iraq; parallel to WMD justification for Iraq war.
- **BBC/New Statesman (Feb 2007):** Two triggers for U.S. action: (a) confirmation Iran developing nuke, (b) high-casualty attack on U.S. forces blamed on Iran.
- **Sunnis vs Shiites:** Bush policy backing Sunni Arab bloc (Saudi, Jordan, Egypt, Fatah, Israel) vs Iran, Syria, "radical" groups; Hersh "The Redirection": U.S. bolstering Sunni extremists, clandestine ops in Iran, ethnic tensions (Azeris, Kurds, Arabs, Baluchis); Saudi money.
- **Wahhabism:** Anti-Shia; Karbala 1802; Saudi funding; Nasr on Shia–Sunni conflict.
- **Ebadi:** War would unite Iranians behind regime; "Democracy cannot be brought to a people with cluster bombs."
- **Hafez couplet (Ebadi):** "If there is no justice, then those who are deprived may one day take to the streets and rise up."

## Chapter 1 – Noam Chomsky: Main Arguments

- **Continuity of U.S. policy:** Over half a century; 1953 coup, shah, 1979, Carter/Huyser, Iran–Contra, support for Saddam in 1980–88 war (Rumsfeld to Baghdad), WMD to Iraq; "Iran disobeyed orders."
- **1982:** Reagan took Iraq off terror list to aid Iraq (WMD, nukes, chem) vs Iran; Saddam executed for 1982 crimes; Iranians remember.
- **Israel:** "Offshore U.S. military base"; 100+ jet bombers to Israel, "special weapons"; Iran surrounded by U.S. (Afghanistan, Iraq, Gulf, Turkey); Israel has hundreds of nukes; Iran surrounded by hostile forces.
- **Deterrence:** Invasion of Iraq was "plea to Iran to develop nuclear weapons"; Martin van Creveld (IHT): if Iran is not seeking nukes they are "crazy."
- **EU–Iran bargain (three years before interview):** Iran stop enrichment (legally entitled) in return for EU security guarantees (i.e. no U.S./Israeli attack); EU under U.S. pressure did not deliver; Iran then resumed enrichment.
- **Kissinger:** Under shah, Iran "needed" nuclear energy; now "must be" developing weapons; "ally then" vs "broken out of U.S. control."
- **MIT–shah deal:** MIT sold nuclear engineering to shah; students opposed, faculty approved; Kissinger supported.
- **China:** Not intimidated; European companies pulled out of Iran (fear of U.S.); China invests; U.S. "terrified" of China; Iran may join Asian energy grid (Shanghai Cooperation Organization, Russia, Central Asia, India, South Korea); Iran as lynchpin; Saudi–China deals; Shiite bloc (Iran, Iraq, Shiite Saudi areas) with most of world oil, not subordinate to U.S., as "ultimate nightmare."
- **Moqtada al-Sadr:** If U.S./Israel attack Iran, Mahdi Army could join against U.S. in Iraq.
- **UN Charter:** Threat of force prohibited; U.S./UK claim anticipatory self-defense (threat alone allows attack); Chirac similar claim; "imperial mentality."
- **Polls (e.g. 57% back hit on Iran):** Propaganda instills fear; people still believe Iraq had WMD after concession; same for Iran.
- **ElBaradei proposal:** Fissile production under international control; Iran alone accepted (via Larijani); U.S. refuses.
- **2003 Khatami offer:** Comprehensive negotiations; Bush did not reply; censured Swiss diplomat; U.S. refuses serious negotiation; Rice "shift" = negotiate only if Iran concedes outcome first (stop enrichment) with "gun pointed."
- **Cost of oil:** Cavallo study: military subsidy ~30% of market price; policy serves "power sectors," not population.
- **Trade deficit:** If U.S. = owners (Dell in China = U.S. exports), deficit "way down"; "country" = who owns it.
- **Institutional role:** Corporate execs maximize profit (legal obligation); officials act in institutional role even if it endangers their own families; MIT faculty vs students on Iran nuke deal.
- **Iraq WMD:** Duelfer/Kay; materials were there (from West); inspectors kicked out; sites looted (100+); high-precision tools, biotoxins "went somewhere"; Jordan border: 1 in 8 trucks from Iraq tested positive for radioactive material; "Stuff happens."
- **Strike on Iran:** Gardiner (war games): "totally insane"; likely not just nuke sites (unknown) but "carpet bombing" except Gulf oil area; secession in Arab Shiite area; "Anything that flies or anything that moves" (Kissinger/Cambodia); new generations of jihadis; provocations and naval deployments could lead to war.
- **Strait of Hormuz:** If Iran chokes off, hawks might want excuse to bomb/occupy oil areas; Vincennes 1988 (Iran Air 655, 290 dead); Bush I never apologize.

## Chapter 2 – Ervand Abrahamian: Main Arguments

- **Iraq vs Iran:** Iraq = neocons planned and got war; Iran = neocons "no longer in driver's seat"; Committee on Present Danger (June 2004) paper: not out to invade Iran; talk of negotiations, embassy, diplomacy; "rational" enough to see they cannot occupy.
- **Danger = miscalculation:** Most modern wars from miscalculation, not plan; both sides misjudge, brinkmanship, loss of face; ultimatums in Iranian history = traitors who give in, heroes who resist; "full nuclear cycle" as birthright; if U.S. ultimatums and harsh sanctions, Iran may withdraw from NPT and go full speed; U.S. might then "limited" air strikes; Iran would hit back in Iraq and Afghanistan (Sadr, Shia militias); U.S. could not handle Sunni + Shia revolt with 130k troops; need 500k and draft; incremental escalation.
- **Irreconcilable difference:** Iran: right to full cycle, no bomb; U.S.: under no condition permit full cycle; ElBaradei/Russia once suggested full cycle with intrusive monitoring and no HEU for bombs; Washington refused.
- **Origins of program:** Shah era; U.S. and West supported; revolution put on back burner; reasons now: national prestige (cutting-edge science), energy (import refined oil, preserve oil), "Japanese option" (ability to build bomb if needed, deterrent option); 1980–88 war: no help from UN on chemical weapons; half million in trenches; U.S. blamed Iran for Halabja initially; Iran wants self-reliance/deterrent option.
- **Internal debate:** Conservatives = hard line, defense; reformers = dialogue, Khatami; Khatami tried pause for deal; U.S. "offer" was insult (no regime-change guarantee, no sanctions lift); U.S. helped hard-liners; reformers splintered; Ahmadinejad won.
- **Dissidents:** Ebadi, Ganji; oppose government but distance from Bush; no Chalabi/Allawi figures; White House invited opposition, almost no one came; Reza Pahlavi "previous engagement"; Ebadi and Ganji oppose Bush policies.
- **India NPT:** Bush–India deal legitimizes Indian nukes; double standard; India could have got Middle East oil more easily.
- **1980–88 war:** ~500,000 Iranian dead; chemicals not main killer; trench warfare; U.S. narrative (Iran used gas on own troops, then Halabja = Iran) until Saddam became "bad guy" (Kuwait 1990).
- **Hostage crisis:** Residual "get even"; can be "tapped" for public if needed; neocon disaster = 1979 revolution; want to "undo" it.
- **1953 coup:** Anyone close to U.S. seen as coup conspiracy.
- **Alternatives:** CFR (Takeyh, Pollack) "Taking on Tehran": right conditions could bring Iran to negotiations, limit to no bomb; U.S. did opposite; Bolton: 30 days or Chapter 7; rhetoric (expansionist, totalitarian, mother of terrorism, WWIV, mortal struggle) sabotages negotiation.
- **Political structure:** Constitution merges popular sovereignty and Khomeini's clerical sovereignty (vilayat-e faqih); pluralistic, oligarchical; supreme leader (Khamenei) can overrule president, key appointments, vet candidates; Assembly of Experts (86) elects supreme leader; can remove him; "republican and Islamic."
- **Vilayat-e faqih:** Khomeini's politicization of clerical authority; twentieth-century invention, not traditional Shia.
- **Ahmadinejad:** Layman, populist, working class; style/rhetoric compared to Bush; axis-of-evil speech undercut reformers and helped hard-liners.
- **Supreme leader vs president:** Khomeini dismissed Bani-Sadr; Khamenei can overrule on key issues.
- **Why confront now:** Both sides overconfident; U.S. has Europe on board, elite unity, India/Brazil at UN, military superiority; Iran has Iraq/Afghanistan leverage, regional ties; after Baghdad fall Iran offered to negotiate; U.S. refused (planning to target Iran next).

## Predictions and Warnings (across book)

- **War from miscalculation:** Brinkmanship, ultimatums, sanctions, limited strikes could spiral; Iran hits back in Iraq/Afghanistan; U.S. could need 500k troops and draft (Abrahamian).
- **Strike consequences:** Carpet bombing except Gulf; secession in oil area; Kissinger-style "anything that moves"; new jihadis; terror (Chomsky).
- **Asian energy grid:** U.S. may drive Iran into China/Russia/SCO system; Iran as lynchpin; Shiite bloc (Iran, Iraq, Shiite Saudi) controlling oil, possibly diversifying out of dollars (Chomsky).
- **Propaganda and fear:** Public pre-programmed to accept war with Iran like Iraq WMD (Chomsky); axis-of-evil hurt reformers and helped hard-liners (Abrahamian).
- **No serious U.S. negotiation:** Refusal to reply to 2003 offer; demand for pre-concession; "gun pointed" (Chomsky).
