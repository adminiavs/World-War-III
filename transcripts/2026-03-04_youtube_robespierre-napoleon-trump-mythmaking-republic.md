## tl;dr

Conclusion comparing great figures. Robespierre: power through virtue and dedication (18 hours a day, no girlfriend, no money, unpaid), saved the French Revolution, "prophet of reason" (everyone can reason; logical argument), could not imagine friends conspiring for self-interest (source of downfall). Napoleon: power through political maneuvering (patrons, coup d'état, betrayed allies), great politician not just general; mythmaking genius, presented himself as Messiah, destroyed the French Republic. Pattern: figures like Napoleon appear at the end of a republic (Caesar: same pattern, destroyed Roman Republic; Hitler: same, outmaneuvered patrons, Nazis incubated by army vs communists, myth as savior). If Trump destroys the American republic in the next ~10 years, the pattern holds and we can foresee history. Why Napoleon succeeded where Robespierre failed: Robespierre believed people must reason for themselves; Napoleon believed people want to believe, obey, follow a Messiah; confidence and charisma; Trump same (mythology, people willing to die for it). Mythmaking: reality vs myth; Trump as failed businessman (Fred Trump built empire, Trump almost bankrupted it); people want mythology of success (Apprentice, beautiful women); Trump turned politics into a TV show; perception more important than reality; Trump creating a religion about himself; MAGA as new religion; hard to remove him because the "TV show" would end. Q: Tariff war will make Americans poorer. A: People think comparatively not absolutely; if the US declines but others decline more, people are happy; "make everyone else poor"; "Make America great again" = myth of being better than others; Trump wants the myth of a great America while the world is at war or bankrupt; he is right in that logic.
