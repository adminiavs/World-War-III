**Context:** Segment on Iran's new wave of retaliatory missiles toward Israel: strikes on Eilat (two attacks, visible from Aqaba/Jordan, 10 locations, 7 injured including 1 critical) and central Israel overnight (Tel Aviv area, military bases, Ben Gurion airport, military/intelligence assets — destruction but targets unknown). ## tl;dr

**Iran missiles / Israel:** Iran says it has launched a new wave of retaliatory missiles toward Israel. Strike in southern city of Eilat; explosions in central Israel; multiple attacks targeted central Israel overnight. Correspondent from Ramallah (occupied West Bank); "Israeli military censor has kept a very tight lid" on what is happening — "frustration of ordinary Israelis and certainly of us journalists."

**Eilat:** Two attacks on Eilat "really impossible to deny because of geography" — Aqaba (Jordan, Red Sea) is exactly opposite Eilat; people saw plumes of smoke; residents posted videos. Damage in 10 separate locations in that city. Local/medical authorities: seven people injured, one in critical condition.

**Central Israel:** Other salvos caused damage to property and buildings, especially central area — larger Tel Aviv area: military bases, Ben Gurion airport, other military and intelligence assets. "We saw destruction there but we do not know what the targets were." Continues to test Home Front Command's ability to ease restrictions; "perhaps by next week 100 people might be able to gather in any one place so long as there are public shelters up to code that can accommodate them."

**Macron / Lebanon:** French president calling for de-escalation and mediating between Israel and Lebanon. "French proposal whereby Lebanon would recognize Israel in exchange for Israel withdrawing from all Lebanese territory and the Lebanese army deploying to the border with Israel. This is set to be under consideration by the US and Israel." Israeli PM has assigned Ron Dermer (confidant) as mediator / go-between Israel and US on all things Lebanon.

**Litani / cabinet:** "This while the cabinet is considering sending more troops and giving the marching orders for the occupation of 10% of Lebanon all the way to the Litani River. Interestingly, 48 years ago today Israel did just that — occupy that part of southern Lebanon all the way to the Litani. That lasted only a few days and Israel was compelled by the UN Security Council to withdraw. No such pressure right now is felt by the Israeli government who doesn't even feel that Washington will really apply a lot of pressure on Israel to do something it's not comfortable with."
