## tl;dr

Iran strategy matrix: from now until invasion everything Iran does must achieve four goals at once. (1) Unite the population: Iranians divided (protests, women's rights) but most would resist invasion given bad history with the West; as US/Israel stay aggressive, population will coalesce. (2) Build alliances: Axis of Resistance (Shia militias Iraq/Syria, Hezbollah, Hamas, Houthis); alliance not full control (e.g. Oct 7, Iran said not involved). Need Russia and China: Russia to distract US and move focus from Ukraine, and to forbid US tactical nukes (we nuke you if you nuke Iran); China needs oil, cannot let US control Iraq and Iran. Iran must prove to Russia and China: we will fight, we will win. (3) Win global opinion: keep spotlight on Gaza (genocide, removal of Palestinians); campus protests in US. (4) Weaken the enemy: create conflict and dissent in US coalition (NATO, Middle East); most Americans don't support war on Iran; tension between NATO and US. Operation True Promise as example: unite (show population Iran can strike back), alliances (show Russia/China Iran will fight), global opinion (designed no casualties, restraint, measured response), weaken (Israel wanted disproportionate response, US restrained Israel, so Israel–US conflict). From military perspective accomplished nothing; from asymmetric strategy accomplished all four goals.
