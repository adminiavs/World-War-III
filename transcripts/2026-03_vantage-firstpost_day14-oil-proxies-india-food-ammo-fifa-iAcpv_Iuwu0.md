## tl;dr

**War status:** Day 14, heading into week three. Dubai hit again (industrial area, DIFC; UAE cracking down on strike footage). Israel hit (60 injured overnight); Iran hit Erbil base (France–Iraqi Kurds), one French soldier killed. Netanyahu first press conference: create conditions for regime change; "wouldn't issue life insurance" on terrorist leaders (Mojtaba). Lebanon: 680+ dead (98 children), 14% evacuation order, ground invasion likely; UN SG in Beirut. Israel struck Shiraz missile facility, Tehran air defence, Avaz command center; US claims 15,000 targets hit. Hegseth: Iran "hiding like rats," "don't need to worry about" Hormuz — but same day Iranian leadership (president, FM, judiciary chief, NSC) on Tehran streets, Quds Day march. US general more cautious: Iran retains capability to harm allies and shipping; escort "tactically complex," want to degrade first to avoid mass casualties; Trump says Navy will escort anyway — "chaotic."

**Oil:** 20 million bpd shortfall from Hormuz closure; two weeks = 200M+ barrels lost. IEA/emergency reserves: JP Morgan says best case +1.2M bpd — "barely a dent." Russia waiver: India bought 30M barrels Russian crude in days; US ambassador praised India for "global oil price stability" (reversal from last year tariffs). Russia produces 6.5M bpd across 40 refineries — cannot fill 20M gap. "30-day waiver like a band-aid on a gunshot wound."

**KC-135 Iraq:** Six dead. CENTCOM: not shot down, refueling mishap. Islamic Resistance in Iraq: we shot it down ("appropriate weapon"), no proof. 11 US dead, 140 wounded, four aircraft lost (three F-15 friendly fire, one tanker). Question: enemy action or chaos of crowded war zone?

**Proxies:** Hezbollah in (heavy casualties, displacement). Iraq Shiite militias in (drones/rockets at US bases, limited effect). Hamas sidelined (Gaza devastation). Houthis holding back — calculated; past US/Israeli campaigns hit them hard; may open Red Sea/shipping front later. "Axis of resistance" not moving in lockstep; "members calculating survival before committing to wider war."

**India:** Modi first wartime call with Pezeshkian (Day 13): safety of Indians, unhindered transit of goods/energy, dialogue and diplomacy. Jaishankar–Araghchi fourth call, second in three days. Iran wants BRICS statement and political support (India chairs BRICS; June 2025 BRICS had condemned US-Israeli attack). Indian sailors: IRIS Lavan (Kochi) 183 crew to be repatriated to Iran; US pressed Sri Lanka not to repatriate (hoping for defections). India wants: safe passage Hormuz (half crude, 90% LPG from Gulf); stability for 10M Indian workers in Gulf. No confirmed safe passage from Iran yet (Indian sources claimed pass, Iranian sources denied). India could mediate if escalation continues.

**Food/fertilizer:** Gulf = 20% global fertilizer trade, nearly half of world's traded urea. Ras Laffan hit; Qatar Energy halted production; hundreds of thousands of tons fertilizer sidelined. Third major food-security shock in six years (COVID, Ukraine, now). Fertilizer prices up 10–30%. Energy ≈ 50% of food cost; oil up + fertilizer scarce = lower yields. India up to 2/3 nitrogen from Gulf; monsoon planting approaching. Brazil 40% nitrogen from Gulf. Africa especially vulnerable. Gulf states themselves produce almost no food; emergency stocks finite. "Hormuz is also a food choke point."

**US ammunition:** "Missile war" — 168 Tomahawks in first 100 hours (~$600M); Patriot ~$4M per interceptor vs $30K drones (100:1 cost exchange). US bought 322 Tomahawks in five years, used bulk in two weeks. THAAD: ~11 produced/year; 25% stockpile gone in June 2025 12-day war. Pentagon: stockpiles strong; lawmakers asking how fast used, how fast replaced, what if another crisis. War cost $11B+ in ~6 days, mostly munitions.

**US domestic:** Michigan: truck through Temple Israel (140 children inside); attacker Ghazali, family killed in Israeli strike Lebanon; FBI treating as targeted attack on Jewish community. Virginia: ODU classroom, 1 dead 2 wounded; shooter ex–National Guard, prior ISIS material-support plea; "Allahu Akbar"; FBI terrorism. "War overseas no longer just where it is happening."

**FIFA:** Iran qualified for 2026 World Cup (US/Canada/Mexico). Iran sports minister: may not participate ("assassinated our leader"). Trump: welcome then "not appropriate for their safety." Iran team: no one can exclude us; FIFA governs. FIFA: qualified teams must participate; withdrawal = penalties, possible ban. No precedent for withdrawal this late.

**Other:** Thailand energy crunch — PM asked save electricity; anchors removed jackets on air. Deep fake of Palki re Bahrain/Mossad arrest — "this never happened"; "war fought with videos too."
