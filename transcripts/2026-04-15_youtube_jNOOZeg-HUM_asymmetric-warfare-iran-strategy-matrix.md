## tl;dr

Iran must be strategic; US and Israel have military dominance but that does not mean they win. Iran and Israel committed to war; possible ground invasion of Iran in about two years. 2002 Millennium Challenge: Team USA vs Team Iran; Iran won. Asymmetric warfare: define terms of engagement (dark forest, traps vs armor and machine gun); carriers $1B vs drone swarms (~$20M); True Promise cost Iran ~$10–30M, Israel ~$1B to defend. Q: Shouldn't Americans respond with more strategic thinking? Second Millennium run: US declared asymmetric warfare "cheating," refight, US won; empires inflexible, hubris; Vietnam example. Iran strategy matrix: from now until invasion Iran must achieve four goals with every move: (1) unite the population (2) build alliances (3) win global opinion (4) weaken the enemy.
