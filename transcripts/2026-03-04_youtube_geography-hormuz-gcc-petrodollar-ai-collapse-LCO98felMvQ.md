## tl;dr

Geographic-strategic overview of the Iran war. The Strait of Hormuz (33 km wide) is the pivot: 20% of world oil flows through to Asia (India 60%, China 40%, Japan 75% dependent); Japan's PM said if Hormuz closes the economy collapses in 8–9 months. GCC gets 80% of food from overseas through the Strait; Iran has closed it, so the GCC faces starvation. Petrodollar: GCC sells oil for USD and recycles it into US markets; if the GCC collapses, the American economy and empire collapse. Iran = mountain fortress (rockets, drones, missiles hidden); GCC = flat desert, exposed. Iran targets (1) US bases (indefensible), (2) oil/energy (drones easily destroy), (3) desalination (60% of GCC water). Iran's weakness: drought; US/Israel plan is to destroy Iranian water (dams, reservoirs, power plants) to make Iran uninhabitable and force rebellion or refugee crisis. "Game of chicken": both can destroy each other; Iran (Shia, martyrdom, Khamenei killed) willing to go far; GCC materialistic, 90% of Dubai foreigners who will flee. Open questions: (1) Will US send ground troops (only way to defeat Iran)? (2) Nuclear weapons if losing? (3) If Europe (Germany, France, Britain) enters, Russia and China may enter on Iran's side = world war. Seven US companies (Nvidia, Microsoft, Google, Apple, etc.) account for ~quarter of stock market growth and are heavily invested in by GCC; if GCC cannot invest, those collapse and the US faces depression.
