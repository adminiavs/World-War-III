## Full transcript

O my dear compatriots, Persian speakers wherever you are in the world who are seeing and hearing this: I am not giving a speech. I am far too small a person to deliver a message to a great nation. This is only a heartfelt sharing with those who feel this pain. More clearly, I want to speak about two of the worries that, these days, are wearing down the souls of us Iranians, especially those outside the country.

The first, and I apologize if it needs to be said, is the damage that at times the words, manner of speaking, and public presence of the lawful president of this country, Mr. Pezeshkian, inflict on the image of great Iran and its brave people. No one expects the president of a country at war with predatory world powers to have every single word and phrase perfectly correct, measured, and dignified. But that is exactly why networks of literary, cultural, and political advisers exist around such officials everywhere in the world. And of course, every day we also witness the absurd nonsense and rambling of President Trump, but that is not what we want for our own president, and we do not accept it.

Mr. President, on behalf of what nation did you apologize for the brave and necessary response of this country's military forces to the nests of American military aggression? Mr. Pezeshkian, perhaps not all people have fully grasped why this totalitarian power maintains more than 750 to 800 military bases across the world, in more than 50 countries. But you know the reason. You know it is so that whenever it decides to attack a country, it already has the necessary bases surrounding that country. You know how much more economical it is to attack from neighboring territory than to send jets from Washington or New York and refuel them repeatedly. The matter is very simple. It has this ability because it buys, and has bought, the leaders of neighboring countries and many weak states around the world. So to which leader are you apologizing, when that leader has handed over his country, in exchange for benefits for himself and his family, to aggressors attacking the sovereignty of neighboring nations?

Mr. Pezeshkian, you are the lawful president of this country, and your legitimacy comes from that very fact. We speak to you on the basis of that same legality. The essence of the responsibility and authority entrusted to you is the protection of the independence, territorial integrity, and dignity of the people of this land. What you said today immediately brought Trump to the threshold he wanted: he responded by issuing an order and a message of surrender for the Iranian nation and promising severe confrontation.

The second issue, so that this does not go on too long, is a pain that these days harms not only our standing in the intellectual society of the West, but also stains the struggle of humanity in the twenty-first century with disgrace. It is the actions of certain people acting in the name of Iran and Iranians. I speak about this a lot; I do not know whether I told you this before or not. A few days ago, when I had rushed out into the street in a hurry, one of my neighbors asked me, "Are you going to a demonstration?" He looked around nervously -- even Germans are still cautious about saying the name of that fake aggressor regime -- and then he said, "But why are you carrying the flag of Israel?" I truly wished the earth would open up and swallow me.

Just yesterday in Berlin, Iranians held a demonstration opposing Trump's criminal aggression against Iran, and that of his well-known lackeys. Yet there was again a group of a few dozen people around and behind us shouting slogans -- what slogans, really, more like chaos and noise -- mostly supporters of the Pahlavis, hurling insults. Even more shameful than that was that once again they were carrying the flag of that most fake of governments, that most tyrannical, illegitimate, and criminal gang in the Middle East formed after the creation of the League of Nations -- a regime that has mocked the dignity of twentieth-century humanity.

How can you carry the flag of such a country? You will not find a single German, Englishman, Western European, or American patriot -- let alone an intellectual -- willing to carry a flag that today is a symbol of war, killing, and child murder. Yet you carry it alongside the flag of your own country, in whatever twisted justification you choose, and appear in public with it. I personally saw the hatred in the voice of one of those German security guards -- whether police or some other official, I do not remember exactly, he wore a vest -- when he said with disgust: "These are supporters of Israel." Why do you do this to your own dignity and to the credibility of your country?

You take pride in Cyrus and in his charter of human rights, and consider yourselves heirs to that legacy, yet you carry the flag of a country that the International Court, the world court, despite all the blatant pressure from America and its allies, has been forced to accuse of crimes against humanity and the killing of children. What are you doing?

Treason, selling out the homeland -- in truth, no one is capable of selling a nation. What is it that you think you are selling? The homeland and the nation are not things that even billions beneath you could sell. What you are really selling, perhaps, is something of your own: your honor, your understanding, and your personal dignity.

Thank you, if you listened.
