## tl;dr

Lecture: how American meritocracy is "destroying the world." 1875: best universities in Germany (then France, UK); US invested in research, won WWII, imported German scientists, now Harvard/Yale/Princeton dominate. More students in college (1940 ~5% of males, now ~35%) but society more unequal: Gini coefficient up, social mobility down (most no longer do better than parents), top 1% control wealth; tuition up, wages flat, student debt (non-dischargeable, passes to children), teen depression (worse among middle-class/wealthy). Creators of meritocracy: James B. Conant (Harvard president, brought in SAT, scholarship students) and Henry Chauncey (dean, then ETS: SAT, TOEFL, AP, GRE); system benefits Harvard. 1940 90% of applicants accepted at Harvard, now ~5%; endowment ~$40B; most US billionaires and ultra-wealthy from Harvard/Ivy/MIT; Nature study: elite (generals, judges, senators, CEOs, etc.) overwhelmingly from same schools; Harvard graduates don't perceive their own dominance. Beyond degree: elite clubs (Porcellian, Ivy Club, Skull and Bones); 2004 Bush vs Kerry both Skull and Bones. 2008: economy collapsed under Ivy League–run banks/politics; Obama (Columbia, Harvard Law) promised hope and change, appointed Larry Summers (Harvard) and Tim Geithner (Dartmouth) to save Wall Street; banks bailed out, homeowners told "moral hazard"; same moral-hazard logic not applied to banks. Banks saved, people screwed; anger produced Trump. Obama mocked Trump at 2011 White House correspondents' dinner (Trump in audience); that humiliation motivated Trump to run. JD Vance (Hillbilly Elegy), Johnny Kim (Navy SEAL, Harvard Med, NASA astronaut): presented as puppets, no ideas of their own; Kim's trauma (father killed by police, domestic abuse, dissociative disorder) as driver of achievement. Conclusion: meritocracy creates extreme inequality, destroys schools (grades not learning), traumatized children, mental illness, death of American dream, wealth concentration, political division, corruption, destruction of American identity (globalization, immigration, woke), incompetence (COVID, $37T debt), soulless elite.
