## tl;dr

Lecture on structural reset in the global system: "Power is not disappearing. It is being redistributed." Post-1991, America is the only game in town; it creates a **new world order** based on global trade. Because the US controls the dollar, it controls **pricing** (the value of everything). A **price hierarchy** divides the world: bottom = **resources** (Russia, Africa, South America), then **manufacturing** (China), then **knowledge** (Europe), then **finance** (America). America offshores manufacturing to China and keeps finance (creating money to create more money). Inherent instabilities lead to the 2008 crisis; central banks at the BIS in Switzerland decide the only way to save the world is for China to spend money (infrastructure, Belt and Road); China takes on debt and demands a seat at the table as America's equal (Huawei, global competition). America says "screw you"; Trump 2016, US–China trade war, sanctions, blockade. 2022 Russia invades Ukraine: Russia is rejecting the price hierarchy; without resources the system fails. Russia and Ukraine together control one-third of the world's carbohydrates; Africa and the Middle East starve without their grain. The entire system is in jeopardy. **America is fighting to save its game** (the system, the dollar, high finance); Russia and China are trying to exert themselves. We are in a time of **game reset**. Q&A: America's purpose in the game is to let China use dollars so America can run the game; China doesn't play the same way. The US can't say "we're greedy and want China subservient," so it says middle class rise and democracy (fraud, hypocrisy). China wants to restructure the game to be more independent; the problem is America controls the game (can cut trade, limit market access). China must diversify, find markets and resources outside the US. **BRICS** is a different concept: not imposing a game on everyone but creating a system not so dominated by America.
