## tl;dr

IRIB special interview on day 10 with **Dr. Fuad Izadi**. The most important claims are: **(1)** Mojtaba Khamenei's selection creates wartime cohesion and Trump's public opposition may actually have helped consolidate support for him; **(2)** the US/Israeli war aim is framed not merely as regime change but as **partitioning Iran**, especially separating its **oil-rich regions** from the rest of the country; **(3)** Trump and Lindsey Graham are cited as evidence that US elites are thinking in terms of **oil control**, not Iranian welfare; **(4)** a ceasefire is acceptable only if it prevents repeated renewed attacks, which in Izadi's view requires imposing sufficiently high **human costs** on the US side; **(5)** the continuation or end of the war depends less on rhetoric than on whether Washington judges the costs, including casualties, oil above **$120**, and domestic pressure, to be intolerable.

---

## Summary (key claims and themes)

- **Succession as cohesion:** Izadi avoids detailed comment on Mojtaba's personal worldview but says wartime conditions make leadership continuity essential for national unity.
- **Trump backlash effect:** Trump's stated desire to influence or veto Iran's next leader is presented as politically counterproductive, strengthening support for the very candidate Washington opposed.
- **Partition thesis:** The interview explicitly frames US strategy as breaking off Iran's oil-rich regions rather than administering the whole country.
- **Oil-control motive:** Lindsey Graham's remarks and Trump's comments are presented as evidence that control of oil reserves is a central war incentive.
- **Iraq analogy:** Izadi argues any partitioned oil zone would become another externally controlled, unstable space like post-invasion Iraq.
- **Ceasefire skepticism:** A ceasefire is only worthwhile if it stops cyclical attacks; otherwise it merely gives the US/Israel time to rearm and strike again.
- **Cost-imposition logic:** The interview argues that raising American human and political costs is the most reliable way to deter renewed attacks.
- **Internal US pressure:** Congress, oil prices, and broader domestic dissatisfaction are all cited as constraints on Trump.
