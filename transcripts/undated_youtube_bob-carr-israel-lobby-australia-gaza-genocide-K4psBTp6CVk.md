## tl;dr

Interview with Bob Carr, former Australian Foreign Minister and longest-serving NSW Premier. Carr describes the "Israel Jewish lobby" in Australia as a "foreign influence operation" that puts Israel's interests above Australia's foreign policy. It is the only foreign-country lobby with offices in every capital city; it organises donations to elevate influence. He co-founded Labour Friends of Israel with Bob Hawke in 1977 ("the honourable gentile") but shifted gradually as he learned Palestinian stories and saw settlements designed to block a Palestinian state. As FM, he secured Australia's abstention (against Gillard's wishes) on upgrading Palestine's UN status; found caucus support was deep but hidden. References Kevin Rudd (on money/donations), Tony Burke (every discussion of Israel/Palestine someone raised campaign donations), and the AIPAC model of funding primary opponents of any critical US politician. Carr stood firm against lobby pressure over presenting a peace prize to Hanan Ashrawi; refused to be silenced. Calls what is happening in Gaza a genocide (80% housing destroyed, starvation as weapon, denial of water/medicine, forced transfers, death marches, shooting children). Says Netanyahu lied to him about supporting two states; settlements always intended to block Palestinian statehood. Australia should label settlement products; should move with like-minded countries (France, UK, Canada, NZ) on sanctions. Australia obligated to arrest Netanyahu if he visits (ICC signatory). Says Australian public opinion has shifted; Israel rendering itself a pariah state. Predicts marches double the size of the Harbour Bridge march. Says media campaigns defending Israel are "not working" and had no effect on the May election (uniform swings to Labour).
