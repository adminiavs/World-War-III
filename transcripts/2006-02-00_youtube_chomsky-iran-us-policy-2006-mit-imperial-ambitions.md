## tl;dr

February 2006 MIT interview with Noam Chomsky (Imperial Ambitions). US policy on Iran: Bush rhetoric (state sponsor of terrorism, Newsweek "next nuclear threat," Ahmadinejad). US policy consistent over half a century: 1953 coup (US/UK, restored Shah), Carter praising Shah; 1979 hostages the only event in US memory vs 25 years of terror for Iranians; US tried to destroy government (Carter NATO general, arming military via Israel/Saudi money); US supported Iraq's war on Iran (Reagan took Iraq off terrorist list 1982, Rumsfeld to Baghdad, aid for WMD/nuclear/chemical/biotoxins); Iran "disobeyed orders"; sanctions, threats, preparations; Israel as US offshore base, 100+ advanced bombers to Israel for bombing Iran, Iraq/Afghanistan surrounding Iran; Israel only serious nuclear power in region; invasion of Iraq = signal that you need deterrence (van Creveld: if Iran not developing nukes they're crazy). EU–Iran bargain two years earlier: Iran stop enriching (legally entitled under NPT), EU firm guarantees on security (US/Israeli threats); Europe reneged under US pressure; Iran then backed off; solution: reduce threats, implement bargain, integrate Iran, remove sanctions. China not intimidated (European firms pulled out, China invests); Bush may drive Iran into Asian energy security grid (China, Russia, India, S. Korea), Iran as lynchpin; China–Saudi deals; Iraq may end up Shiite-dominated, ties to Iran, Sadr (US made him major); nightmare for Washington: Shiite bloc (Iran, Iraq, Saudi Shiite/oil areas), Asian grid; Bush alienating Canada (NAFTA), Venezuela (2002 coup failed, Latin America backlash), Latin America kicking out IMF (Argentina, Bolivia). UN Charter: threat of force ruled out; US/UK claim anticipatory self-defense; James Traub "outlaws" (Saddam) but not US; US only state to reject World Court (Nicaragua); St Augustine/Alexander and pirate (emperor vs pirate); NYT on Pakistan strike "legitimately aimed"; single standard = subordination to power; Howard Friel/Richard Falk on NYT and international law (Iraq editorials never mentioned UN Charter). LA Times 57% back military hit on Iran; polls (Iraq WMD still believed); propaganda; Iran resumed enriching after Europe reneged on security guarantees, almost never reported in US. Tariq Ali: wounded beast more dangerous. Iraq: looting of WMD sites (US/UK-supplied), "stuff happens"; two policies (enrich friends, intimidate world); control of hydrocarbons, Brzezinski leverage; institutional role vs human (MIT students vs faculty on Iran nuclear deal). IAEA referral to Security Council; "UN" sanctions as US sanctions; Iran closing Gulf could give Pentagon excuse to bomb/occupy oil areas; USS Vincennes shot down Iranian airliner 290 dead. Howard Zinn: problem is civil obedience; resistance = recreate functioning democracy (Bolivia, Haiti).
