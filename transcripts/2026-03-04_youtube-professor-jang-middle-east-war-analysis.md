## tl;dr

Professor Jang, recording from Toronto, gives a first analysis of the escalating US-Israel-Iran conflict. He frames US intent as regime change via the same three pillars used in Iraq, Libya, Syria: decapitation of leadership, revolutionary intent through economic sabotage and destroyed infrastructure, and fomenting sectarian violence. He simplifies the strategy as bombs, propaganda, and money, but argues it will not work in Iran: geography (mountainous, large), Western media’s lack of credibility, and Iranian identity as Persians with a 5,000-year civilization and memory of what happened to Iraq. He says Iran has more strategic flexibility; US allies (Saudi desalination, oil fields, Strait of Hormuz, American bases) are vulnerable to Iranian proxies. War is decided by willingness to fight and die; Iran has studied US strategy and decentralized (militias, cells). He answers viewer questions: no nukes (taboo, Putin back-channel red line); three unknowns (supreme leader assassination/succession, Putin’s response and possible trap of drawing the US into a ground invasion and sunk-cost spiral, China staying out to protect regime stability). He plugs game theory as his analytical model and invites viewers to his Discord (Predictive History) and a possible move to Rumble.
