## tl;dr

Democracy Now! (Amy Goodman, Nermeen Shaik) with Noam Chomsky on Iran, Syria, and what the US could do to change Middle East dynamics. Chomsky: For 60 years not a day has passed without the US "torturing" Iranians: 1953 coup (parliamentary regime overthrown, Shah installed; Amnesty described him as one of worst torturers); 1979 onward US backed Saddam's assault on Iran (hundreds of thousands dead, chemical weapons; US supported Saddam's chemical attacks on Kurds; Reagan blocked censure of Iraq); US effectively won the war against Iran via Iraq; Saddam favourite of Reagan and Bush I; GHW Bush 1989 invited Iraqi nuclear engineers to US for advanced nuclear-weapons training; then harsh sanctions on Iran to the present. Mafia principle: Iran overthrew US-imposed tyrant and took an independent path; credibility must be maintained, so Iran must be punished. Pretext: Iran nuclear program. NYT reports Iran developing nukes; US intelligence says we don't know, maybe, and if so it would be deterrent strategy (reports to Congress). Iran low military spending, surrounded by nuclear powers (Israel, India, Pakistan) backed by US, none NPT signatories, all developed with US assistance; Israel occupying part of Syria; US and Israel constantly threaten Iran (violation of UN Charter). US position: Iran "gravest threat to world peace" (Obama–Romney debate consensus). World: Non-Aligned Movement just met in Tehran, vigorously supported Iran's right to enrich (Iran NPT signatory). Arab world: Iran disliked but not seen as threat; small percentage see Iran as threat; polls show Arabs see US and Israel as serious threats. Media report "Arabs support US on Iran" meaning dictators (e.g. Saudi Arabia, extreme fundamentalist, Wahhabi/Salafi missionary state with US backing), not populations. What can be done: 2010 Iran–Turkey–Brazil deal (ship uranium to Turkey, no further enrichment, West provide medical isotopes); Obama, press, Congress bitterly condemned Brazil and Turkey; Obama rushed harsher sanctions; Brazilian FM released Obama letter showing he had proposed exactly this (assuming Iran would refuse); Iran accepted, so Brazil and Turkey condemned. Could be reinstituted. Broader: since 1974 proposal for nuclear-weapons-free zone in the region; huge international support; US formally agrees but says it can't be done. Last December: conference planned in Helsinki; Israel said it wouldn't attend; Iran said in November it would attend with no conditions; Obama called off the conference. US/Israeli reason: no NWFZ until general regional peace settlement, which won't happen while US blocks Israel–Palestine diplomatic settlement (35 years). Segment ends with thanks; today is September 11 (12 years after 2001, 40th anniversary of Chile coup against Allende).
