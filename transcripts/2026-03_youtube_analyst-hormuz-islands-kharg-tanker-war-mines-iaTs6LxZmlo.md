# Analyst: Hormuz America's Achilles' heel; islands/Kharg as fixed targets; Tanker War logic; mines

**Date:** March 2026 (undated)
**Source:** YouTube (analyst/commentator; Persian Gulf, energy, military logic; likely Iranian or regional)
**URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iaTs6LxZmlo

**Context:** Monologue or Q&A on Strait of Hormuz as US Achilles' heel (oil prices, US polarization, global economic pressure); proposed US scenario of attacking/occupying three Iranian islands (Abu Musa, Greater Tunb, Lesser Tunb) or Kharg to control Strait; analyst argues occupying Kharg or Abu Musa/Tunb would make occupiers "fixed targets" that "could not last," require vast resources for little gain; escalation would shift Iran to Tanker War logic (if you cut my oil I won't let you export) and to Gulf vulnerabilities (desalination, electricity, etc.); IEA 400M barrel release = only ~4 days global consumption; Gulf bypass pipelines (Habshan–Fujairah, East–West Petroline) and Iran's Goureh–Jask (recently attacked); US focus for now = escorting ships, subsidies, mine-clearing; USS Abraham Lincoln targeted/retreating; international coalition under CENTCOM, NATO involvement — "they never thought it would reach this point"; Mojtaba Khamenei letter, Houthis/Hezbollah; Iran 5,000+ naval mines, US short of minesweepers — mining = "international economic chaos."

## tl;dr

**Hormuz as Achilles' heel:** "The Strait of Hormuz and the possibility of it remaining closed has become America's Achilles' heel, which naturally drives up oil prices." Rising oil "will certainly cause a deep rift within the U.S. government"; US already "intense polarization and politically turbulent atmosphere." Closure "will exacerbate these differences and have negative economic impacts on the entire world."

**Scenario: three islands or Kharg:** A scenario proposed for Americans to "manage the Strait themselves": attack three Iranian islands — **Abu Musa, Greater Tunb, Lesser Tunb** — "strategically vital"; if they occupy them they "could gain significant control over the Strait and potentially sideline or eliminate Iran's influence." Most discussion has been **Kharg Island**: Americans want to capture/occupy it, cut off Iran's oil exports and destroy capacity — "90% of our oil is exported from Kharg"; massive facilities, T-type and Azarpad piers, 500,000-ton tankers; pre-1979 could export ~6 million barrels. **But:** "Occupying this island would turn those who seize it into a fixed target — they would not be mobile, and they truly could not last. To accomplish this, or even to take Abu Musa, you would need to deploy ships in the area, provide 24-hour air support, and utilize vast amounts of your own resources — and for what gain?"

**Escalation / Tanker War logic:** "If they escalate to this point, they will face other reactions. You are broadening the war, and this will shift Iran's focus toward other targets on the other side of the Persian Gulf — the same logic seen during the Iran–Iraq 'Tanker War.' If you cut off my oil exports and remove my capacity, I will not let you export oil either." Result: "over 400 ships and tankers were damaged." Such actions "shift the Islamic Republic's focus to other places. Countries across the Persian Gulf, in addition to having military bases, have severe vulnerabilities, including their desalination plants, electricity production sources, and other targets … When you strike Qeshm, you must expect that if Iran wants to respond in kind, it can do so. Therefore, this action would be extremely dangerous for the U.S. and the Persian Gulf states. Iran will react because it will have no other choice."

**IEA reserves:** International Energy Agency (32 members) said they would release **400 million barrels** from strategic reserves. Usually countries' reserves cover ~3 months; 400M barrels is only enough for **4 days** of global consumption (world ~100–111 million bpd; US largest consumer). "This will not have much of an effect; it might shake the market a little."

**Bypass pipelines:** Gulf states built pipelines to bypass Hormuz if closed: **Habshan–Fujairah** (Abu Dhabi); Fujairah on Sea of Oman opposite Jask; ~1.5M bpd. **Saudi East–West Petroline**: Abqaiq to Yanbu (Red Sea), ~7M bpd. "Iran has done the same" — pipeline Goureh to Jask, export terminal at Jask, "which the Americans recently attacked." "Despite these facilities and the vulnerabilities of these pipelines, the situation in the Persian Gulf is critical for the whole world. All of this puts economic pressure, especially on European countries, to pressure the U.S. and Israel to stop their aggression." Iran has stated no ceasefire unless guarantees and compensation.

**Abu Musa / Tunb:** Would become "fixed targets." "The distance is short; if you stand on Greater Tunb, you can see the Bandar-e Bosteh port on Qeshm island with the naked eye." "Play the sycophant for the Southern Persian Gulf states? I don't think so, because those operations are militarily complex and involve too many resources." **My opinion:** "Their focus for now is on escorting ships — ensuring sea lines of communication are safe, that they aren't mined, and if they are, what steps to take for mine-clearing. They want to provide subsidies to ship owners and insurance companies so they agree to resume movement."

**Feasibility / Abraham Lincoln:** "The news says the USS Abraham Lincoln has been targeted — or, as the Americans claim, there was a fire on board. I have read in several news outlets that Iran targeted it, and significantly, the Abraham Lincoln is turning back — it has retreated or at least moved away from the region." Escorting tankers "requires U.S. logistical and military defense, and if they enter the region, they will be targeted and become targets themselves." **Impression:** "The U.S. will send a few ships, get some more from Britain, France, the Netherlands, and others, and give it an international veneer, just like during the Tanker War. They will need full air support to protect both the tankers and the warships, and to stop attacks from Iran. Essentially, NATO will become involved; a new coalition will be formed under the command of CENTCOM. But until then, these issues remain. **This shows they never thought it would reach this point; it wasn't in their plans.**"

**Mojtaba Khamenei / Houthis:** Letter spoke of groups allied with Iran (Houthis, Hezbollah) continuing their work. "The Houthis haven't shown any movement yet" — reasons: past understanding with US after US bombed Hodeidah and other ports; "they don't want to be weakened." "But if they do, the problems will double. Any disruption they can create in the Bab-el-Mandeb and the Red Sea will multiply the issues for the U.S. regarding maritime traffic and the free flow of energy."

**Mines / no plan:** "Ultimately, America is just now thinking about this — they hadn't anticipated it. Trump mentioned forming an international force, but it's a complicated task." **Iran has over 5,000 naval mines** (Russian, Chinese, acoustic, magnetic, pressure-activated). "If pushed to the wall, Iran might resort to mining. Clearing mines is a very time-consuming process. The U.S. doesn't have many minesweepers; they retired most of them before this conflict started. They are now relying on robots, helicopters, and potentially help from allies. Iran's potential to mine the area remains a serious threat that would cause international economic chaos. Even now, all countries are tightening their belts."
