## tl;dr

(3) Who gets involved? Europe (Germany, France, Britain) talking about entering; if so, Russia and China may enter on Iran's side. "This is World War III"—importance of Middle East means everyone has to get involved. **US bases:** Originally to protect monarchies from their own people (Anglo-American empire); not really to defend from external enemies but to impose American authority. Empire as "image, hallucination, aura of invincibility"—if you fear it you obey it; not designed to fight a real war. Many bases in GCC give Iran perfect pretext to attack and destroy GCC economy → destroy American empire. **War map:** Red = US/Israel, yellow = Iran; Iran attacking everywhere (Israel, Bahrain, Qatar) to destabilize; US/Israel focused on Tehran. **Shock and awe:** Decapitate enemy, body falls. But Iran sees religious war, announced total war; command and control in Iran decentralized—every region its own orders; knock out Tehran and everyone else keeps going. US/Israel use decapitation as doctrine; Americans not equipped for 21st-century war (drones, destabilization, religious fanatics) but will keep attacking Tehran. **Irony:** Urban educated progressives most likely to support regime change; rural Shia militants will fight to death—US/Israel destroying the former and leaving the latter. **Asymmetry:** US has empire, resources, USD; Iran poor, sanctioned 45 years, but "poor nations have more energy, more open, more cohesive." Shahed drones ~$50k (as cheap as $35k); 500/day, ~80k stockpile; one can knock out desalination plant, oil field, hotel; mobile, hide anywhere. US defends with THAAD (he says "thermal high altitude air defense"), Patriot; missile ~$1M; often need 2–3 per drone → $2–3M per $50k. THAAD big, slow, easy for Iran to spot and attack. Military doctrine: Cold War was "flexing," not fighting; systems designed to impress, cost a lot, "don't do anything"; not resilient, not innovative, not open.
