## tl;dr

Compilation documentary on Iran: centrifuge uses (power, medical, weapons); MEK history (Tehran University, armed opposition to Shah, assassinations, Capitol Hill lobbying, FTO delisting, “parachute” government parallel to Chalabi). Khatami election (late 1990s), Iran’s culture and “something gone wrong” since Mossadegh. 1953 coup (CIA, Operation Ajax, Kermit Roosevelt, Dulles, Eisenhower, Mossadegh overthrown, democracy destroyed); long-term effects (Islamic Revolution, Soviet fear/Afghanistan, Saddam invasion, US embrace of Saddam). Iran diverse, 5000 years, pride; youth/western culture, cinema, sanctions hitting civil society and middle class more than regime; medicine/banking collateral damage. Iran–Iraq war (1980, US blessing, Rumsfeld, intelligence/helicopters to Saddam, chemical weapons, Iran did not retaliate with chemical; fatwa/religious restraint). Nuclear: 20% concern, Bushehr/Natanz/Arak/Qom targets, bombing would cause rally and “bomb decision” (Iraq Osirak precedent); no weaponization decision per US intel; NPT, disarmament; Shah-era US encouraged nuclear. Sanctions and diplomacy; no law banning medicine but banking blocks; “deadly” situation from sanctions. Regime change vs democratization; three models (military, religious, Mossadegh-style); 2009, children of officials in protests; democracy from within, war would delay generations. Iraq war lobby parallels (Chalabi, MEK, same advocates, Ali Gharib); AP graph debunked (NIAC); objectivity as “within Washington debate.” Chomsky: Iran most pro-American people he’s seen; US isolated; polls (Arab support for Iran, majority for Iran nukes); Turkey, Brazil, Non-Aligned support Iran; threats violate UN Charter. End state: enrichment at 3–5%, transparency, Additional Protocol; US remove sanctions; Congress risk; diplomacy patient and broad; diplomatic relations with enemies; human rights on agenda; Israeli pressure “dominant”; Israeli official (private): not fear of Iranian strike but arms race and intimidation; Iran to emerge from isolation.
