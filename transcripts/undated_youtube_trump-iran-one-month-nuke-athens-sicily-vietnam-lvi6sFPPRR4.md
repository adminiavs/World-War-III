## tl;dr

**Trump "one month from nuclear bomb":** US has said Iran is "one month away" from a nuclear bomb for 10 years; no evidence; this is how the invasion will be explained. Even if Iran had a bomb, they likely wouldn't use it. **Only way US "wins":** nuke the country; America will do it if it has a pretext; Iran is afraid of nukes; this matters when American soldiers are trapped. **Why would US send 100k soldiers to get trapped?** Two methods: (1) historical analysis — find analogues; (2) game theory — rational actor motivation. **Athens–Sicily (415 BCE):** Peloponnesian War stalemate; Alcibiades proposes invading Sicily to win; Nicias opposes, tries to scare Athenians by demanding 5,000 soldiers and 100 ships (huge for ~50k population); Athenians agree anyway. Athens had never sent a large expedition against another power; **biggest problem = resupply**. Syracuse had a navy and stopped Athenian resupply; Athenian army wiped out in Sicily; empire collapsed. Historians: "hubris"; "addicted to empire" — same as America today. **Vietnam:** 1960 most Americans had never heard of Vietnam; by 1969 half a million US soldiers, 58,000 dead. **Pentagon Papers (1971):** three major points — (1) military leadership expanded the war without public knowledge (mission creep: observers → advisors → trainers → soldiers); transcript cuts off before points 2 and 3.
