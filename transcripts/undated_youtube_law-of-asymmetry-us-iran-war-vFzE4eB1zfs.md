## tl;dr

Opening segment of a game-theory lecture on the US-Iran war. Introduces the **law of asymmetry**: the stronger side (empire) usually loses; the underdog has the advantage. Historical examples: Persia vs Greece and Macedon, Romans, Aztecs, Vikings. Three empire advantages that become disadvantages: **mass** (lots of people, draw on allies) leads to inequality, debt, complacency, no will to fight; **organization** (hierarchy, tech, weaponry) means an elite that becomes parasitic (rent-seeking), **elite overproduction** (Peter Turchin), factionalism, polarization, insularity; **death** (can afford to lose wars) leads to hubris, arrogance, repeated mistakes. Empire needs an enemy to collapse it. The winning enemy has **energy** (motivation), **openness** (admit mistakes, learn, meritocracy), **cohesion** (united, common purpose). Key question for the war: Will Iran become energetic, open, and cohesive? If yes, Iran will triumph. The empire will not change (hubris); Iran, if it transforms, will win. Don't focus on US tech or economy; focus on whether Iran is transforming.
