## tl;dr

**Background:** Mark Wales — army from mid-90s, SAS from 2005, heavy Afghanistan (Oruzgan), Iraq, Lebanon, Gulf. Al-Qaeda (caliphate, 9/11) vs Taliban (nationalist, Kandahar, warlord order); Australia SAS recon (e.g. Cha valley), night ops, intel for Brits; "we dealt with al-Qaeda quite quickly … then the war transitioned into a war against the Taliban."

**Tech and war:** "Every time we go into battle there's some new tech … 'This is going to change warfare.' And it never does" — incremental, not revolution. Drones "complimentary to people"; "you do need people on the ground to shape the outcomes"; "what you're seeing now in Iran — there are limits to what air power can do." Robotics could be "game changer" (autonomous partners, Ukrainian rescue drone 30 km); US has "capabilities really far advanced," "keep it in the locker." Hypersonics = >5× speed of sound; Chinese "carrier killer" ballistic missiles, satellite-tracked; carrier sunk = "black swan," broader war; Iran couldn't do that without China/Russia. Nukes: major powers don't go total war because "they've all got nuclear weapons" — escalation could destroy world.

**Iran and the bomb:** If Iran got bomb → arms race (Saudi said they'd get one); Israel under existential threat; US red line, negotiations ("we don't want you enriching … we'll give you what you need") but "could never get that assurance." Iran's view: "minute you give up that capability your country can be hit" — Libya gave up WMDs, Iraq had none; North Korea has nukes "not going to get invaded anytime soon." "Chicken and egg." Proliferation → more weapons → "chance of mistake much higher" — false alarms (e.g. 80s ICBM launch from Alaska toward Russia turned out geese/rising moon); Cuban missile crisis Soviet sub commander refused to fire. US nuclear launch: "one person … the president"; "football" (targeting + comms) "never further than 10 ft from him"; "no double-checking"; book *Nuclear War Scenario* (Andy Jacobson).

**Law of war:** "You don't have to follow unlawful orders"; trained in laws of war; "blow that school up … not a legitimate target … you can say no." "In practice not always that easy." Can "challenge," or "hit a grid 500 metres to the north and say they missed."

**Iran mission decision:** "They debated this heavily"; "couple of camps," "vice president [JD Vance] actually against it." Mates in business school had "planned for these missions to go and capture the enrichment sites" — 160th pilot, Black Hawk/Little Bird: "we had plans … one-way mission … try and secure these sites … really no way for us to get out." Wales didn't think they'd do it: "much bigger country," 90 million, ~1M security forces; "wouldn't have happened unless driven by the political leaders." "Nothing would have happened if the president didn't want it."

**Trump not bluffing:** Wales made a video ~day before strike: "you can't as a political leader put that much military pressure … amass that much force and then not use it" — "big blow to credibility internationally and … your own military." "Big bluff" if you don't use it — "he wasn't bluffing." Putin same: once Wales heard "bakeries, mortuaries, blood banks" he knew "they're actually going to do this." Trump: when he said he'd do something militarily "he almost always followed through" — Syria chemical, Venezuela talk, Iran first time; "this was the fourth."

**Israel's plan / "both things true":** "It was Israel's plan all along," especially Netanyahu (brother killed Entebbe raid, Netanyahu wounded, "hardcore military guy"). Hamas attack = "opportunity to knock down … Hamas, Hezbollah, Houthis … final piece of the puzzle was Iran." "When I've seen this unfold" — that's the reading.

**Duration:** "Now that he's started it, it's not up to him when hostilities cease." "Genie out of the bottle." 2003 "mission accomplished" — "we fought there for another seven years." "Same thing will happen in Iran … maybe even worse because Iran's much better organized." "Huge risk, potentially huge reward" — subjugate, sympathetic admin, Middle East "secured for generations" or "descend into regional conflict." Wales: "I think it will descend into conflict. I don't think it'll end the way they think it will."

**Ground troops:** Trump says no boots; "bombing … control the skies … these guys probably burrowing underground … somebody got to send some people in." "Cancelled training and leave" for 82nd and 101st Airborne — "storied … D-Day"; "chance they could use them to secure key infrastructure" — "enrichment sites," "opening of … Strait of Hormuz." "Once you do that you're open to more casualties … then the grind starts … you're in a war."

**IRGC / Iraq:** In Baghdad 2006–07 "Iranians had gotten in … supplying militias with really advanced shaped charges" (copper disc → molten slug, penetrates armor). "Americans were getting hit … Bradley's, armored Humvees … double, triple amputations. Iranians really gave them hell." "Thousands of troops killed and injured." "Trump's side … fair game … they certainly had a lot of blood on their hands."

**Ground ops now:** "Ground force operators in there already … CIA … organizing air strikes, sabotage … not conventional … not uniformed." "Uniformed boots on the ground … Trump said he wouldn't rule it out … I think there'll be a chance they'll do it." Israel: "securing their immediate" (Gaza, Lebanon); "might have some in coalition"; Mossad "undercover agents and operatives in Iran" — "one of the reasons they were so successful." "Middle of last year … weapons prepositioned, drones, launch sites, air defense sites targeted" — "playbook … target air defense, get more aircraft in, hit more targets … crack … break the whole system open."

**Khamenei strike — intel:** Open source: "hacked some traffic cameras … picked up vehicles associated with his protection detail … knew he was likely there … human intel, probably signals intel." "Fuse all that … probability event … very high confidence he's in this building." "They might have done this 10 years ago … sitting on it until they need it." CIA and Israelis "collaborating pretty closely."

**Tech didn't win Afghanistan:** "All the best tech … none of it mattered in the end. We didn't win." "Comes down to political priorities and political will and whether it's an achievable mission." "Wasn't really achievable … wasn't the political impetus … too costly relative to benefit." "We deluded ourselves … it was an achievable task and it wasn't." Wales "towards the end … this isn't working … every year four or five years … worse, more casualties … bound by Pakistan" (ammo, IEDs, troops over border), "no functioning Afghan government." Biden pulled out; Trump had wanted to retain key bases.

**Multi-front / WWII parallel:** "If ever there's been a probability of major escalation, something like this makes it more likely." "Multifront wars … major European war, major Middle East war, and chance of major conflict in the Pacific (Taiwan)." "Summer of 1941 … no Eastern front yet, Americans not at Pearl Harbor … didn't feel like world war … within six months the whole world aflame." "Spot fires … if you wound the clock 10 years you'd be shocked by one; we now have two at once with possibility of a third." Putin: "world criticizing me for Ukraine … they're doing exactly the same in Iran … attention's off Ukraine … America spending a lot, run out of stuff … good time to put more pressure on Ukraine." China: "who are you to tell us not to take Taiwan when you've just done that in Iran." "Iraq … very hard for Russia to take us seriously when we say don't invade … we did exactly the same … and we've done it again now."

**War of choice / regime change:** "National imperative to win … Ukraine, backs against the wall." "Just war versus war of choice … we're in a war of choice here … much harder to win once they get hard." Securing territory: "if they don't secure it … they'll go back to doing what they were doing … you got to go secure it." "Try to secure … 'this is my country, I'm going to fight to the death.'" Regime change: "that's what they're hoping for … opposition government … don't need to go in with troops." "Sounds a bit fanciful … Iranian … guard … million … if they want to suppress a revolution they might still be able to … structures, equipment destroyed … doesn't mean out of the ashes comes opposition … might descend into civil war … ones with the guns [win]." New Iranian leader (Khamenei's son): "dead man walking … they'll be pretty desperate to target him." "They don't actually know where it's going to go … once you start a war like that you can't predict … out of their hands."

**Hormuz / oil:** "Can Trump control … protect tankers?" "They can … war-gamed a lot … ability to do it but … lot of naval assets, lot of air … only 39 km wide at the mouth … tiny … they'll have a plan to secure and keep traffic moving but it's another drain of resources." "How long can they do that … one carrier already 8 months … cycle it out … don't have infinite resources … another front opens … half your resources diverted." Oil as Iran pressure: "big pressure point"; Gulf States "want to keep this as short as they can"; "average consumer in the West, higher inflation … 5% predicted." US only 3% of exports from there; "Asia suffers big time"; Australia "maybe a month's worth of supplies." "90 days supply" norm; Australia "30 something days" — "runs out … heavy industry, hospitals, medicines, food … grind to a halt." "If Pacific war … fuel rationing … very different economy … haven't been there since World War II."

**Australia:** "Very vulnerable" if on knees. "70,000 [military] roughly … doesn't even fill the MCG." "Good equipment, good training … relies on external support, allies." "Why would you not have a fully self-sufficient defense force … we won't have [allies] if there's a major war." World War II "we lost Singapore … US, UK … totally committed elsewhere." Taiwan + US stuck in Middle East = "nothing left for us." Kevin Rudd 2009 white paper: 12 submarines, protect shipping to the north — "15–16 years later we still don't have submarines." Chinese fleet circumnavigating Australia: "testing ability to reach out and hit … eastern seaboard … capabilities … simulations." "Pine Gap … satellite … Australia's physical position … pretty important … at risk … if I'm the enemy … stuff their signalling … they're all targets." Northwest WA: "ultra low frequency towers … communicate with submarines … nuclear conflict … our bases pass that signal." "Probability of something catastrophic … gone from 1% to 10% … take dramatic steps … if cut off, economy grinds to a halt … fuel, northern waterways … what do we need set up now." Howard credited: post-Vietnam "neglected 15+ years," Timor 99 "400 km off Darwin we could barely deploy"; he said build military back, "10 years … pretty good capability." Recruiting: "off the mark … present as opportunity, don't work too hard … traditional marketing … service-oriented, adventure … works better"; unemployment up → recruiting "kicks off."

**US rebrand:** "Trump credit … television and business … understands branding … repackaged [military] … fighting force, country, flag, adventure … Department of War … point to a certain feel … suited to what they wanted to use it for." Australia "hasn't really done that."

**Australia and the war:** PM, foreign minister, defense — "support to Dubai/Emirates … only part is defense … defending environments … 24,000 Australians … submarine that sunk … frigate … they had no part … war tourists." Wales: "everyone knows that's not true … they're participating … they'd have a role." "We're partnered with the US … bases up north, submarines, Orcus … if major conflict we're going to war with the Americans. That decision has been made for us … AUKUS … wasn't debated nationally … foisted upon us."

**Defense manufacturing / AI:** Artillery: Ukraine "6–8,000 rounds a day"; Australia "6,000 a year"; new automated US factories; "Chinese fully automated … cruise missiles." Australia could do "niche manufacturing … robotics, rocket companies, tech, AI … data centers" — "UAE trying same … UAE bit of a drama … Australia less risky … desert, remote … AWS … heavily rely on us." "Not a complex economy … mining, engineering, tourism … take rocks out … more lucky than good."

**Worries / who gets heard:** "Only thing I'm worried about … people don't understand how at risk our country is … not doing the things with lead time … build military capabilities, fuel storage, defense infrastructure … takes long time, money, effort, political commitment … we're at that point … better to have and not need." "Government … sometimes … Andrew Hastie … we talk … people who have seen war zones … don't want that coming … not listened to very much. Jim Molan … banging the cage … Pacific … lessons haven't landed … budget hasn't changed, psychology hasn't changed." "Polls … if they thought polls said do something they would … we're so far removed … don't get touched by war … World War II we did, bombed Broome, submarines Sydney Harbour … net across … if it happened again they could reach us … psychology needs a shove … prudent … defense, fuel, manufacturing … resilient."

**Cells / influence:** ASIO evidence Iran behind synagogue attacks etc.; "terrorism … soft form of war." "Influence without going to war … Americans good at this … CIA … avert third world war … interfere … morally bad? If it averts world war?" "Iran … influence a war … everyone doing it … boxing: hit and don't get hit." Hezbollah, Iran, Russians "put people in from young age, 20+ years, activate at certain point … only hear when it goes wrong."
